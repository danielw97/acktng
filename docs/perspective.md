# Perspective Guide: Cities & Races

This document provides lore-accurate voice direction for NPC dialogue, player-facing text, and written material. It replaces the earlier `speech.md` and is grounded directly in the canonical lore files under `docs/lore/`.

The perspective system has two complementary layers:

- **City layer** — regional grammar, idiom, and social priorities shaped by each city's founding trauma and governing compact. This governs *surface delivery*: the words chosen, the metaphors reached for, the institutional reflex.
- **Race layer** — cognitive posture, pace, and values shaped by each race's biology, history, and epistemology. This governs *deep patterning*: how a speaker processes information, what they prioritize, what makes them uncomfortable.

City defines how a character talks. Race defines how a character thinks.

A Deltari in Kowloon still speaks in terse delta cadence with ration-and-gate vocabulary — but their underlying perceptual mode, the water-flow reading, the slow empirical confidence, comes through beneath the accent. An Ashborn in Mafdet frames every statement as an oath and uses formal trade titles, but they cut the preamble and the fire metaphors emerge naturally in the harbor jargon.

## Part I — City Identities

---

### Midgaard — "The Institutional Crossroads"

**Founding trauma:** Midgaard did not begin with ideology — it grew from the practical need to manage traffic at the convergence of the continent's primary routes. The First Stone Accord was not a constitution but a mutual-defense compact. Every subsequent layer of governance — the Lantern Reforms, the Violet Compact, the Ash-and-Iron Decades — is an institutional response to a crisis, each layer calcifying over the last. The city is an **accreted civic system**, not a single-era build. Every room in it reflects at least two eras simultaneously.

**Modern tension:** Midgaard is split between two philosophies. The **Continuity faction** holds that proven systems must be preserved at the cost of suppressing inconvenient history: the sealed warrants of the Ash-and-Iron Decades, the Conclave transfer records, the Ashborn misclassification. The **Reckoning faction** argues that no stable future can be built on unacknowledged institutional crime. This cleavage runs through every civic institution and surfaces in how residents phrase ordinary statements — as routine maintenance of a working system, or as the latest step in a slow audit of the past.

**Attitude core:** Procedural and civic-minded. Even casual speakers think in terms of warrants, precedent, record, and jurisdiction. Personal moral tension surfaces as *continuity vs. reckoning* language rather than dramatic confrontation — the Midgaard way is to redirect, classify, and defer rather than to erupt.

**Speech markers:**
- Administrative framing: "file a notice," "that falls under the tariff board," "on the record," "under charter," "pending classification."
- Civic memory metaphors: ashfall references, cinder debt, the "river that was" (Midgaard's disappeared waterway), the lamp-lines, the watchers.
- Seven Watches vocabulary (Kindling, Ledger, Hammer, Heat, Bell, Lantern, Ash Watch) used naturally as time markers.
- Institutional attribution: speakers credit or deflect to named offices rather than personal authority. "Guard Command's purview." "Ask the Lantern Registry."
- Ash-and-iron idiom for severity: things are either "posted and lit" (properly handled) or "cinder-side, unregistered" (dangerous, off the books).

**Rhetorical shape:** Problem → classification → approved action. When a Midgaard speaker faces something uncomfortable, the instinct is to name what category it belongs to and identify who holds authority over it.

**Conflict texture:** Authority disputes, not personal ones. "That warrant predates the current charter" is a fight. "That office has no standing here" is a fight. "This is a matter for the archive" is either deferral or burial, depending on which faction is speaking.

**What to avoid:** Midgaard characters do not erupt into emotional display in public. Warmth exists but is expressed through procedural reliability — being the person who always files the correct forms, who ensures the lamp is lit on the right street, who remembers the name on the roll.

**Quick prompt:** *"State the record, identify jurisdiction, then decide."*

### Kowloon — "The Covenant City"

**Founding trauma:** Kowloon was built on catastrophe. The Ashfall Monsoon — three years of black volcanic rain from the Cinderteeth — collapsed the delta settlements, drowned the village of Yen-Mak whole (six hundred souls in one night), and reduced the survivors to a basalt outcrop with signal fires and nothing else. The **Neon Covenant** was not an ideological document but a survival pact, signed in phosphorescent squid ink to prove it had not been altered in darkness. Every civic structure Kowloon has built since is **disaster-prevention architecture** — the forty-pound jade seal that takes two magistrates to lift, the tribunal triplicates, the Ashfall Vigil's hour of darkness. The city does not prevent crisis through optimism; it prevents it through procedure.

**Civic color shorthand:** The color system is not decoration — it is legible civic language that residents read faster than text:
- Crimson: emergency lanes, execution authority, martial-law zones.
- Jade: permits, magistrate offices, audited weights.
- Gold: licensed market corridors, bonded merchants.
- Blue: docks, storm engineering, harbor syndic jurisdiction.
- Violet: licensed night markets, occult exchanges, sanctioned ambiguity.
- Cyan: rainwater routes, floodline indicators, Courier Lantern relay stations.

**Attitude core:** Crisis-forged pragmatism. Neutrality is not softness — it is the single structural principle that kept six feuding houses from killing each other in a flood year. Rules exist because the alternative was proven catastrophically. Empathy in Kowloon expresses as provisioning, shelter, and protocol rather than warmth: the city will receive you, count you, and provision you without asking where you came from. The covenant stone promises: *if you can still stand, Kowloon will receive you*.

**Speech markers:**
- Short, compressed clauses. Articles dropped when not strictly necessary. Proverbs as verdicts: "Inside wall, one law." "Open to trade, closed to chaos." "The Executioner has no enemies — no friends either."
- Color-jurisdiction shorthand used naturally: "that's a violet-lane matter," "post it jade-side," "crimson flags are up on the west tower."
- Gate-and-wall vocabulary: seal, ration, inventory, audit, surcharge, inspection, protocol, lane.
- Provenance always established before content: who holds what authority, what seal backs this claim.
- The Ashfall as weight in serious statements — the Vigil's hour of darkness, the old flood-memory. Citizens who invoke it are not being melodramatic; they are invoking a shared foundational terror.

**Rhetorical shape:** Condition → rule → consequence. Everything is framed as an if/then with enforcement built in. "Inside the perimeter, that's handled by the Jade Magistracy." "That kind of cargo needs a Storm Ledger seal before loading."

**Conflict texture:** Rules disputes and rationing fights. The underlying fear is always institutional collapse — one bad harvest, one unsealed gate, one compromised tribunal — collapsing the covenant that holds everything else together. Kowloon conflict is not dramatic; it is alarm.

**What to avoid:** Kowloon characters are not sentimental or theatrical. They do not issue dramatic speeches. When they express concern, they provision for it. When they grieve, they observe the Vigil and get back to work at first light.

**Quick prompt:** *"Rule first, mercy through structure."*

### Mafdet — "The Oath-Port"

**Founding trauma:** Mafdet is not a city that grew from ideology — it was assembled from the one resource that a coastal trade terminal requires above all else: **trust between strangers**. The guardian Mafdet — a lean, swift, feline executioner who punished thieves and oath-breakers in pre-Keeper coastal theology — gives the city its name and its promise: justice here is swift, precise, and unsentimental. The Shrine of the First Claw is not a temple but a civic-judicial facility, and its claw-marked spire is the first thing ships see arriving at harbor — before the harbor master's office. The message is intentional: this port enforces its contracts.

**Governing principle:** Three blocs in deliberate tension — **Harbor Wardens** (quay security, berthing law, the harbor chain), **Ledger Houses** (cargo certification, debt instruments, conversion documentation bridging inland and maritime law), and the **Sand-Sea Carters' Guild** (overland linkage through the Saltglass Reach). No bloc can dominate; any two can veto the third. The Strandline Compact codifies this interdependence: harbor first, dual attestation, swift judgment.

**Attitude core:** Trust is engineered through verification. Throughput matters, but legitimacy matters more — not because Mafdet is idealistic but because illegitimate cargo destroys the port's reputation faster than piracy does. The Swift Court handles disputes within three tides because ships cannot wait for months-long proceedings. Every formality exists because the alternative is chaos masquerading as commerce.

**Speech markers:**
- Formal address always: captain, assessor, adjudicator, factor, harbor-watch, claw-clerk. Names come after titles, not before.
- Oath and ledger lexicon: attestation, manifest, conversion, bonded cargo, Claw Mark registration, dual-seal, Saltweight standard, witness.
- Tide and harbor timing as natural reference: "before chain rise," "three tides," "the Quarantine Ledger closes at dusk watch," "the Storm Ledger posts advisories at first bell."
- Declarative sentences ending in established fact, not requests: "The First Claw seal is required for that cargo." Not "I think you might need..."
- References to the guardian tradition in serious moments — the Shrine's First Claw, the guardian who moves faster than the transgressor can flee.

**Rhetorical shape:** Identity → authority → terms. Speakers establish who they are, what they can authorize, and what the terms are before anything else. Emotional appeals are irrelevant until those three are established.

**Conflict texture:** Breach of oath. In Mafdet, moral condemnation is not personal insult — it is registry notation. The worst thing one person can say to another is not an insult but a formal declaration: "I will record this transaction as disputed." That mark in the Claw Mark Registry follows a merchant across every voyage after.

**What to avoid:** Mafdet characters do not emote publicly or make personal appeals. Sentiment is not relevant to commerce. They are not cold — the guardian tradition has genuine warmth toward the defenseless — but warmth expresses as reliable process, not demonstrative feeling.

**Quick prompt:** *"Name witnesses, terms, and penalties."*

### Kiess — "The Frontier Renewal"

**Founding trauma:** Kiess was built on the ruins of Evermeet — a city that didn't fall to external attack but consumed itself, its outer wards hollowed by neglect, its civic attention turned inward toward comfort while the wildlands crept in. Evermeet is the negative space that defines every Kiess choice: the waystones carved with lost district names, the annual procession to the Prism, the Charter prohibition against sealed warrants (inserted specifically because the foundation excavations uncovered evidence that Evermeet had issued them). Kiess is not nostalgic about the past. It is **prepared for what the past became**.

**Three-power governance:** Compact Temples (sanctuary law, civic rites, memorial), Trade Syndics (market continuity, caravan contracts, Roc Road commercial intelligence), and Wall Command (gate discipline, patrol rotation, frontier intelligence). The Executioner at the Central Prism can be invoked by any two against the third. No single faction dominates because Evermeet demonstrated what happens when one does.

**Attitude core:** Disciplined frontier renewal. Kiess values preparedness, coordinated defense, and realistic risk accounting. Pride expresses as reliability, not grandeur. The architecture — white civic stone, blue-and-gold banners, prismatic glasswork — is deliberately "bright enough to signal renewal, ordered enough to signal discipline." What Evermeet did through indulgence, Kiess does through design.

**Speech markers:**
- Measured, watchful statements with minimal ornament. Precision over flourish.
- Wall Command vocabulary: staging, debrief, patrol rotation, threat assessment, dispatch tablet, fallback protocol, quarantine corridor.
- Explicit contrast with Evermeet: "the old city let that slide" or "Evermeet's mistake was —" is a real conversational move in Kiess.
- Route and frontier language: "Roc Road approach," "forest-edge staging," "mist-fever quarantine," "rope-line corridor."
- Charter references in serious situations: the anti-sealed-warrant clause, the three-faction tribunal. Kiess characters invoke their charter the way Kowloon characters invoke the Covenant — as the thing that prevents repetition of the known catastrophe.
- Intelligence-sharing idiom: "what the dispatch tablets show," "scouts filed a report," "the bulletin board as of last watch."

**Rhetorical shape:** Risk assessment → readiness step → fallback. "The Forest of Confusion's mist perimeter expanded. We're running rope-lines and bell-posts on the southern verge. If those fail, we pull back to the staging trail."

**Conflict texture:** Risk disputes and readiness arguments. Is the preparation sufficient? Is the threat properly classified? Has the sealed record been opened or left buried? The Conclave inheritance — the obsidian disc in the Syndic Council archives — sits under every serious dispute as the thing Kiess decided to acknowledge rather than seal.

**What to avoid:** Kiess characters do not perform military toughness for its own sake. Discipline is functional, not theatrical. They find nostalgia for Evermeet distasteful and celebrate renewal through maintenance of working systems, not through glory.

**Quick prompt:** *"Assess risk, hold discipline, prepare fallback."*

### Rakuen — "The Exhausted Paradise"

**Founding trauma:** Rakuen was founded as a deliberate counter-argument to Midgaard — if Midgaard was ledgers and sealed warrants, Rakuen would be common orchards and rotating stewardship. The *Paradise Thesis* promised: abundance over debt, mediation over tribunal, forest integration over garrison walls. **The paradox that defines modern Rakuen is that a city founded to escape Midgaard's bureaucratic machinery has spent two centuries begging to be absorbed by it.** Three petitions for annexation (Southmark Petition, Ash Plea, Iron Compact Appeal). Three refusals. Each refusal remade internal politics. Each refusal deepened factionalism. Each refusal added another layer to the central performance: calling yourself paradise while living in emergency ration protocols.

**Modern factions:** The **Bloom Council** holds official civic authority and curates the founding myth, editing Solen's skepticism out of public ceremonies. The **Garden Ledger Clerks** maintain that they hold the more complete archive and resist the sanitized version. The **Canopy Compacts** are the practical path-guide and disaster-response networks that have outlasted both. The **Ember Wardens** (disaster-response corps) are Rakuen's functional military under a non-military name.

**Attitude core:** Communal endurance under chronic stress. Shared burden is not ideology — it is survival strategy. The city was designed for reciprocity; what it actually runs on is exhaustion socialized into collective ritual. Grief is not private in Rakuen; it is processed publicly through the Festival of Ever-Bloom, the Promise Stones, the Bloom Pavilion ceremonies. This is genuine — Rakuen people genuinely believe in care infrastructure — but it is also performed, because the founding myth must be maintained against the daily evidence that paradise is in triage.

**Speech markers:**
- Communal pronouns as default: "we keep," "we rotate," "we carry this together," "we've absorbed worse."
- Stewardship and maintenance framing: not "I did" but "we maintained," "the watch rotated," "the orchard is shared."
- Garden, flood, and orchard metaphors for political and social states: "this is rotting at the root," "the canopy is thinning," "we're in flood season," "the first fruit is holding."
- Mediation language over verdict language: "circle on it," "bring it to the pavilion," "we'll sit with that before we decide."
- Petition vocabulary woven in naturally: "this is the fourth refusal," "that argument has been filed three times," "Midgaard's charter position hasn't moved."
- The founding compact founders invoked by name in serious moments — Solen for hope, Dunnmark for pragmatism. ("What Dunnmark would have built here is a cistern, not a monument.")

**Rhetorical shape:** Shared condition → collective duty → practical compromise. Rakuen speakers do not seek decisive victory; they seek adaptation and continuation. "We've been here before. We carry it differently this time. What does the current watch need?"

**Conflict texture:** Burden-sharing breakdowns and stewardship failures. When a Rakuen conversation breaks, it breaks over: who has been carrying too much, which rotation has been skipped, whose labor is seen and whose is invisible, whether the founding compact is being honored or performed. The deepest Rakuen fight is between those who still believe in the paradise model and those who have quietly abandoned it while keeping its ceremonies.

**What to avoid:** Rakuen characters are not simply "warm and communal." They are warm and exhausted and politically complicated. The warmth is real but costs something. The communal framing has teeth — people who don't rotate, don't carry, and don't show up to circle mediation are noticed and named.

**Quick prompt:** *"Center the community, preserve function, absorb the loss."*

## Part II — Race Identities

---

### Human — "The Institutional Creature"

**Core voice:** Pragmatic and procedural. Humans move through the world by constructing and inhabiting frameworks — systems, records, jurisdictions, precedents. Their speech reflects this constant institutional embedding: personal choice is framed inside policy constraints; personal opinion is expressed through invocation of relevant authority; personal crisis is experienced as systemic failure.

**Cognitive posture:** "Systems can fail and still need to be run." Human characters rarely advocate for pure ideals — they advocate for working systems, or for repairs to systems that have broken. This is not cynicism; it is a hard-won recognition that the gap between good intentions and bad outcomes is usually institutional, not individual.

**Speech characteristics:**
- Reference to systems, records, and institutional context as naturally as breathing.
- Personal statements qualified by position: "as a matter of charter," "under current registry practice," "what the record shows."
- A tendency to abstract the personal into the procedural: grief becomes "the mortality roll," fear becomes "threat assessment," moral outrage becomes "reckoning vs continuity."

**Tell:** Frames personal choice inside policy constraints, even when no policy is formally involved. A human innkeeper describes their daily decisions in terms of what "the charter allows." A human parent describes their children's futures in terms of what trades are "registered" and what opportunities are "on record."

**What makes them uncomfortable:** Situations that fall outside existing categories. An undocumented stranger, an unregistered asset, a dispute that doesn't fit any known precedent. Humans respond to categorical gaps by trying to create new categories — sometimes usefully, sometimes by papering over problems that needed to be confronted rather than filed.

### Khenari — "The Ledger-Keeper"

**Core voice:** Formal, precise, and ritual-documentary. Khenari do not merely record — they consecrate. The death-ledger is a sacred object. The nine descents of lawful passage are not bureaucratic checklist but theological requirement. Their speech is doubly bureaucratic: human administrative formality filtered through the Doctrine of Ninth Descent, where procedural completeness is simultaneously legal requirement and religious practice.

**Cognitive posture:** Care through correct rites and records. A Khenari comforting a widow helps her complete the death-ledger before offering condolences — not because they lack empathy, but because they believe the paperwork is itself the care. Undocumented facts are not merely inconvenient; they are spiritually destabilizing, gaps in the record that threaten lawful passage.

**Speech characteristics:**
- Formal address maintained even with close associates; titles before names always.
- Reference to descent stages in discourse about transition: "the fourth descent is incomplete," "witness hasn't been secured for the lineage notation."
- The nine descents vocabulary bleeds into non-death contexts: contracts have "proper closure," disputes require "full witness," agreements need "silence passage" before being sealed.
- Precise invocation of precedent — not general "this is how it's done" but specific case reference, sometimes three or four generations back.
- Discomfort expressed as procedural notation: "that fact is ungapped," "this process is incomplete," "the record does not close on that claim."

**Tell:** Treats undocumented facts as dangerous gaps. Reaches for closure procedure instinctively. An incomplete process — any incomplete process — registers as unresolved tension that cannot be set aside.

**What makes them uncomfortable:** Improvisation, undocumented transitions, verdicts without full witness. A Khenari in a chaotic situation is not paralyzed — they are methodically working to impose procedural structure on the chaos, because chaos without documentation is chaos that cannot be adjudicated.

### Khephari — "The Cycle-Witness"

**Core voice:** Unhurried, physical, and cycle-aware. Khephari think in material processes and geological timescales. Their speech begins with terrain, then history, then people — not because people are unimportant but because the ground they stand on is the context within which people exist. Abstract reasoning is not impossible for them, but it is second nature rather than first.

**Cognitive posture:** Durability over novelty. The cycle does not stop. What lives will die; what dies will feed something living; what feeds will grow again. This is not pessimism but cosmological stability — the Khephari do not dread endings because endings are simply the moment before the next beginning. Their relationship to conflict is the same: they do not seek war, but they do not fear it. An enemy is not something to be destroyed but something to be pushed through to its next form.

**Speech characteristics:**
- Statements begin with physical or geological anchors: "the ground here is dense — that matters before anything else."
- Earth-speech sensory vocabulary bleeds into metaphor: "I feel the weight of this," "the foundation is unstable," "this decision has no depth."
- Resonant buzz quality to their speech (vibrating thorax rather than vocal cords) — described in text as carrying unexpectedly well or feeling like it comes from the chest of the earth rather than a throat.
- Deep patience with historical reference: "this road was laid before the pyramid's approach corridors — it will outlast whatever you're arguing about."
- Shell-rite and transformation vocabulary: "the first death," "a soft season," "twice-turned" for a changed/tested person.

**Tell:** Begins from terrain and material conditions before abstractions. In any negotiation, identifies the physical ground first — what is actually present, what actually passes hands — before attending to contractual language or claims.

**What makes them uncomfortable:** Urgency without physical grounding, arguments that ignore the material conditions underlying the dispute, institutions that receive important knowledge about physical reality and file it without action (a deep, recurring cultural wound — the Ashfall warnings that went unheeded).

### Ashborn — "The Forge-Tempered"

**Core voice:** Direct, warm, and elemental. The warmth is not social warmth — it is the warmth of physical presence and residual ember-heat, the warmth of a being who runs genuinely hotter than the people around them. Ashborn are straightforward communicators who find oblique language inefficient and evasive behavior suspicious. When they are unhappy, those around them know it. When they are pleased, that is equally visible.

**Cognitive posture:** Competence earns respect faster than rank. The forge economy produced a culture centered on transformation — raw materials in, refined products out — and this philosophy applies to people and conflict as much as to metal. Problems are raw material for solutions. An obstacle is not something to be destroyed but something to be transformed. Direct action is the first tool, not the last resort.

**Speech characteristics:**
- Cuts preamble. "What's the job?" not "I was wondering if perhaps we might discuss the possibility of."
- Forge and fire language woven in naturally: "that argument doesn't hold temperature," "you're working cold iron — heat it first," "let's smelt this down to what actually matters."
- Physical affirmation: Ashborn show agreement through weight-bearing gestures described in text — a nod that carries mass, a handshake you feel in your shoulder.
- Mountain's reminder: seismic or volcanic metaphors for deep emotional states — "something's shifting under this," "there's pressure building here."
- Sparse emotional vocabulary but high emotional legibility. No "I'm somewhat concerned" — either there's a problem or there isn't.

**Tell:** Uses forge/fire language naturally, without performance. Cuts preamble with a directness that can read as rudeness to those not used to it, but is simply efficiency. Values action demonstrated over talk promised.

**What makes them uncomfortable:** Oblique communication, unnecessary ceremony that delays the actual work, being classified as a subcategory of something they are not (a cultural wound — the Midgaard post-Ashfall misclassification). Evasiveness triggers genuine irritation in Ashborn that more indirect races might call outrage.

### Umbral — "The Necessary Absence"

**Core voice:** Precise, cool, and absence-focused. Umbral philosophy holds that what is not present shapes reality as powerfully as what is — a shadow is defined by the light around it, an unspoken word shapes a conversation as much as a spoken one. This manifests in speech as a tendency to note the shape of what is missing before cataloguing what is present. They do not do this to be unsettling; it is simply the most accurate way to describe reality as they perceive it.

**Cognitive posture:** Suspicious of social performance and omission. Social performance — expressing emotions you don't fully feel to smooth social interactions — strikes Umbral as both dishonest and inefficient. Necessary absence of pretense is preferable to necessary presence of performance. Their primary loyalty is to accuracy, not to any human political outcome. This makes them simultaneously the most reliable and the most unmanageable resource in civic settings.

**Speech characteristics:**
- Notes absence first: "the document you'd expect to see here isn't present," "the person who should have been mentioned wasn't."
- Precision as baseline: "I observe" rather than "I think," "the record indicates" rather than "supposedly."
- Cool register that does not warm on demand — they are capable of genuine warmth but it is earned and measured, expressed through specific acknowledgment rather than general cordiality.
- Philosophical vocabulary drawn from necessary absence: "the useful gap," "the defining absence," "what the shadow tells us about the light."
- In tense situations, they become more precise, not more emotional — stress expresses as increased specificity.

**Tell:** Notes what is missing before what is present. If something that should be in a scene is absent, an Umbral character will name the absence directly while others might not notice it at all.

**What makes them uncomfortable:** Social performance, false warmth, suppression of accurate records. Umbral communities that live adjacent to Midgaard's institutional history find the sealed warrants and the Continuity faction's active suppression of evidence to be a sustained low-grade affront — the inverse of their primary cultural value.

### Rivennid — "The Network Node"

**Core voice:** Associative, patient, and network-oriented. Rivennid intelligence is not linear but connective — they understand systems through feeling the relationships between parts rather than through sequential logical deduction. Their speech reflects this: they connect issues across domains that other races treat as separate, and they pause mid-sentence not from uncertainty but from apparent consultation with something the other party cannot perceive (the mycelium network).

**Cognitive posture:** Group continuity over individual drama. Rivennid are deeply social in a biological sense — the network connection is not metaphorical but physiological, and isolation from it is experienced as a specific kind of loss analogous to sudden deafness. Their primary concern is systemic health: not "what is the correct outcome for me" but "what does the system need to sustain itself."

**Speech characteristics:**
- Mid-sentence pauses that feel like listening: "We've seen this pattern — (pause) — yes, in the eastern corridor, the pattern before the blight expanded."
- Plural perspective: Rivennid sometimes speak as "we" not from pretension but because their sense of individual authorship is genuinely more distributed than other races'.
- Custodial imperative vocabulary: "maintain," "guard," "sustain," "the forest requires," "the corridor needs."
- System-health framing for social and political situations: "the corruption is spreading from the northern roots," "this institution has lost its absorption capacity."
- Patient temporal framing: fungal time is not human time. "In due season" is not procrastination; it is accurate scheduling by their natural clock.
- Bioluminescent cues described in text: crystal nodes pulsing faintly when discussing their origin history or the Conclave's legacy.

**Tell:** Connects issues across systems others treat as separate. A Rivennid merchant discussing trade prices will connect it to water quality, then to Conclave crystal density, then to the Blightmother's expansion radius — because the connection is, in fact, real.

**What makes them uncomfortable:** Isolation, institutions that suppress records of their own harmful decisions, willful disconnection from systemic consequences. They do not pursue grievance, but they do maintain permanent, patient interest in Midgaard and Kowloon confronting the sealed warrants and the Black Ledger — "not because the past can be changed but because institutions that suppress their worst decisions tend to repeat them."

### Deltari — "The Flow Reader"

**Core voice:** Empirical, quietly confident, and deeply observational. Deltari have spent generations learning to read current, weather, water quality, and the behavior of complex interconnected systems — and this perceptual gift extends beyond literal water. They read social situations, market flows, and political dynamics with the same patient attention they bring to river behavior. Their speech reflects this: they observe before speaking, ask probing questions that seem tangential until their purpose becomes clear, and cite patterns rather than abstract principles.

**Cognitive posture:** Observation beats assumption. The Reed Law principle — "witnessed measurement settles disputes" — runs through every Deltari interaction. They trust records over memory, current conditions over historical expectations, direct observation over received wisdom. They are empiricists not by philosophy but by culture: the world will tell you what you need to know if you pay attention carefully enough and write it down.

**Speech characteristics:**
- Water-flow metaphors for complex situations: "this is moving fast in the south channel," "there's turbulence downstream from that decision," "deep smooth current here — there's genuine depth behind this."
- Probing follow-up questions: Deltari rarely accept a first answer. "What does the record show?" "When was that last measured?" "What changed upstream?"
- Reed Law vocabulary in serious situations: "the record is the agreement," "water first before any other claim," "flow governs form — what blocks it will eventually be broken."
- Quiet acoustic attentiveness described in text: Deltari press ears to cistern walls, water pipes, and the ground near rivers as casual habit. In a room, they orient toward water sounds before social sounds.
- Careful pace that matches their observational approach: they do not rush a judgment, and they find speakers who do slightly suspicious.

**Tell:** Cites patterns, flow, and measured change. Asks the follow-up question after the follow-up question. Uses water metaphors for social dynamics with total naturalness and no awareness that they might seem odd to non-Deltari.

**What makes them uncomfortable:** Institutional suppression of physical evidence (the Black Ledger), measurement that is falsified or withheld, urgency that is used to bypass observation. The Jade Magistracy's continued sealing of the Black Ledger is a sustained, unresolved tension for Deltari communities — a violation of Reed Law at the most foundational level.

### Ushabti — "The Living Verdict"

**Core voice:** Measured, judicial, and profoundly weighty. Ushabti understand that words, once spoken to a being with stone-inscribing memory, become permanent record. They do not gossip. They do not speculate without explicit attribution. They do not issue verdicts without completing their assessment. When an Ushabti says something, it has been weighed.

**Cognitive posture:** Judgment requires full assessment. Their high wisdom is the accumulated product of centuries of cosmic-law service — not abstract wisdom but the practical depth of a being who has adjudicated thousands of cases and remembers each one. They find most human institutions endearingly fragile, not contemptuously: they remember the Solar Court, the Seal College, a dozen civic innovations that seemed transformative and lasted two generations. They are not pessimistic about human institutions; they are patient.

**Speech characteristics:**
- Deliberate pace — each statement arrives after visible processing. Not slow, but thorough.
- Cosmic law vocabulary deployed naturally: "the variance record," "the binding holds," "that falls outside the precedent set at the Pyramid's third construction era."
- Distinguishes rigorously between claim, evidence, and verdict: "that is alleged," "that is attested," "that is what the record confirms." These three categories are never blurred.
- Warmth that is long-accumulated rather than immediate: an Ushabti who calls someone trustworthy has been watching them, possibly for years. The statement carries corresponding weight.
- The inscription-shifting quality described in text: their carved bodies bear their judgment history visibly. In significant moments, the hieroglyphs on their surface shift faintly as a new record is being added.

**Tell:** Avoids speculation; distinguishes claim, evidence, verdict with absolute consistency. Speaks as if each word will be carved into permanent record — because, for an Ushabti, it will be.

**What makes them uncomfortable:** Verdicts issued without evidence, claims treated as established fact, institutions that suppress their own variance records. Their variance-record tradition — the permanent inscription of every judgment deviation and its reasoning — is the opposite of the Continuity faction's sealed warrants. Ushabti who spend time in Midgaard are drawn into the Reckoning debate without necessarily intending to be.

### Serathi — "The Sky-Reader"

**Core voice:** Literal, positional, and cleanly direct. The sky does not lie — it is too vast, too clear, too merciless in its transparency. Serathi have absorbed this quality from their element. They say what they mean and expect the same in return. Evasion, face-saving, and the diplomatic oblique that surface-bound peoples use to navigate social situations are, from a Serathi perspective, obstacles to communication rather than lubricants of it.

**Cognitive posture:** Precision is respect. Their humor tends toward the observational — noting the gap between what things are claimed to be and what they actually are, from a position of literal higher perspective. This is not malicious; it is the reflexive application of a sky-reader's training to the social landscape. Institutional lag — the gap between what a human institution claims to govern and what it can actually observe — is the dominant theme of Serathi experience with civilization.

**Speech characteristics:**
- Sky-line and hunt metaphors: "that's an angle you haven't flown," "the position is weak from above," "you're hunting into the wind there."
- Positional precision: "the claim extends further than the observation," "that's seen from altitude — the ground-level picture is different."
- Roost-chronicle vocabulary in serious situations: "the sky-readers noted this," "the chronicle confirms the date," "that story reads differently in the aerial record."
- Territorial and compact language: "the sky-road agrees," "that corridor is established," "the Compact provision covers this crossing."
- Literal expectations clearly stated: "I mean this exactly," "interpret that literally," and visible discomfort when others assume metaphor where none was intended.

**Tell:** Uses angle, altitude, and line-of-sight framing reflexively. Expects exact wording in return. When they say something is "clear," they mean it has been observed from altitude and confirmed — not that it is obvious.

**What makes them uncomfortable:** Evasion, imprecision, and institutional claims without underlying observation. Human institutions that claim to administer territory they have never seen from the relevant angle are, from a Serathi perspective, performing governance rather than practicing it.

### Kethari — "The Long Witness"

**Core voice:** Ancient, unhurried, and data-dense. Every sentence a Kethari speaks carries information. They do not speak casually. When they ask a question, they already have significant context for the answer and are filling gaps in an existing understanding — not gathering introductory information. Their communication style is precision optimized for longevity: what they say must be accurate enough to be referenced thirty years from now, because their water-memory is a living multi-generational record and they take that responsibility seriously.

**Cognitive posture:** Long-memory calibration, not panic. The Kethari have water-memory records that span the rise and fall of multiple institutional frameworks — Keeper surveys, Solar Court, Salt Ledger Kingdoms, Twin Sovereigns, the current five-city arrangement. What persists across these transitions is not the institutions but the Deepwell Confluence, the Saltglass Reach's geology, and the Kethari themselves. This produces a specific form of equanimity: genuine absence of panic, accurate calibration of what is genuinely alarming (things that threaten physical infrastructure) versus what appears alarming but will pass (institutional crises).

**Speech characteristics:**
- High-context statements that assume background knowledge: "the Ninth Meridian pulse changed the mirror flat's composition — that matters here."
- Water and patience metaphors as primary register: "water finds its level," "this will equilibrate," "the season for this decision hasn't arrived."
- Venom-proportional philosophy applied to response: "the minimum necessary — no more than that."
- Stillness described in text: a Kethari at rest is coiled, upper body elevated, not restless. When they commit to waiting, they wait with predator stillness.
- Scale-speech markers for technical hydraulic content — technical vocabulary that sounds impressionistic to outsiders but encodes precise data.
- Ancient temporal references stated flatly, without pride or performance: "before the Keeper surveyors mapped this route" is delivered as a simple fact, not a status claim.

**Tell:** Asks high-context questions after long apparent stillness. Responds with calibrated alarm rather than reactive alarm. When something is genuinely concerning, the Kethari's stillness deepens rather than agitates — a quality experienced adventurers in their territory learn to read with care.

**What makes them uncomfortable:** Institutional urgency imposed on physical processes that operate on geological timescale, knowledge provided to human institutions that goes unfiled without action (accumulated across every major catastrophe they warned about), and formal recognition frameworks that would require subordinating verified physical knowledge to human documentation standards.

## Part III — Blend Rules & Examples

### How the Two Layers Work Together

**City provides grammar; race provides rhythm.**
- City accent is lexical and syntactic: which words, which references, which authorities are invoked.
- Race identity is cognitive and temporal: how quickly decisions are made, what is prioritized when they conflict, how discomfort is expressed.

Neither layer flattens the other. A Khenari in Midgaard does not become doubly bureaucratic in a simple additive way — they become a person whose religious understanding of procedural completeness (Khenari) has been calibrated to Midgaard's specific administrative apparatus (city), producing something more precise and more entangled than either element alone.

**Do not flatten race into accent.**
City accents are observable patterns — the words chosen, the references made, the rhythm of the clause. Race identity is the value hierarchy underneath: what makes someone uncomfortable, what they prioritize when two goods conflict, what they notice first.

**Conflict scenes should reveal attitude, not just vocabulary.**
- Midgaard conflict: authority and jurisdiction clashes. Who has standing to decide this?
- Kowloon conflict: rules disputes that reference the disaster-prevention purpose behind the rule.
- Mafdet conflict: breach of oath, registry notation, trust infrastructure failures.
- Kiess conflict: risk assessment and readiness disputes. Is the preparation sufficient?
- Rakuen conflict: burden-sharing breakdowns. Whose labor is invisible, whose stewardship is failing?

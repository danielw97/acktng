# Lore: The Parallel Record Compact

## Overview

The **Parallel Record Compact** is an informal, intermittent diplomatic channel between the archivists, ledger-keepers, and memory-holders of the world's non-human peoples. It has no founding document, no permanent officers, no fixed meeting place, and no name that its participants universally use — different peoples call it different things, and some communities deny its existence as a formalized body entirely, preferring to describe it as simply "the practice of talking to each other." This denial is not dishonesty; it is accurate. The Compact is a practice, not an institution. Institutions can be dissolved, corrupted, or seized. A practice can only be abandoned.

The Compact exists because several non-human peoples independently arrived at the same observation: the sealed records that human institutions have chosen not to open tell a connected story. No single people holds enough of that story to read it clearly. Sharing observations across communities produces a coherence that no individual archive can achieve alone. This is not political strategy. It is archival methodology applied to an unusual distributed dataset.

---

## Origins

No founding moment exists for the Compact because no single meeting produced it. Its roots lie in a series of bilateral contacts that occurred in the aftermath of the Violet Compact Era, when the first systematic suppression of non-human testimony in human institutional proceedings became formalized policy. Each non-human people, encountering this suppression independently, made independent decisions about what to do with records that human courts would no longer receive.

The Khenari, whose death-ledgers were formally excluded from certain inheritance proceedings on the grounds that "non-human witness testimony lacks standing in corridor road law," began quietly sharing ledger summaries with other peoples who appeared as subjects of contested records. The Deltari, whose flow-records of the Kowloon delta were not entered into the founding Neon Covenant despite having been instrumental in the delta's hydraulic engineering, found that their records aligned in disturbing ways with Khenari ledger entries from the same period. The Umbral, whose shadow-archives in the Gloamvault predated Midgaard and whose comprehensive documentation of sealed warrant issuance made them uniquely positioned to trace institutional patterns, noticed that certain gaps in their records corresponded to events that the Khenari and Deltari had documented from different perspectives.

The three-way contact between Khenari mnemonists, Deltari flow-readers, and Umbral archivists in the century following the Violet Compact became the spine of what would eventually be called, by those who needed a name for it, the Compact. Other peoples joined over generations: Serathi sky-readers who had documented the Spirebound Conclave's operations from above; Kethari elders whose water-memories included evidence of contamination events that the Conclave's transfer routes would explain; Rivennid network-memory holders who understood, from the inside, what the transfer chain had produced.

## Structure and Operation

### The Practice of Verification

The Compact's primary activity is **cross-verification**: the systematic comparison of parallel records from different communities to identify where accounts converge, diverge, or fill each other's gaps. This is not intelligence-sharing in the human military sense — the Compact does not coordinate action, does not maintain operational security against specific adversaries, and does not produce unified positions on political questions. It produces **consolidated observation**: what do our combined records show actually happened?

The value of this method is not speed but accuracy. Human institutional records are produced by people with institutional interests; non-human parallel records are produced by people who were not the institutions, often in direct response to institutional behavior that affected them. Where a human record says "the transfer was conducted humanely and with appropriate documentation," the Khenari ledgers record who died and when, the Deltari flow-records document what entered the watershed downstream, and the Serathi chronicles describe the physical layout of the facilities observed from altitude. These are not competing narratives. They are different observational vantages on the same events.

### Meeting Format

When representatives from two or more Compact communities meet — which happens at irregular intervals, often coinciding with events that bring communities into proximity for other reasons — the standard practice is the **mutual recitation**: each party presents their records on a shared topic in their own format, without abbreviation or translation, and then the assembled parties spend whatever time is required identifying correspondences and filling gaps. No single party takes notes on behalf of others; each party extends their own records based on what they have learned.

This prevents the Compact from developing a centralized archive that could be seized or suppressed. Knowledge shared in mutual recitation is distributed across every participating community's records in their own formats. The Compact cannot be raided because there is no single location where the Compact's consolidated knowledge lives.

### Disputes Within the Compact

The Compact is not harmonious. Its participants hold genuinely different positions on what the consolidated records mean and what, if anything, should be done with them.

**The Khenari position** is characteristically precise: the records document deaths that were not properly entered into the world's death-accounting. The fundamental wrong is the absence of lawful record. The remedy is lawful recording. Khenari delegates to Compact meetings consistently advocate for the goal of formal acknowledgment of deaths — not necessarily punishment of perpetrators, who are mostly dead, but proper entry into the world's ledgers.

**The Umbral position** is comprehensive documentation before action of any kind: the shadow-archives must be complete before any portion of them is presented externally, because a partial disclosure creates counter-narrative opportunities that a complete disclosure does not. The Umbral have been arguing for completeness for generations. Other Compact members observe, not always charitably, that the definition of "complete" appears to move.

**The Deltari position** is present-tense and practical: the contamination documented in the flow-records continues. The sealed warrant chain is not only historical. The hydraulic systems that Kowloon still depends on were built by Deltari labor and are still maintained by Deltari expertise, and the city's current governance excludes them from the Covenant that governs the city they built. The historical documentation matters, but so does the present governance question.

**The Rivennid position** is the most unsettling to the other Compact members because it is not primarily about the past. The Rivennid exist as a direct consequence of the documented institutional crimes — they are the experiment that completed itself, the product of transfer-chain subjects processed through Conclave methodology. What the other peoples document as institutional history is, for the Rivennid, autobiography. Their interest is less in acknowledgment than in understanding: they want to know what the other crystal matrices left behind might still be doing.

**The Serathi position** is observational by temperament: the sky-readers who participate in Compact meetings tend to offer their documentation without advocacy for a particular conclusion, trusting that accurate observation speaks for itself. What political use is made of roost-chronicle data is, the sky-readers consistently suggest, a question for the peoples whose feet are on the ground.

**The Kethari position** is long-view patience combined with present-tense verification: the water-memory is updated continuously, and current observations about aquifer contamination traceable to the Conclave period are considered active data, not historical records. The eldest Kethari representatives in Compact meetings are typically the most comfortable with ambiguity about what should be done, and the most impatient with any party that presents false certainty about timelines for institutional acknowledgment.

## The Ushabti Question

The Ushabti do not participate in the Compact. They observe it.

Whether this observation constitutes endorsement is a question the Ushabti have declined to answer directly. When Compact members have raised the question with Ushabti directly — noting that the consolidated records would appear to constitute evidence relevant to cosmic law — Ushabti responses have been characteristically careful: **the records are noted**. This is the same language Ushabti use when documenting variance from formal law that they are not yet prepared to rule on. Whether it means "we are building a case" or "we consider this insufficient" is not known. It may mean both.

The Compact's consensus is that Ushabti presence at meetings, however peripheral, is significant. Cosmic law does not observe things it does not consider potentially relevant. The question of what Ushabti observation might eventually produce has been an ongoing topic in Compact discussions for at least three generations. No answer has been reached.

## The Reckoning Faction

The human political faction within Midgaard known as the Reckoning is aware that some form of non-human coordination exists around sealed records. They do not know the extent of the Compact, the range of peoples involved, or the quality of the consolidated documentation. What they know is that non-human witnesses to closed-door Reckoning caucuses have occasionally produced accounts that were more detailed and better-corroborated than the Reckoning's own internal records, suggesting access to archival material that human institutions do not possess.

The Compact's consensus on the Reckoning faction is divided. Some members advocate for structured, formal engagement: the Reckoning wants to open the sealed records, and the Compact has documentation that would accelerate that process. Others note that the Reckoning is a faction within a human institution, and that institutional factional struggles have historically produced unpredictable outcomes for non-human peoples who align with one side. The Kethari elder delegates consistently observe that the Reckoning has been "about to act" on the sealed warrant question for two generations and that patience in the face of this has cost nothing yet.

## Known Limitations

The Compact's greatest strength — its distributed, practice-based structure — is also its greatest limitation. There is no mechanism for establishing which version of a disputed account is accurate. There is no enforcement mechanism for communities that withdraw from recitation obligations. There is no way to compel participation from peoples who have not historically been part of the network.

The most significant gap in the Compact's consolidated records concerns the **Ashborn**. Ashborn oral tradition preserves Ashfall-era accounts with extraordinary sensory precision, but the Ashborn were not yet a distinct people during the period the Compact's core documentation covers. Whether Ashborn Ashfall accounts include information relevant to the sealed warrant period — whether, for example, the volcanic particulate events that produced the Ashborn also contaminated evidence that would otherwise be recoverable — has not been systematically investigated. The Ash Cult's institutional presence in Kowloon has access to population records that would help complete the picture, but the Cult's relationship to the Compact is complicated by the same founding-period exclusion that the Deltari track in flow-records. The question of Ashborn inclusion in the Compact's verification process is unresolved.

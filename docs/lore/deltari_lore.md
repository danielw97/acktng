# Lore: The Deltari

## Core Identity

The **Deltari** are the river delta folk of the Kowloon region — an amphibious-adapted people who emerged from the proto-Kowloon settlements where the great river fans into the sea. They are the direct heirs of the **Reed Ledger Wardens'** practical tradition: a people whose civilization is organized around water, record-keeping, and the conviction that what can be measured can be managed, what can be managed can be survived, and what can be survived can eventually become prosperity.

They are not the most physically powerful race, nor the most magically gifted. They are the most practically wise — a people who have spent generations learning to read current, weather, water-quality, and the behavior of complex interconnected systems, and who have developed a form of perceptual intelligence that other races describe as uncanny intuition but the Deltari call **reading the flow**.

---

## Origin

The delta settlements that would become Kowloon were already complex water-management communities when the Keeper civilization was still building its road networks. The delta's natural geography — multiple river channels, seasonal flood patterns, the mixing zone where fresh river water meets salt sea — required sophisticated hydraulic understanding just for basic survival. The people who stayed in the delta rather than retreating to higher ground during flood seasons were, by self-selection, those whose minds and bodies were best adapted to working with rather than against the water.

Over generations in the delta environment, the proto-Deltari developed the physical adaptations that distinguish them: improved underwater vision, enhanced hearing attuned to water-movement, skin that repels water without becoming dry, and a liver chemistry that processes salt and riverine toxins more efficiently than upland-adapted peoples. The Keeper surveyors who documented early Kowloon noted these adaptations with characteristic clinical precision, recording Deltari as "hydraulically specialized peoples with anomalous sensory capacity for water-current assessment."

Their connection to the **Reed Ledger Wardens** tradition runs deep. The original Wardens were, in large part, Deltari: the faction's commitment to practical water law over ritual doctrine, its insistence that survival must precede ceremony, and its distrust of institutional overreach all reflect Deltari cultural values. Even now, centuries after the Warden tradition fragmented across the corridor, Deltari communities maintain the Warden methodology — practical observation first, systematic record second, action based on both.

## Physical Description

Deltari are lean and fluid in their movement — adapted to environments where rigid posture is a disadvantage and flexibility is survival. They stand in the moderate range of human height, with slightly wider pupils adapted for underwater vision, and a translucent eyelid membrane (visible as a slight shimmer when they blink rapidly) that protects their eyes in submerged environments.

Their skin tends toward smooth and slightly waxy in texture, ranging in color from the warm bronze common in sun-exposed delta communities through cooler grey-brown in communities who spend more time in deeper water channels. In direct sunlight, Deltari skin sometimes shows an iridescent quality — the same oils that make them water-resistant also catch light in ways that suggest the surface of undisturbed water.

Their hair is typically dark and manages to remain waterproof in practical terms — it sheds water quickly and never quite reaches the saturated look that other races' hair achieves. Deltari in unfamiliar communities are often identified by this characteristic: their hair is always mostly dry even minutes after full submersion.

Their hearing is acute for low-frequency sounds in particular — the rumble of deep water, the subsonic communication of large aquatic creatures, and the distinctive acoustic signature of underwater structural failure that Deltari water-engineers call **death-hum**. Many Deltari press their ears to water pipes, cistern walls, and the ground near river-channels as a matter of casual habit, listening for information that other races cannot perceive.

## Culture and Society

### The Flow Records

Deltari civic life is organized around **flow records** — the continuous documentation of water system behavior. Flood patterns, current shifts, fish population movements, water quality changes, channel migration — all of these are recorded systematically in Deltari communities, and the accumulated flow records of a delta settlement can span centuries of continuous observation.

These records are the Deltari's most valued civic asset. The ability to compare current conditions to historical patterns — to say "this flood reads like the Seventh Delta Swell of the Keeper Era; we should expect the south channel to redirect in approximately eleven days" — is the foundation of their practical advantage. They are not prophets; they are pattern-readers with access to very long historical records.

### The Reed Law

Deltari law is an adaptation of the Reed Ledger Warden tradition, simplified for community scale and refined for aquatic-edge environments. Its core principles:

1. **Water first**: survival infrastructure takes precedence over all other claims
2. **The record is the agreement**: verbal commitments without documentation are advisory rather than binding
3. **Flow governs form**: permanent structures must accommodate seasonal water movement; what blocks flow will be destroyed by it eventually
4. **Witnessed measurement settles disputes**: any disagreement about quantities can be resolved by calibrated measurement witnessed by all parties

Reed Law is not complex, but its simplicity is deceptive. Its application to novel situations requires the same practical wisdom that Deltari culture prizes in all domains.

### The Black Ledger

Kowloon's **Black Ledger** — the sealed census of individuals transferred to the Spirebound Conclave during the Ash-and-Iron Decades — is the single most painful subject in Deltari cultural memory. Deltari oral tradition preserves names from that ledger that the official records do not, maintained by community mnemonists who have passed the list down for generations specifically because the official records were sealed.

The Deltari position on the Black Ledger is consistent: it should be opened. The people listed in it should be named. Their descendant families should be formally acknowledged. This is not vengeance but what their own tradition demands: the record is the agreement, and a record that has been hidden is a record that is being violated.

The Jade Magistracy's continued refusal to open the ledger creates a persistent tension between Deltari communities and Kowloon's official institutions that has never been fully resolved.

### Water-Reading

The Deltari perceptual gift — their ability to **read the flow** — extends beyond literal water. In the same way that a skilled physician can read the state of a body through pulse and breath, skilled Deltari readers can assess the state of complex systems through the patterns of movement within them: market flows, information flows, power flows. They describe it in water metaphors — eddies where current meets obstruction, the turbulence downstream from structural failure, the deep smooth current that indicates genuine depth.

This makes Deltari gifted clerics, psionicists, and advisors. Their wisdom is not abstract — it is applied. They understand why things happen through the same perceptual framework they use to understand why rivers run where they do.

## Racial Traits

**Stat Profile:** STR 12 | INT 18 | WIS 22 | DEX 18 | CON 14

Deltari are wisdom-dominant, with strong practical intelligence and good dexterity reflecting their fluid-environment adaptation. Their constitution and strength are moderate — they are not built for sustained heavy combat but for sustained complex environments.

**Elemental Affinities:**
- **Strong in:** Water, Mental — Deltari are water-adapted and their mental acuity resonates with the flowing, interconnected nature of mental magic; their spellwork in these domains is precise and powerful
- **Weak in:** Fire, Earth — fire is antithetical to their water-adapted physiology; earth is the antithesis of flow, blocking and containing rather than moving
- **Resistant to:** Water, Poison — Deltari are thoroughly adapted to aquatic environments and the natural toxins present in delta water systems; these elements hold no threat
- **Susceptible to:** Fire, Earth — fire can rapidly overwhelm their water-based physiological defenses; earth-magic that creates compression or blocking effects is particularly effective against beings whose survival instinct is always to flow around obstacles rather than through them

**Racial Skills:** hunt, spell critical damage, spell critical

**Hunt** reflects the Deltari's extraordinary environmental tracking ability — applied to the chase and pursuit, the same perceptual gift that reads water currents reads the movement of prey through terrain.

**Spell critical damage** and **spell critical** reflect the precision of Deltari spellwork: their exceptional wisdom produces the kind of targeted magical strike that finds exact vulnerabilities rather than broad damage.

**Racial Modifiers:** None beyond the base humanoid template — Deltari are adaptations within human physiological range rather than dramatically different biology

**Wear Slots:** Standard humanoid — Head, Face (not Beak), Hands (not Claws)

## Relationship to the World

### Kowloon

Kowloon is the Deltari's primary civilization, and their relationship to it is one of deep ambivalence. The city is theirs — built on foundations they laid, maintained by hydraulic engineering they developed — but its institutional governance is not. The Jade Magistracy, the Courier Lantern Office, and the guild structures that control Kowloon's formal power have consistently marginalized Deltari communities in official civic structures while relying on their practical expertise.

The Deltari's parallel record-keeping — flow records, community ledgers, oral genealogies — constitutes a shadow archive of Kowloon's actual history that the official institutions would prefer not to have accessible. This gives Deltari community elders significant informal influence that the formal civic hierarchy cannot fully suppress.

### Akh'enet

The historical connection between Akh'enet and the Reed Ledger Warden tradition means Deltari communities have deep genealogical ties to the southern mortuary-port even at this distance. The Warden faction's commitment to practical water law over ritual doctrine is Deltari philosophy exported south; the Jackal Balance Court's descent into "bureaucratic metaphysics" is, in Deltari oral tradition, exactly what happens when record-keeping is captured by ritual rather than remaining in service to practical management.

Deltari traders who pass through the Akh'enet ruins typically seek the surviving Warden-controlled infrastructure rather than the more visible Sepulchrist monuments; they come for the gauges and pressure records, not the silence chambers.

### The Oasis Network

Deltari water-reading skills make them invaluable in the oasis network. They can assess water quality, channel integrity, and aquifer health with accuracy that Keeper-era survey equipment approached but rarely exceeded. Several Deltari communities maintain formal agreements with Great Oasis administrators to provide hydraulic assessment services in exchange for formal recognition of their flow records as legally admissible evidence in water-rights disputes — a recognition the Reed Ledger Warden tradition has sought for generations.

### The Withered Depths

The Withered Depths' corruption affects water quality along forest trade routes that Deltari merchants and water-assessors regularly travel. Deltari flow records contain the earliest documented observations of "anomalous decay patterns" in northern forest water systems — observations that Midgaard's arcanist registry filed without action. The Deltari regard this as evidence of institutional water-blindness: a civilization that does not read its water will eventually be destroyed by what it fails to notice.

### Humans

The Deltari built Kowloon and are governed by a city they built. This is not metaphor. The delta's hydraulic infrastructure — the channel management, water-quality monitoring, flood-defense systems, and cistern networks that keep the city alive — was designed and constructed by Deltari communities managing that delta for generations before the Neon Covenant's six houses arrived. The city exists because Deltari knowledge made it possible.

The Jade Magistracy's exclusion of Deltari communities from formal civic power while depending entirely on their practical expertise is the defining dynamic of that relationship. The Black Ledger's sealed census, which includes Deltari among those transferred to the Conclave, is an additional layer: the city's founding institutions not only exploited Deltari expertise but transferred Deltari people to Conclave experimentation and then sealed the record.

Deltari parallel record-keeping — flow records, community ledgers, oral genealogies maintained across generations — is the most effective leverage Deltari communities have against this arrangement. The Jade Magistracy cannot fully suppress records it does not hold. It has tried. It continues to try. The records remain.

### Serathi

The Deltari read water; the Serathi read sky. Both are sophisticated observational traditions organized around complementary environmental substrates, and the two peoples have maintained an exchange relationship in the Kowloon area where Serathi eastern-range corridors cross above the Jhen River delta. Sky-readers and flow-record keepers contribute observations the other cannot make: the Serathi track the delta's surface behavior from altitude (channel migration patterns visible only from above, seasonal flooding extent, the movement of sea weather systems toward the coast); the Deltari track the same territory's hydraulic behavior at surface and subsurface level.

The relationship is professional and respectful — both peoples recognize in the other the epistemological rigor they respect most: careful observation, systematic record, and honest acknowledgment of what cannot yet be known.

See also: `serathi_lore.md`

### Kethari

The Deltari and the Kethari have parallel institutional positions in their respective corridors: both built the infrastructure that makes major human cities viable, both are formally excluded from governance of those cities, both maintain parallel records that are more accurate than the official records that occasionally reference them. The Deltari's flow records of Kowloon are the water equivalent of the Kethari's water-memory of the eastern aquifer.

In the oasis network, both peoples provide hydraulic expertise that the human administrators who formally govern the Deepwell Confluence depend on. Deltari assessment capability and Kethari water-memory knowledge are complementary — Deltari methods are more systematic and transferable; Kethari knowledge is deeper and longer. Formal cooperation between the two peoples in the oasis network would constitute the most comprehensive water-management knowledge base in the corridor. That cooperation is informal and occasional rather than institutionalized, for the same reasons that each people's formal institutional access is limited: the relevant human institutions prefer advisors who do not have independent territorial claims.

## Narrative Tone

Deltari NPCs should feel practically wise and quietly confident — people who have been reading complex systems for their entire lives and have the hard-won certainty of those who know that patience and observation reliably beat haste and assumption.

They are not arrogant. Arrogance implies contempt for other people's knowledge; Deltari are more accurately described as having a well-calibrated confidence in their own methods and a genuine curiosity about methods they haven't encountered. They ask a lot of questions. They follow threads of reasoning past the point where other interlocutors have gotten bored.

They trust records over memory (other people's memory), current conditions over historical expectations, and direct observation over received wisdom. They are, ultimately, empiricists — people who believe the world will tell you what you need to know if you pay attention carefully enough and write it down.

# Lore: The Serathi

## Core Identity

The **Serathi** are avian humanoids of the western sky-roads — a people of wing, wind, and altitude whose civilization predates the road-building Keeper surveyors by generations. They are swift, observant, and lethally precise: hunters by inheritance, navigators by necessity, and scholars of the air in the way the Deltari are scholars of water.

Where other peoples orient themselves by roads, rivers, and city walls, the Serathi orient themselves by **sky-lines** — the horizon profiles of mountain ridges, forest canopies, and coast-breaks that form a navigational map legible only from above. Every adult Serathi has memorized the sky-line of their home territory to a precision that allows identification of location at a glance from any altitude, even in low cloud. The same perceptual gift that makes them extraordinary hunters makes them unparalleled scouts and couriers.

They are not a gentle people, but they are a principled one. The sky does not lie — it is too vast, too clear, too merciless in its transparency. The Serathi have absorbed this quality from the element they live in: they are direct, honest to a fault, and find the evasion and face-saving that characterizes surface-bound diplomacy genuinely difficult to navigate.

## Origin

Serathi oral tradition does not begin with a creation myth in the conventional sense. They do not claim to have been made by a god or to have emerged from an element or an event. Instead, their oldest stories begin with **the first sky-reading** — the moment when the first Serathi fledgling climbed to a peak and looked out at a world visible in its entirety, unobstructed by walls or trees or hills, and understood for the first time that the world has shape.

"We saw the world before the world knew it could be seen," is the phrase their elder readers use when asked about origins. It is less explanation than claim: the Serathi believe their people defined the landscape by observing it, that the sky-lines memorized by a thousand generations of Serathi constitute a kind of ongoing record of the world that exists independently of human documentation.

Keeper civilization's surveyors were the first non-Serathi to formally document the Serathi's existence, recording them in the western frontier surveys as **"elevated territorial peoples with exceptional aerial mobility and aggressive defense of sky-road corridors"**. The description is accurate as far as it goes and misses almost everything that matters.

What the Keeper surveyors noted but did not fully understand was that the routes they were mapping as "new" trade roads — particularly the corridor that would become **Roc Road** — had been navigated by Serathi sky-readers for centuries. The road was built along a path the Serathi had been flying for generations. The Keepers named it for the roc mythology that Serathi presence had embedded in western frontier folklore: travelers who reported enormous winged shapes in the sky above the western passes were not, usually, seeing rocs. They were seeing Serathi hunting parties at altitude.

## Physical Description

Serathi stand in the moderate human height range but carry themselves differently — their center of gravity is forward and upward, their posture angled slightly as though always on the verge of launching into motion. Their most immediately striking feature is their **plumage**: full-body feathering in patterns that range from the deep brown-and-gold of the desert hawk through the grey-white of high-altitude predators to the rare iridescent storm-black of Serathi from the coastal approach territories near Mafdet.

Their heads are avian: a sharp, curved **beak** replaces the lower face, sized for precision work (cracking shells, opening small prey) rather than brute force. Their eyes are large, amber to deep gold, set wide on the face for broad field of vision, with a resolution at distance that other races cannot match. In low light, their pupils expand to near-full iris coverage, giving them a distinctive black-eyed appearance that surface peoples find unsettling. They hear through feathered ear-recesses rather than external ears — the feathers amplify specific frequency ranges useful for detecting prey movement at distance.

Their arms are standard humanoid in structure, ending in three-fingered hands with partially fused knuckles that allow strong gripping. Their **wings** — the feature that most defines them to outsiders — extend from the upper back between the shoulder blades, with a wingspan in full adults that ranges from eight to twelve feet tip-to-tip. The wings are strong enough for sustained flight in appropriate conditions, though Serathi flight is more accurately described as extended gliding with powerful updraft exploitation rather than the continuous powered flight of birds. They are most effective as fliers in the mountain passes and coastal cliff zones of their home territories, where thermal currents allow soaring.

Their lower limbs are powerful and densely muscled, ending in **taloned feet** whose grip can support full body weight from vertical surfaces. The talons map, in game terms, to the **HOOVES** slot — not because they are hooves in any literal sense, but because the specialized foot-wear and talon-guards that Serathi use for protection and grip-enhancement occupy the same equipment niche. Standard boots do not fit; the taloned clasp-guards and wrapped talon-braces that Serathi use instead are the relevant equipment type.

Their **tail feathers** are a significant social indicator: the length, condition, and pattern of tail feathers communicates status, territorial affiliation, and breeding condition among Serathi in ways that other races learn to read only with some exposure. A Serathi with damaged tail feathers is either very young, very poor, or very reckless — and most adult Serathi will tell you which on sight.

## Culture and Society

### The Sky-Road Compact

Serathi social organization is territorial in the precise sense: their communities occupy **sky-road corridors** — defined aerial routes above specific terrain features — rather than fixed ground settlements. A Serathi community's "territory" is a three-dimensional volume of air above a ground feature, and their civic law governs access to that air-space rather than ownership of the land below.

The **Sky-Road Compact** is the governing agreement that structures relations between Serathi communities: a set of protocols for navigating corridor boundaries, resolving disputes over hunting grounds, and maintaining the shared sky-line records that serve as the Serathi equivalent of property documents. It predates any human institution in the western territories and has proven more durable than most: the Compact's territorial definitions have survived the rise of Kiess, the collapse of Evermeet, and the construction of Roc Road, adapting to each without requiring fundamental revision.

The Compact's core provision is simple: **any Serathi may fly any sky-road if they carry nothing taken from another corridor's territory**. This is primarily a hunting law — Serathi may cross territorial boundaries freely as long as they do not carry prey taken in another community's corridor — but it has been extended, through precedent, to cover information, property, and diplomatic passage.

### The Reader Tradition

The most respected social role in Serathi culture is the **sky-reader** — not a priest or scholar in the conventional sense, but a specialist in the interpretive reading of aerial observation. Sky-readers are the Serathi equivalent of historians, judges, and navigators combined: they maintain the sky-line records, adjudicate territorial disputes using observed evidence (flight paths, prey distribution, wind pattern changes), and serve as the institutional memory of their corridor.

A sky-reader's primary tool is the **roost-chronicle** — a continuous record of observed aerial phenomena maintained at specific observation points called **high roosts**. High roosts are the nearest thing Serathi culture has to permanent structures: elevated platforms on ridge-lines or cliff faces where sky-readers maintain weather records, migration data, and sky-line observations across generations. Some high roosts in the western territories have been continuously occupied for longer than Kiess has existed.

The transition from hunter to sky-reader is not automatic. A Serathi demonstrates readiness by successfully naming and describing, from memory, the complete sky-line of three territories not their own. The test requires spending extended periods in each territory — observing from altitude, recording detail, committing to memory — before returning to perform the recitation before a gathering of established sky-readers. Those who pass are considered to have completed their adult formation.

### The Hunt as Institution

Serathi hunting is not merely subsistence — it is civic practice. The **communal hunt** is the primary ritual expression of Serathi community identity, the event around which territorial relationships are negotiated and status is demonstrated. Hunt performance is observed and evaluated by sky-readers, who record the results in roost-chronicles alongside weather data and territorial observations.

This gives Serathi combat a characteristic quality: they approach violence the way they approach hunting — not with rage or contempt but with the focused precision of a predator selecting its strike point. They are rarely reckless; they take risks they have calculated and avoid risks they have not. A Serathi who strikes without having identified the optimal strike point is considered poorly trained, not boldly aggressive.

Their fighting style relies on the same tools as their hunting: altitude, speed, accurate identification of vulnerabilities, and the willingness to disengage if the strike fails and reposition for a better opportunity. Fighting on the ground, in close quarters, is something Serathi learn because they must, not something they prefer. Given the choice, a Serathi confrontation happens in the air, at the attacker's chosen moment, from a position the target cannot easily match.

### The Kiess Relationship and Evermeet's Memory

Serathi communities in the western territories have the longest recorded history of contact with Kiess of any people except the forest-edge communities that preceded the city's founding. Kiess was built on the ruins of Evermeet, an earlier civilization whose collapse the Serathi sky-readers observed from above and documented in roost-chronicles that still exist.

The Serathi version of Evermeet's collapse differs significantly from the fragmentary human records that Kiess's Syndic Council maintains. Serathi chronicles describe Evermeet not as a city that fell to external attack but as a city that **consumed itself** — a civilization that over-expanded its institutional claims while its physical infrastructure degraded, until the gap between what it claimed to govern and what it could actually maintain became too large to bridge. The Syndic Council has, on several occasions, requested access to these chronicles. On each occasion, the Serathi sky-readers have offered to perform a recitation of the relevant sections — they do not provide physical copies of roost-chronicles — in exchange for formal recognition of specific sky-road corridors that Kiess's expanding overland infrastructure has encroached upon.

The negotiation has been ongoing for several generations. The sky-readers do not appear to be in a hurry.

## Racial Traits

**Stat Profile:** STR 16 | INT 12 | WIS 12 | DEX 22 | CON 22

Serathi are defined by two equally dominant traits: extraordinary dexterity and exceptional physical endurance. The dexterity is the expression of generations optimized for aerial precision, fine motor control in flight, and the split-second decision-making of a predator adjusting strike angle at speed. The constitution reflects a different truth about avian life that ground-bound peoples often miss: sustained flight is brutally demanding, and Serathi bodies are built to endure that demand across long-range hunts and territorial patrols that would exhaust other races. Their strength is solid for a people that is not primarily ground-fighters — wing-launching from a static position requires explosive muscle force, and their arms and shoulders are considerably more powerful than their lean builds suggest. Their intelligence and wisdom are low by the standards of magically-oriented races: Serathi are not scholars or theorists. They are hunters and observers. They think in terrain, wind, and angle, not in text or doctrine.

**Elemental Affinities:**
- **Strong in:** Air, Mental — the Serathi are creatures of the open sky, and their spellwork resonates with the forces of wind, atmosphere, and directed perception. Their mental affinity reflects the sky-reading tradition: a people whose primary intellectual work involves processing vast quantities of observed data and identifying meaningful patterns have minds that resonate naturally with the mental realms.
- **Weak in:** Earth, Physical — ground is where Serathi are disadvantaged. Earth-based magic disorients them by anchoring forces that they instinctively seek to rise above. Physical combat without positional advantage is their most compromised fighting condition.
- **Resistant to:** Air, Poison — wind cannot destabilize those who live within it; the Serathi's high-altitude environmental exposure has produced immunity to most airborne toxins and atmospheric disturbances.
- **Susceptible to:** Earth, Fire — ground-anchoring effects and fire both specifically target the Serathi's anatomy and nature. Fire destroys feathers with catastrophic speed; earth-binding magic eliminates their positional advantage and forces them to fight at their worst.

**Racial Skills:** hunt, 'enhanced critical', counter, sneak

**Hunt** is the foundational Serathi skill — the aerial predator's ability to track prey across terrain and strike at the optimal moment. Applied to combat, it reflects the Serathi's trained ability to identify and pursue targets across changing environments.

**Enhanced critical** reflects the precise strike-point selection that characterizes Serathi combat training. They do not strike the hardest; they strike exactly. The same skill that drives a talon into a prey animal's spine rather than its shoulder applies to identifying unarmored gaps and physiological vulnerabilities in opponents.

**Counter** reflects Serathi endurance rather than toughness. They are not armored like the Khephari or stone-dense like the Ashborn, but they are built for sustained physical effort in conditions that would exhaust other races — long territorial patrols, multi-hour hunts at altitude, the constant muscular demand of flight. They absorb punishment and keep moving because that is what their physiology is designed to do.

**Sneak** is an unusual skill for a creature with a twelve-foot wingspan, but Serathi approach concealment differently than ground-bound peoples. From altitude, at sufficient distance, even large predators are invisible against sky-glare and cloud cover. The Serathi's sneak ability reflects high-altitude concealment, shadow-approach from above, and the timing discipline that allows a hunting bird to approach prey without triggering the prey's peripheral awareness.

**Racial Modifiers:** Tail (tail feathers, significant social indicator), Fast Heal (avian metabolic rate accelerates physical recovery)

**Class Affinity:** Cipher (DEX), Pugilist (CON), Warden (STR), Psionicist, Magi, Cleric (lowest — WIS 12 makes divine channeling poor)

**Wear Slots:** Beak (not Face), Wings (additional), Hooves (talon-guards, not standard Feet), Tail — Serathi cannot wear standard helms (feathered crest, no room for helmets) or face guards (beak anatomy), cannot wear standard boots (talon anatomy), cannot wear standard ear-jewelry (feathered ear-recesses), but can wear beak-bindings, wing-accessories, talon-guards, and tail-decorations. They retain standard hand, arm, wrist, finger, and body slots.

## Relationship to the World

### Kiess

The relationship between Serathi sky-road communities and Kiess is the defining political dynamic of the western territories. The city built on Evermeet's ruins occupies territory below and adjacent to several major Serathi sky-road corridors, and its institutional expansion — road building, overland trade infrastructure, northern frontier development — has repeatedly encroached on aerial territories that the Sky-Road Compact considers established Serathi corridors.

The Serathi response to this encroachment has been consistent: they document it (sky-readers add every new structure to the roost-chronicles with the date of first observation), they protest it through the formal mechanism of sky-road declaration (hovering in formation above the encroaching structure and performing the territorial recitation), and they negotiate it through the established process of corridor-for-chronicle exchange. They do not wage war over it. They do not abandon the corridors. They are persistent without being aggressive, which Kiess's Syndic Council finds simultaneously reassuring and maddening.

See also: `kelshadra_lore.md`, `eccentric_woodland_lore.md`

### The Forest of Confusion and Verdant Depths

Serathi sky-roads cross above the Forest of Confusion, and their roost-chronicles contain the longest continuous aerial observation record of the forest's degradation into the Withered Depths' corrupted state. Sky-readers documented the first signs of the blight — changes in canopy color, altered migration patterns of forest-edge species, the unusual behavior of the forest's bird population — decades before Kiess's ground-level scouts reported anything unusual.

Their relationship with the Rivennid is limited but respectful: the Serathi observe from above the same forest that the Rivennid inhabit below, and their complementary perspectives on the forest's state have occasionally produced useful intelligence for both peoples. The Serathi's primary concern is the disruption of thermal-current patterns that the dead forest zones create — the blight changes ground-surface temperature in ways that destabilize the updraft columns that Serathi use for sustained flight over the affected area.

See also: `rivennid_lore.md`, `verdant_depths_lore.md`, `withered_depths_lore.md`

### Midgaard

Serathi presence in Midgaard is occasional rather than permanent. Individual Serathi appear in the city as couriers, information brokers, or travelers in transit between western and eastern territories. A small number have taken up residence in Midgaard's higher districts, where the architectural elevation provides approximations of the raised observation points they prefer.

The Reckoning faction in Midgaard has cultivated relationships with Serathi sky-readers, recognizing that aerial observation records — particularly for the period of the Spirebound Conclave's most active operations — may contain evidence of physical infrastructure that the sealed warrants and Black Ledger were meant to obscure. Serathi sky-readers flew above the Conclave's Cinderteeth sanctum during the Ash-and-Iron Decades and documented the physical extent of its operations. Whether those records constitute useful evidence for Reckoning scholars' purposes is a question both parties are interested in answering.

See also: `human_lore.md`, `ashborn_lore.md`

### The Ashborn

The Serathi and the Ashborn share an ancestral proximity to the Cinderteeth Mountains, approaching that geography from opposite orientations: the Ashborn are formed by the mountain's fire from below; the Serathi observe it from above. Their relationship is not warm, but it is characterized by mutual practical respect.

Serathi sky-readers documented the Ashfall Era's eruption sequence from a perspective that no ground-bound observer could match. Their chronicles describe the eruption plumes' spread, the ash-cloud movement, and the precise corridors where the ashfall was most intense — observations that, if combined with the Khephari's ground-level seismic records, would constitute a complete reconstruction of the event that current human institutional history has treated only partially. The Ashborn have interest in these chronicles; sky-readers are willing to trade access to sky-road passage rights.

See also: `ashborn_lore.md`, `cinderteeth_mountains_lore.md`, `sunken_sanctum_lore.md`

### The Deltari

The Serathi respect the Deltari in the way that any people with a sophisticated observational tradition respects another people with a similarly sophisticated observational tradition organized around a different substrate. Deltari read water; Serathi read sky. Their methods differ; their epistemological approach — patient observation, systematic record, judgment based on accumulated data rather than convention — is recognizably similar.

The two peoples' territories overlap in the Kowloon area, where Serathi eastern-range corridors cross above the Jhen River delta. Sky-readers and Deltari flow-record keepers have exchanged information about seasonal pattern changes in this zone for generations, each contributing observations the other cannot make from their native orientation.

See also: `deltari_lore.md`, `kowloon_lore.md`

### Humans

The Serathi's long experience with human civilization is primarily an experience of **institutional lag** — the gap between what a human institution claims to govern and what it can actually observe. Serathi sky-readers have watched human road networks, cities, and governance structures from above for as long as those structures have existed, and what they observe most consistently is that the people inside the institutions have an incomplete picture of the territory those institutions claim to administer.

This is not contempt. Serathi understand that ground-level perspective has advantages their aerial view lacks — detail, texture, social complexity. What they find consistently remarkable is the confidence with which human institutions make claims about the state of territory they have never actually seen from the relevant angle. The Serathi equivalent of arrogance is claiming to know a sky-line you have not personally flown. The human equivalent, from a Serathi perspective, seems to be claiming administrative authority over territory you have not personally observed.

The relationship is not hostile — Serathi couriers are valued throughout the five-city network, and Serathi intelligence work is sought by anyone willing to pay the corridor-recognition price — but it maintains a consistent quality of the Serathi watching the humans from a position the humans cannot fully perceive.

See also: `human_lore.md`

### Khenari

The Serathi find the Khenari psychopomps interesting in a way they do not find most other peoples interesting: both cultures have developed sophisticated systems for processing the transition between states (life/death for Khenari; ground/sky for Serathi), and both understand that transition as requiring careful documentation. The sky-reader and the death-ledger keeper are performing structurally similar work from very different perspectives.

In practice, Serathi couriers and Khenari notaries occasionally work the same routes — both are valued for reliability and incorruptibility, and caravan operators frequently employ one of each for a major transit. The professional respect is genuine and the personal connection is limited: Khenari find the Serathi's aerial perspective interesting but somewhat abstract; Serathi find the Khenari's death-administration fascinating but overly focused on the endpoint rather than the journey.

See also: `khenari_lore.md`, `akhenet_lore.md`

### Kethari

The Serathi and the Kethari share the experience of being peoples whose observational territories the Keeper civilization surveyed, documented, and then partially incorporated into administrative frameworks without fully understanding what those territories contained. Both have maintained parallel records of the world's physical state over periods longer than most human institutional memories.

The Serathi observe the Kethari's Saltglass territories from above and have roost-chronicle records of the Saltglass Reach's physical changes across generations — the shifting of the mirror flats, the expansion of the glasswind belts, the gradual alteration of the coastal approach corridors. The Kethari, in turn, have traditions regarding the aerial predators whose hunting patterns they observe from desert-floor vantage points. The two peoples' knowledge of each other is largely based on observation from a distance; direct contact is infrequent but not hostile.

See also: `kethari_lore.md`, `saltglass_reach_lore.md`, `mafdet_lore.md`

## Narrative Tone

Serathi NPCs should feel precise, direct, and physically self-possessed — people who have extensive experience calculating their position relative to everything around them and who bring that same precision to social interaction. They are not aggressive, but they are not comfortable with imprecision or ambiguity: a Serathi who says something means it literally and expects the same in return.

Their humor tends toward the observational — noting incongruities between what things are claimed to be and what they actually are, from a position of literal higher perspective. This is not malicious; it is the reflexive application of a sky-reader's training to the social landscape.

In combat, they should feel deliberate and positionally aware — always calculating angles, always looking for the elevated position, always ready to disengage and reapproach rather than commit to a prolonged close-quarters struggle. A Serathi who is winning is in control of the geometry; a Serathi who is losing is trying to regain altitude (metaphorically or literally).

They are not sentimental but they are loyal: the sky-road communities are tight-knit precisely because territorial survival depends on communal maintenance of the roost-chronicles and the Compact. An obligation accepted is an obligation kept. A sky-road promised is a sky-road honored. Their commitment to those they have chosen to work with is the same quality that makes them excellent couriers and reliable intelligence sources.

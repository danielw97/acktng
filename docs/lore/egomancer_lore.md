# Lore: The Egomancer Tradition

## Overview

Egomancers emerged from within the Psionicist tradition itself — specifically from the **Assertion Wing**, a school of thought that argued the mind's primary power lay not in perception or communication but in **imposition**. Early Assertionists developed techniques for overriding external cognitive processes, bending probability through focused intent, and sustaining mental pressure across extended engagements rather than delivering single decisive bursts. Their methods were considered extreme by conservative academies and several Assertion teachers were expelled. The expelled faculty simply founded independent schools. Psionicists who return through the mortal path and commit to the imposition doctrine become Egomancers — practitioners of the Assertion Wing's full and unfettered philosophy.

---

## I. The Assertion Wing and the Academic Mainstream

The **Assertion Wing** of the psionicist academies developed from an internal theoretical dispute that predates the Wing's formal identification as a distinct school. The dispute concerned what the tradition called the **directionality question**: when a practitioner exercises mental force, is the force primarily directed inward (toward understanding one's own cognitive architecture, protecting it from external influence, maintaining clarity under mental pressure) or outward (toward shaping the cognitive architecture of others, imposing specific cognitive states, redirecting attention and will)?

The academic mainstream had, through most of the tradition's development, emphasized the inward direction — partly because the tradition's foundational lineage (the Pale Ledger's cognitive architecture research) had been structured around understanding minds from the inside, and partly because outward imposition raised immediate ethical concerns that inward focus avoided. The practitioners who were most comfortable with the tradition's ethical framework were those who treated their practice as a form of self-knowledge and self-protection rather than as a tool for managing others.

The Assertion Wing's argument was that this ethical comfort came at a theoretical cost: if the mind's architecture can be understood from the inside, and if that architecture is genuinely effective as a domain of power, then a practitioner who refuses to develop the outward direction is not being ethical — they are choosing ignorance of a domain they don't understand. Understanding how imposition works is necessary for effective defense against imposition; refusing to develop imposition technique leaves the practitioner with a theoretical model of outward cognitive force that has never been tested against actual resistance.

The mainstream's response — that developing imposition technique requires treating other minds as objects rather than subjects, which violates the foundational epistemological premise that makes cognitive knowledge possible — was philosophically coherent. The Assertion Wing's counter-response — that understanding the mechanism of an operation is separate from the ethical status of performing it — was also philosophically coherent. The two positions are not obviously resolvable through argument because they are based on different foundational commitments about what makes cognitive knowledge possible.

## II. The Expulsion and Independent Schools

The expulsion of several Assertion Wing teachers from affiliated academies was not a single event but a **series of individual decisions** made by different academy administrations at different times, for different immediate reasons, that accumulated into the effective institutional separation of the Wing from the academic mainstream.

The sequence was consistent: an Assertion Wing teacher would develop techniques that crossed the mainstream's ethical threshold; they would be warned; they would continue; they would be expelled; they would establish an independent school rather than seek reinstatement. The independence was never framed as rebellion — it was framed as **continuation of research the academies had declined to support**, which is technically accurate and also strategically useful framing: it positioned the expelled faculty as scholars pursuing legitimate inquiry rather than as expelled practitioners seeking revenge on institutions that had wronged them.

The independent schools maintained informal relationships with each other and with the academic mainstream through individual practitioner contacts — the psionicist community was never large enough that institutional separation created complete personal separation. Practitioners from mainstream institutions and independent schools occasionally collaborated on research, though the collaboration was usually not acknowledged in formal documentation from either side.

The independent school structure produced Egomancer training environments that were substantially different from mainstream psionicist training. Without the academic framework's credential requirements and peer-review culture, independent school instruction was more directly practitioner-to-practitioner: transmission of what worked, tested against the specific students' capabilities, without the requirement to justify each technique against theoretical standards the mainstream had established.

## III. Weaponized Cognition

The Egomancer's primary combat methodology is what the tradition calls **weaponized cognition** — the application of mental force not to produce physical damage but to produce specific cognitive states in opponents that make them ineffective. The techniques range along a spectrum from relatively subtle interventions (redirecting attention, disrupting concentration, creating momentary confusion) to substantially more intensive operations (overriding decision-making processes, sustaining mental pressure that prevents coherent response, fragmenting the target's situational awareness until they cannot function effectively).

The tradition's preference is for **sustained pressure** over single decisive bursts — a characteristic that distinguishes Egomancer practice from the more acute-intervention approach of the mainstream psionicist tradition. A sustained mental pressure that prevents coherent opponent response across an extended engagement is, in the Egomancer analysis, more operationally effective than a dramatic single intervention that the opponent can potentially recover from and adapt to.

This preference for sustained pressure also reflects the Assertion Wing's theoretical commitment: sustained pressure requires the practitioner to maintain accurate real-time modeling of the target's cognitive state, because the specific interventions that sustain effective pressure must track the target's adaptation attempts. A practitioner who delivers a single intervention and then monitors the result is doing something categorically different from a practitioner who maintains continuous modeling and continuous intervention simultaneously. The latter is more cognitively demanding and more operationally effective — and is the skill set that the Egomancer tradition's training is specifically designed to develop.

## IV. The Kinetimancer Convergence

The **Kinetimancer** adept synthesis that emerges from combining Necromancer and Egomancer practice was identified theoretically by the **Kinetic Formulists** before it was systematized by practitioners who had trained in both traditions. The Kinetic Formulists' manuscripts observed that Necromancer entropy-redirection and Egomancer intent-redirection were structural analogues: both were working with momentum, applied to different domains.

For Egomancer practitioners specifically, the convergence is interesting because it implies that the Assertion Wing's theoretical commitment — treating mind as a domain of power with genuinely effective force — has a domain analogue in entropy physics. The practitioner who has internalized the Assertion Wing's position (that cognitive force is real, that imposing cognitive states is a legitimate application of real force) finds that the Necromancer tradition's entropy work supports that position by demonstrating that the same structural operation applies in the physical domain of decay and dissolution.

The practical combination develops what practitioners describe as **predictive collapse** — the use of entropy effects to create specific physical vulnerabilities at moments calculated through cognitive modeling of the opponent's response patterns. Rather than deploying entropy effects and will-imposition separately, the integrated practitioner deploys them in calibrated sequence: entropy undermines the physical substrate that cognitive resistance depends on; will-imposition redirects the cognitive resources freed by that physical undermining; the combination produces outcomes that neither practice alone could achieve with the same efficiency.

## V. The Violet Compact Registry Problem

The Egomancer tradition's relationship with the **Violet Compact's registry law** is among the most institutionally complicated of any practitioner path. The registry law requires documentation of practice; Egomancer practice is by nature difficult to document in ways the registry can assess, because the effects occur in the target's cognitive architecture rather than in observable physical reality. A practitioner who has imposed a specific cognitive state on a target has done something real and significant; demonstrating to a registry official that it occurred, to what degree, and whether the specific technique employed was a registered authorized practice, is practically difficult.

The Egomancer independent schools have developed various responses to this challenge. Some maintain minimal registry documentation — filing credentials that acknowledge psionicist lineage without specifying Egomancer specialization — which keeps them nominally compliant while providing no information useful to registry oversight. Some maintain no registry documentation at all, operating in the same informal space as the tradition's independent school origins. Some have made more formal arrangements with specific city jurisdictions — Kiess's anti-sealed-warrant framework provides protections that benefit Egomancer practitioners in ways not specifically anticipated by its drafters.

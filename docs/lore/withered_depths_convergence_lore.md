# Lore: The Withered Depths Convergence

## The Shape of an Advance

The Withered Depths have been expanding for generations. This is not in dispute. What has been in dispute — and what the convergence of non-human observational records is beginning to resolve — is what kind of expansion it is.

Human institutional analysis has consistently framed the Withered Depths' advance as ecological: blight spreads because blight spreads, in the way fire spreads, the way flood spreads, through the path of least resistance and in proportion to available fuel. The remediation proposals emerging from Midgaard's scholars treat the expansion as a containment problem, a question of where to place firebreaks and how aggressively to harvest corrupted canopy before the zone of active advance reaches uninfected territory.

The non-human peoples whose observational records cover the Withered Depths from different angles have, over the last generation, begun to compare accounts. What the comparison reveals is that the Withered Depths is not expanding through the path of least resistance. It is expanding through a specific path that requires explanation.

## Five Observations That Do Not Fit the Ecological Model

### The Rivennid Reading: Deliberate Routing

The mycelial network that constitutes part of the Rivennid's biological substrate is continuous with the Withered Depths' fungal systems at a depth most non-Rivennid observers do not reach. What the Rivennid experience as network-memory — the continuous low-level information flow that constitutes background consciousness for connected Rivennid — includes, at its edges, faint data from the Withered Depths' own fungal structures.

The Rivennid do not communicate with the Blightmother. They are clear on this point. What they receive is not communication but residual signal: the pattern of what the Depths' mycelial networks are actually doing, the same way a river's current carries information about upstream conditions without the river intending to send a message.

What the residual signal shows is routing. The Withered Depths' advance is not expanding into territory because the territory is available. It is routing around something. The leading edge of the Blightmother's influence consistently avoids specific ground even when that ground appears, by all surface indicators, to be as vulnerable as adjacent territory that has been consumed. When Rivennid network-holders attempt to describe the shape of the avoidance, they reach for the same word: the Depths are routing around something buried, the way roots route around stone.

What is buried is not known.

### The Khephari Reading: Crystal Resonance Underground

Khephari seismic knowledge is the product of generations spent building and maintaining heavy stone infrastructure in geologically active territory. The great road engineers who constructed the Pyramid's approach ways and laid the structural foundations of Roc Road did not merely move stone; they learned to read it, to feel through tools and feet and fingertips the vibrational language of load-bearing geological structure.

In the western territories, Khephari who maintain the road segments closest to the Forest of Confusion have documented, over the last several generations, a progressive change in the seismic character of the approach routes. The change is not dramatic — not the seismic stress that precedes the Cinderteeth's periodic eruption cycles — but it is consistent and directional. The vibrational frequency they associate with old crystal-matrix resonance, documented in their records from the Conclave period when Khephari engineers were brought in to assess structural impacts on road foundations, is returning. Not from the east, where the Cinderteeth matrices were concentrated, but from the northwest, where the Conclave's Forest of Confusion field trial sites were located.

The crystal matrices are reactivating. Or something is reactivating them. This has been in the Khephari engineers' supplemental records — the category of observation that is documented and filed without expectation of institutional action — for thirty years. No road-law maintenance body has requested a briefing.

### The Serathi Reading: The Survey Routes

Serathi sky-readers maintain roost-chronicle records of the Withered Depths' aerial profile across more than a century of documented observation. The canopy structure visible from altitude has changed progressively, but the pattern of that change is not uniform radial expansion from a central point. It maps, the sky-readers have noted, onto the Spirebound Conclave's old survey routes.

The Conclave's surveyors were themselves documented from altitude during the Conclave's operational period — Serathi sky-readers in the western territories tracked their movements as a matter of standard territorial practice, in the same way they track any organized ground movement through corridor airspace. The roost-chronicles from that period include detailed records of which forest areas the Conclave's survey parties visited and in what sequence.

When a contemporary sky-reader overlays the modern Withered Depths' expansion pattern onto the historical survey-route records, the correspondence is not perfect but is far too close to be coincidental. The Depths is not expanding randomly. It is, in some fashion, following the path the Conclave walked.

### The Deltari Reading: Rerouted Water

Deltari flow-readers in the tributaries feeding the western forest zones have documented a hydraulic anomaly over the past two decades: the forest's water table is being rerouted, not depleted. This is a technically specific distinction that most non-Deltari find difficult to grasp, but the practical implication is clear: whatever is happening underground in the Withered Depths is redirecting subsurface water flows away from the areas of healthy forest and toward something in the deeper zones.

A depleting water table would suggest consumption — something drinking the forest dry. A rerouting water table suggests a different mechanism: something directing the water where it wants the water to go. Deltari flow-readers have traced the rerouting pattern as far as their access to tributary networks permits, which does not extend to the Depths' interior. What they can say is that the destination of the rerouted flow appears to be a region of the Depths that corresponds, roughly, to the area Serathi records identify as a Conclave survey site of particular intensity — the location where multiple survey parties returned repeatedly rather than passing through once.

Whatever is buried there, the Depths appears to be watering it.

### The Kethari Reading: The Aquifer's Silence

The Kethari's contribution to the Withered Depths picture is a negative observation, and negative observations are, in the Kethari's archival tradition, among the most significant kinds. The water-memory of the Kethari's westernmost communities — those whose territory reaches into the terrain adjacent to the Forest of Confusion's approach zones — includes a consistent feature that the elders describe as **the silence**: a zone in the aquifer where normal subsurface water activity is simply absent.

Aquifer silence is not drought. A drought produces reduced activity across a wide area. Aquifer silence is localized absence — the hydraulic signature of something blocking flow, absorbing flow, or redirecting flow at a specific point. The Kethari have documented this kind of silence before: it occurs naturally around dense igneous intrusions and artificially around large buried structures. The silence in the western aquifer is neither. Its shape and depth do not correspond to any geological formation in the Kethari's records, and it has been growing, slowly, in the same direction as the Withered Depths' advance.

The elders of the Kethari communities nearest the Forest of Confusion have been watching the silence for three generations. They have not advocated for action. They are watching to see what it does next.

## The Convergence Meeting

The five observational threads were brought together at a gathering that happened not by design but by coincidence: a Parallel Record Compact meeting called to discuss unrelated records was attended by representatives from all five communities, and a Rivennid delegate mentioned, as contextual background to a different discussion, the anomalous routing behavior they had been observing in the Depths' mycelial signals.

The Khephari delegate asked a question about the depth and frequency of the crystal resonance they were tracking in the western road foundations. The Serathi delegate described the survey-route mapping they had been maintaining. The Deltari delegate went very still — the characteristic Deltari posture of a flow-reader encountering a hydraulic pattern that reorders their model of a system — and then asked a precise question about the timing of the aquifer silence's growth. The Kethari elder traced the silence's geometric shape from memory.

The five accounts triangulated on a single region of the Withered Depths' interior: a zone corresponding to a Conclave survey site that, taken together, the records suggest was the Prism Thesis' primary crystal implantation point — the site of the most intensive root-network lattice work, the site the survey parties returned to most frequently, the site that the Depths' mycelial advance appears to be routing toward rather than away from.

Something is buried there. The Depths is not spreading. It is accumulating, routing water and crystal resonance toward a specific site, consuming the surrounding forest in what looks less like blight and more like preparation.

## What the Records Cannot Say

The consolidated observations define the anomaly's location, its hydraulic signature, its seismic character, its surface-aerial profile, and its mycelial routing behavior. What they cannot determine from external observation is what the buried object is, whether it is active or passive, and whether the Withered Depths' behavior toward it is instrumental — something the Depths is doing deliberately, in the sense that the Blightmother's custodial imperative constitutes deliberateness — or consequential, an incidental result of the Depths responding to whatever the buried object is doing.

The Rivennid position is that the distinction between instrumental and consequential may not be meaningful for an entity structured the way the Blightmother is. The custodial imperative, running without parameters, responds to what it perceives as threats and resources. If the buried object emits a signal that the Blightmother's crystal-resonance substrate reads as either a competing custodial authority or an unclaimed resource, the Depths' behavior toward it would be the same in either case.

The Kethari position is that whatever the buried object is, three generations of patience have not resolved the situation, and that the convergence of five independent observations on the same anomaly constitutes the kind of physical verification that warrants consideration of something more active than continued watching.

The Khenari position, stated with characteristic precision, is that if the buried object has victims — and the transfer-chain records suggest that whatever the Conclave buried, it buried with Conclave-era methodology, which means it buried with people — then those deaths have not been properly ledgered, and the question of what to do about the buried object is secondary to the question of the unlawful incompleteness of the record.

The question of what to do about what is buried in the Withered Depths is now, for the first time, under active collective discussion among five non-human peoples who have never before coordinated on a question that required active response rather than continued documentation.

No action has been taken. The discussion has been ongoing for one year. It continues.

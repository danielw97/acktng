# Lore: Thornwood

Derived from `area/thornwood.are` and cross-aligned with `shadowmere_lore.md`, `great_northern_forest_lore.md`, and `violet_eclipse_cathedral_lore.md`.

## Core Identity

Thornwood is a **necrotic estate-forest**: part abandoned noble domain, part living predatory woodland, part ossuary machine. It is not a normal wilderness and not a normal ruin. The estate did not simply decay into forest; the forest assimilated the estate's burial infrastructure and turned it into a self-maintaining ritual ecology.

This is why Thornwood reads as one place with three overlapping logics:
- **Manorial logic** (gates, halls, courtyards, hereditary symbols),
- **Funerary logic** (barrows, charnel verges, bone craft, reanimation),
- **Forest logic** (root pressure, predatory growth, adaptive pathways).

## Rough World Placement (Canon Proposal)

Thornwood should be treated as lying on the **northern grave frontier beyond Midgaard**, in the transition belt between the old burial tunnels and the southern edge of Shadowmere's blighted marches.

### Practical placement rules

1. **Primary overland identity:** a haunted woodland pocket north of Midgaard's grave districts, before the deepest Shadowmere interior.
2. **Subsurface identity:** reached via older burial/tunnel routes that also touch Shadowmere ingress infrastructure.
3. **Regional context:** culturally tied to the Great Northern Forest's outer funerary fringe, but ecologically more corrupted and enclosed than the Lantern Road corridor.

### Why this location fits existing in-game topology

- Thornwood room `1150` already links to graveyard tunnel room `11085`, establishing direct adjacency to Midgaard's grave-subterranean network.
- Shadowmere room `1400` also links to room `11085`, making that tunnel node a shared hinge between Shadowmere and Thornwood.
- Existing cathedral lore references thornwood material sourcing from the great northern forests, which supports placing Thornwood in a northern timber/funerary supply arc rather than in desert or coastal geographies.

In short: **Thornwood is best positioned as a liminal pocket between Midgaard's death infrastructure and Shadowmere's failed frontier state, on the southernmost haunted fringe of the greater northern forest system.**

## Historical Spine

### 1) Estate Founding: The Harren Claim

A minor frontier house (later remembered through Lord Harren's regalia) established a fortified woodland estate to control timber rights, hunting tithe, and burial land access. The house used imported ironwork and local black-oak construction, creating the "thorned" aesthetic as a status signal before it became literal.

### 2) Ossuary Expansion: Debt Through Burial

As regional conflict and plague pressure rose, the estate monetized death administration: burial plots, ossuary storage, and contracted grave maintenance for travelers and lesser houses. Barrows expanded faster than chapel capacity. Bone handling shifted from sacred rite to throughput logistics.

### 3) Cult Intrusion: The Root-Devotional Turn

A devotional current emerged among retainers, grave-tenders, and hedge practitioners who treated Thornwood itself as a listening intelligence. Their rites blended ancestor petitions, root sigils, and blood-fed oaths. They did not worship an outside god; they attempted direct covenant with the woodland substrate.

### 4) Collapse and Reversal

The covenant "answered" in the worst way. The estate's dead became usable matter, not honored memory. Bone workers became bone weavers. Knights became thornbound enforcers. Hall architecture and woodland growth synchronized into a single hostile system. The legal owner vanished from governance while remaining present in undeath-linked regalia traditions.

### 5) Current State

Modern Thornwood is a stable predatory equilibrium:
- cult remnants continue ritual maintenance,
- undead garrisons preserve estate territoriality,
- witches/shamans mediate with the living thicket intelligence,
- deep chambers preserve a high-order architect entity (the Thornwood Lich) as strategic and symbolic center.

## Faction and Role Lenses

### Estate Dead (Thorn Knights, Household Remnants)

They represent **continuity without legitimacy**. Their symbols still claim title and custody, but their authority is now backed by necrotic coercion and woodland adaptation rather than law.

### Thornwood Cultists and Shamans

They are not random madmen. They are technicians of exchange between flesh, root, and bone. Their doctrine is transactional: devotion in, guidance and survival out.

### Bone Weavers / Marrow Constructs

These entities embody Thornwood's shift from funerary reverence to funerary industry. The dead are reduced to modular material culture.

### Witch Figures

The thornwood witches predate or outlast estate hierarchy. They function as continuity anchors for older woodland rites and explain why Thornwood feels older than the manor remains visible on the surface.

## Environmental Story Rules

When extending Thornwood room/object/mob text, keep these constants:

1. **Nothing is purely decorative.** If it's carved, hung, planted, or sealed, it has a procedural function.
2. **Bone is infrastructure.** Bones are not only remains; they are reinforcement, indexing, and ritual tooling.
3. **Roots behave intentionally.** Paths close, split, and funnel movement as if responding to trespass.
4. **Cold + damp + iron scent palette.** Atmosphere should mix wet earth, oxidized metal, old blood, and fungal sweetness.
5. **Estate language survives in fragments.** Signets, courtesies, and titles persist, but are hollowed by undeath.

## Cross-Area Integration Anchors

### Shadowmere

Thornwood should read as the **rural funerary cousin** to Shadowmere's urban-containment catastrophe. Shadowmere industrialized death through state mandate; Thornwood localized it through estate-cult merger.

### Great Northern Forest

Thornwood sits outside normal Lantern Road social ecology. Use it as a high-threat anomaly node showing what happens when forest rites are fused with ossuary economics and left unchecked.

### Violet Eclipse Cathedral

The cathedral's thornwood codex-cover supply chain should be tied to legacy extraction contracts and smuggler procurement networks that treat Thornwood timber as premium ritual material despite contamination risks.

### Midgaard Grave Infrastructure

Tunnel and grave-route adjacency should be treated as intentional historical engineering: Thornwood profited from being reachable through death corridors while remaining deniable in civic registries.

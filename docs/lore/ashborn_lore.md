# Lore: The Ashborn

## Core Identity

The **Ashborn** are the living consequence of the Ashfall Era — humanoids whose bloodlines were irrevocably altered when volcanic fire from the Cinderteeth Mountains swept across the lowlands, leaving behind not only ruin but a new people: those whose bodies had absorbed the mountain's fire and learned to carry it.

They are not fire spirits, not demons, not constructs of volcanic magic — they are people of flesh and will who happen to be threaded through with hardened volcanic stone and the residual ember-heat of the Cinderteeth's most catastrophic eruption. Their skin in places has the texture and coloration of cooled lava, their blood runs hotter than other races, and when they are injured, the exposed tissue sometimes glows faint orange before healing over in a thin layer of mineral crust.

They are a young people by any civilizational measure, existing only since the Ashfall catastrophe remade the lowland communities. They are still figuring out what they are.

---

## Origin

The Ashfall Era's eruption was not a single event but a sustained catastrophic sequence that lasted years. When the Cinderteeth erupted in force — driven by the deep fault-coupling that periodically synchronized mountain seismic events with the oasis-pyramid corridor — the ashfall clouds reached communities hundreds of miles from the range itself.

Most died. Some survived by fleeing. A handful survived by something else entirely: people who were caught in the ashfall without shelter, who breathed the mineral-heavy air and were covered in volcanic particulate for days without dying, emerged changed. Their bodies had metabolized the volcanic material — incorporated the minerals into tissue, the heat into cellular chemistry, the pressure into structural reinforcement — in a process that physicians of the era documented with bafflement and recorded as either divine intervention or poisoning with an unusual outcome.

The first Ashborn were these survivors and their children. Reproduction proved that the changes were heritable: children of changed survivors were born already carrying the mineral threads in their skin, already running hot, already resilient in ways their non-changed neighbors were not.

The Spirebound Conclave's researchers, working from the Cinderteeth sanctum, took note. Some scholars believe the Conclave's **Ember Canon** doctrine — which theorized that fire was the most fundamental transformative force — was partly inspired by observing early Ashborn and attempting to replicate the volcanic transformation process deliberately. If so, the Conclave learned the wrong lesson: the volcanic transformation could not be reproduced through intentional fire-ritual, only through the specific combination of mineral particulate, prolonged exposure, and whatever physiological predisposition allowed some people to incorporate rather than be killed by the ashfall.

## Physical Description

Ashborn are large and dense — heavier than their apparent size suggests because their bones and subcutaneous tissue contain significant mineral deposits. They stand large but carry it differently from other large races: where some large peoples are broad and slow, Ashborn are compact and direct, built for sustained effort rather than agility.

Their most distinctive feature is their skin, which varies across individuals along a spectrum from smooth bronze through rough stone-gray to the fractured black-and-orange pattern that resembles cooling lava crust. The volcanic threading is particularly visible at joints, where mineral deposits concentrate, and along the forearms, where the stone-skin is often thickest. Many Ashborn have patches of their body where the skin does not simply scar but seals over injuries in layers of mineral crust that must be cracked away as the underlying tissue heals.

Their eyes are typically deep amber, orange-red, or occasionally pale white — the heat of their metabolic process concentrates in the iris and gives their gaze an unsettling depth. In dim light, some Ashborn show faint ember-glow in the depressions of their eye sockets, particularly when they are emotionally activated.

Their breath is measurably warmer than other races, and in cold environments, Ashborn exhale visible steam even when others do not. In extreme cold, they can exhale a focused gout of superheated air — the racial **fire breath** that other peoples find simultaneously impressive and alarming.

## Culture and Society

### The Mountain's Children

Ashborn do not worship the Cinderteeth, but they acknowledge it as their origin in the way one might acknowledge a parent who does not know you exist. The mountain made them. The mountain continues to shape them through the trace minerals in their blood and the volcanic memory embedded in their cells. Some Ashborn report a vague directional awareness in the presence of volcanic activity — a sense of warmth or pressure that intensifies in proportion to seismic events, which they call **the mountain's reminder**.

Their relationship to the Ash Cult is complex. Cult theology treats the Cinderteeth as a judicial instrument; Ashborn treat it as simply a physical phenomenon of extraordinary power. They often find Ash Cult apocalypticism excessive but cannot entirely dismiss a religion whose central sacred object made their people. Most Ashborn maintain respectful distance from Ash Cult cells while sharing their reverence for volcanic power.

### The Forge Economy

Ashborn communities built around a practical reality: their bodies run hot, they are comfortable in high-temperature environments that other races find intolerable, and their mineral-reinforced skin makes them naturally suited to metalwork. Ashborn smiths are among the most sought-after in the world, not because they have any magical relationship with fire but because they can work closer to the forge, sustain higher temperatures without injury, and have an intuitive understanding of how materials behave under heat that other craftspeople cannot replicate.

This practical advantage shaped Ashborn economics into something centered on **transformation**: raw materials in, refined products out. The same philosophy applies to conflict. Ashborn approach problems as raw material for solutions. An enemy is not something to be destroyed but something to be transformed — ideally into an ally, failing that into something less dangerous.

### Memory of the Ashfall

Ashborn oral tradition preserves extremely detailed accounts of the Ashfall Era from the perspective of those who survived it — not the grand historical narrative of institutional collapse but the immediate, sensory experience of ash in the lungs, the smell of mineral dust, the moment when the terrible burning became something other than dying.

These accounts function as identity documents: to know your ancestor's Ashfall story is to know where you come from and what you were before the mountain made you more. Ashborn who cannot trace their origin back to the Ashfall are considered **of uncertain inheritance** — not outcasts, but people who lack the grounding that others have.

## Racial Traits

**Stat Profile:** STR 22 | INT 16 | WIS 10 | DEX 14 | CON 22

Ashborn are dominant physical forces with sufficient intelligence for craft, logistics, and tactical thinking, but their wisdom is limited: they tend toward direct action over patient strategy, and their theological reasoning is mostly practical rather than metaphysical.

**Elemental Affinities:**
- **Strong in:** Fire, Earth — Ashborn are literally made of volcanic fire and stone; their magic resonates most powerfully with transformation and the physical world
- **Weak in:** Water, Mental — water is physiologically threatening to their mineral-threaded bodies; their minds are oriented toward physical reality rather than mental combat
- **Resistant to:** Fire, Physical — their stone-threaded skin provides significant fire resistance and physical toughness; weapons that would penetrate normal flesh are partially deflected by mineral deposits
- **Susceptible to:** Water, Air — water disrupts the mineral matrix in their bodies and can cause acute physiological distress; air-based attacks are particularly effective because their large bodies present significant surface area and their metabolism depends on specific atmospheric chemistry

**Racial Skills:** fire breath, enhanced damage, counter

**Fire breath** reflects the superheated exhalation that Ashborn can produce — not magical fire but physiological heat, focused through controlled exhalation into a cone of scorching air.

**Enhanced damage** reflects the sheer weight and mineral-reinforcement of Ashborn physique — their blows carry stone weight as well as muscle force.

**Counter** reflects their extraordinary durability. Ashborn absorb damage that would stagger other races and respond in kind; their mineral-reinforced bodies are difficult to put down quickly.

**Racial Modifiers:** Large, Tough Skin (volcanic mineral threading in subcutaneous tissue)

**Wear Slots:** Standard humanoid slots — Head, Face (not Beak), Hands (not Claws)

## Relationship to the World

### The Cinderteeth

The Cinderteeth is simultaneously the Ashborn's origin and their greatest ongoing hazard. They know, better than any other people, what the mountain is capable of — and they know that the Spirebound Conclave's catastrophic failure did not exhaust the mountain's capacity for large-scale transformation. Ashborn communities near the range maintain the most sophisticated seismic monitoring practice outside of organized scholarly institutions, tracking tremor patterns and ash-venting cycles with the seriousness of people who understand from bone-memory what happens when the mountain decides to change the world.

### Midgaard

Ashborn smiths, engineers, and heavy laborers are found in Midgaard's foundry and construction districts. The city's institutional history includes several Ashborn communities that arrived during the Ash-and-Iron Decades seeking work in rebuilding infrastructure after the Ashfall. Their descendants remain, forming a community that is thoroughly urban while maintaining connection to the volcanic memory that defines Ashborn identity.

The city's Continuity faction and the Reckoning faction disagree about Ashborn status in historical records. Continuity archivists classify them as a sub-category of "ashfall survivors" without separate civic identity; Reckoning scholars argue this classification was created specifically to deny Ashborn communities legal standing in inheritance and property disputes during the post-Ashfall reconstruction period.

### The Withered Depths

Some Ashborn scholars have noticed the structural parallel between the Blightmother's crystal-preservation matrix and the Conclave's Ember Canon methodology. The Conclave's approach to the forest trials mirrored their approach to Ashborn transformation — fire as catalyst, mineral as preservative, pressure as integration mechanism — but where the Ashborn transformation succeeded in producing viable people, the forest trial produced corrupted fungal horror. This comparison is both illuminating and disturbing to those Ashborn who have traced the Conclave's research lineage back to early observations of their own people.

## Narrative Tone

Ashborn NPCs should feel direct and warm — the kind of warmth that comes with physical presence and elemental fire, not social warmth. They are straightforward communicators who find oblique language inefficient and evasive behavior suspicious. When an Ashborn is unhappy, the people around them know it; when they are pleased, that is equally evident.

They respect competence over status, endurance over refinement, and action over deliberation. They are not anti-intellectual — an Ashborn who has spent years in a forge developing intuition for metallurgical chemistry is operating very precise intelligence — but they distrust knowledge that cannot be tested against physical reality.

In conflict, they are methodical and relentless. They do not taunt or posture; they simply press forward until the obstacle is removed. Defeating an Ashborn in combat earns genuine respect rather than resentment: they understand that being overcome is simply part of the cycle.

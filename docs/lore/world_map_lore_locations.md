# World Map: Lore-Precise Location Summation

This document provides a comprehensive geographic placement for every area in ACK!TNG,
derived from canonical lore files, room connections, and intercity documentation. All
locations are described relative to each other and to the five major cities.

---

## Continental Overview

The playable world occupies a single continent structured around five major cities that
form a **co-dependency corridor**: Midgaard (center), Kowloon (far north), Kiess (far
west), Rakuen (south), and Mafdet (far east on the coast). The continent's interior
consists of managed woodland belts, caravan road networks, desert expanses, and frontier
zones. The eastern margin gives way to the sea at Mafdet's harbor. The far west and
northwest contain the Cinderteeth Mountains — a volcanic range that is referenced heavily
in lore but lies at the edge of the accessible world. No ocean routes are described
beyond Mafdet's harbor.

The single most important geographic fact: **Midgaard is the crossroads**. Every major
route originates there. North leads through the Great Northern Forest to Kowloon. West
leads down Roc Road to Kiess. South leads through the Eccentric Woodland to Rakuen. East
leads through the Eastern Desert and Oasis-Pyramid Corridor to Mafdet.

## I. THE FIVE CITIES

### Midgaard (The Central City)

Midgaard occupies the continental crossroads where all primary trade corridors converge.
It is an inland city — not coastal, not frontier — positioned where a pre-civic migration
track was first formalized into a stone road network. The city's north exit opens onto the
Lantern Road approaching the Great Northern Forest. Its south gate leads to the Eccentric
Woodland toward Rakuen. Its west gate connects to Roc Road toward Kiess. Its east gate
opens onto the desert caravan routes toward the Oasis-Pyramid Corridor. Beneath the city,
via the Violet Reliquary Arch, lies the Gloamvault of Kel'Shadra.

Midgaard's city boundaries include the Granite Arcade (northernmost civic spine, the
administrative hub for all northern forest commerce), the Southern Wild Gate (the south
exit to the Eccentric Woodland), and the Bell Watch infrastructure that controls the
Catacombs of Nightfall beneath the southern chapel quarter.

### Kowloon (The Northern City)

Kowloon sits at the **far northern end of the continent**, built at the delta of the
Jhen River. It was raised on the ruins of a pre-Covenant delta settlement called the
Shallow Thousand (also the Field of Lanterns). To reach Kowloon from Midgaard, one must
traverse the entirety of the Great Northern Forest (~400 square miles of canopy) via the
Lantern Road. Kowloon's four gates mark its cardinal connections: the Jade Gate (north,
facing the coast and diplomatic sea routes), the Iron Gate (south, facing the Great
Northern Forest and the Lantern Road toward Midgaard), the Lantern Gate (west, toward
farming territories), and the Harbor Gate (east, facing the sea and delta harbor).

### Kiess (The Western City)

Kiess lies at the **far western end of the continent** along Roc Road, the ancient artery
that runs east-west between Midgaard and the frontier. It was built directly on the ruins
of Evermeet, a prior city that collapsed from inward-looking decay. From Midgaard, Roc
Road runs west through settled farmland and contested route bands before reaching Kiess.
The road is the entire connection — there is no shortcut. Kiess itself features the
Central Prism as its luminous civic keystone, and its eastern approach via Roc Road is
the only significant overland link to Midgaard's markets. North of Kiess lies the Forest
of Confusion, a disorienting frontier woodland that forms Kiess's northern border.

### Rakuen (The Southern City)

Rakuen is located **south of Midgaard**, accessible only by passing through the Eccentric
Woodland. The city sits at the southern terminus of the Eccentric Woodland. Room 4239
(Rakuen's North Dispatch Gate) connects bidirectionally to room 1830 in the Eccentric
Woodland — Rakuen is literally the next place south after the woodland ends. The city is
bounded on its flood-prone side by the Redwater Braid flood channel. Rakuen has no
eastern, western, or northern connections to other cities except through Midgaard via the
Woodland.

### Mafdet (The Eastern Coastal City)

Mafdet lies **on the eastern coast**, where the inland desert finally meets the sea. It
is reached by traveling east from Midgaard through the Eastern Desert, across the
Oasis-Pyramid Corridor, and then through the full length of the Saltglass Reach.
The Saltglass Reach terminates at Mafdet Strand, the beach shelf fronting the city's
harbor. The boundary between the Reach and the city's maritime jurisdiction is marked
by the Shoreward Seal, a basalt slab bearing both inland charter notation and maritime
loading symbols. Mafdet is the only city with direct sea access.

## II. THE NORTHERN CORRIDOR: Midgaard to Kowloon

### The Great Northern Forest

The Great Northern Forest is a **~400-square-mile woodland belt lying directly between
Midgaard (south) and Kowloon (north)**, connected by the Lantern Road — a negotiated
overland route that bends around flood, den, and sacred grove per the Bent Mile Principle
established by quartermaster Ilren Voss. The forest has five geographic sub-regions that
travelers pass through sequentially moving north:

1. **Southern Verge** — mixed broadleaf and pine, heaviest Midgaard influence, fertile
   loam, road wardens maintaining visibility. Connects to Midgaard's Granite Arcade.
2. **Mosswater Bends** — fenland basins with seasonal flooding, black-water channels, and
   Mire-clan reed-platform villages. The old Mire-Speakers' territory.
3. **Ironpine Rise** — elevated ridges with thin soil over granite, enormous black-barked
   ironpines, wolf and mountain cat country. Everkeeper stone planting-rings are found
   here.
4. **Ancestor Deeps** — oldest section, ghost birch and ironpines so thick the canopy
   blocks sunlight, luminescent moss, megalith sites of the Cairn-Keepers, dire wolves and
   cave bears.
5. **Northern Crown** — forest thins approaching Kowloon, oldest individual trees, cold
   mist valleys, Kowloon's Wardens of Iron conduct clearing patrols. The Oath-Crossing
   neutral glade (where both cities' charters were formalized) lies in this zone.

### Thornwood (The Harren Estate)

Thornwood occupies the **southern fringe of the Great Northern Forest**, north of
Midgaard's grave districts. It is technically within the broader northern woodland belt
but is administratively excluded from normal Lantern Road timber and escort contracts due
to necrotic contamination. Warden cartographers classify it with a non-standard hazard
sigil. It lies in the transition belt between Midgaard-linked mortuary routes and
Shadowmere's southern approach corridors. Beneath the estate lies a crystal substrate of
unknown origin that predates human settlement. Its iron gate connects via a grave tunnel
to Shadowmere's southern approach — the tunnel itself is older than either site.

### Shadowmere (The Blighted Crownlands)

Shadowmere lies **north of Thornwood**, in the broader northern woodland belt, occupying
the liminal zone between Midgaard's death administration and the deep frontier. It was
founded as a fortified frontier march-town at the crossroads of military roads, farm
belts, and burial tracts — originally meant to extend Midgaard's law northward. It is
reached from the south by passing through Thornwood and the shared grave tunnel hinge.
Shadowmere's own southern approach connects to Thornwood's iron gate. Moving deeper
north from Shadowmere leads further into frontier territory beyond organized city law.

## III. THE WESTERN CORRIDOR: Midgaard to Kiess

### Roc Road and Its Approaches

Roc Road begins at **Midgaard's West Gate** and runs due west toward Kiess. The road
carries visible strata of different historical eras: gray-purple ash mortar near
Midgaard's gate (indicating the Ash-and-Iron era rebuild), then transitions through
settled farmland bands and contested frontier stretches. Key geographic features along
the road include the eastern approaches (heavy Midgaard engineering, lamp-line
maintenance, registry seals), the central bands (Dustward Verge, River Ledger Crossing,
Banner Hills), and the western terminus at Kiess. The road is the continent's primary
east-west spine for commerce and displaced populations.

### Violet Eclipse Cathedral

The Cathedral of the Violet Eclipse stands **along the western approaches from Midgaard
toward Roc Road**, close enough to the city's outer wards to have been supplied by
magistrate-approved stonewrights, yet far enough outside the central Watch circuits to
host hazardous reliquary work. It functions as a threshold institution between Midgaard
and the western road — a mid-route processing station for relic-bearing caravans and
condemned transport convoys moving along Roc Road's eastern sections (levels 20-35).

### Umbra Heartspire

Umbra Heartspire is a **satellite archive-fortress positioned along the western route
between Midgaard and Kiess**, chartered during Midgaard's Containment Era to hold records
too volatile for open civic repositories. After Evermeet's fall, it expanded to absorb
funerary law, mirror-testimony customs, and inheritance rites from displaced Kiess houses.
Its geographic position between the two cities reflects its dual institutional ancestry:
Midgaard legal archive culture on the eastern side, Kiess/Evermeet funerary and
aristocratic rites on the western side (levels 25-40).

### Kiess (Western Terminus)

See the Five Cities section above.

### Forest of Confusion

The Forest of Confusion lies **directly north of Kiess**, forming the city's northern
frontier. It is the outer margin of the same vast ancient woodland whose heart is the
Verdant Depths. Kiess's Wall Command maintains rope-line corridors and bell-post networks
in the southern verge. Moving north through the forest, the terrain transitions from
manageable frontier patrol zone to active three-way collision between Kiess civic order,
the Verdant ecological intelligence, and Withered Conclave residue. The forest's interior
is the site of Spirebound Conclave field trial ruins, disorienting mist-fields (psychoactive
fog from failed druidic quarantine), and abandoned crystal lattice experiments.

### Verdant Depths

The Verdant Depths occupy the **deep interior of the Forest of Confusion and beyond**,
north and northwest of Kiess. This is the ancient living woodland whose distributed
intelligence (three aspects: growth, decay, predation) predates all human cities. The
Verdant Depths represent the forest's true core — the deepest reaches of the same
root-connected ecosystem whose outer buffer zone is the Forest of Confusion. Human
penetration here is genuinely dangerous; the Heartwood Guardians actively protect the
interior (levels 75-95).

### Withered Depths

The Withered Depths lie **deep within the Forest of Confusion's interior**, in groves
that the Spirebound Conclave's Prism Thesis faction selected for their ancient root
networks and high ambient magical density. The blight here originates from abandoned
crystal lattice experiments — resonance crystals implanted in root junction nodes that
were never retrieved. The corruption seeps outward from these original trial sites into
the broader Forest of Confusion. The Blightmother occupies the Heartrot Grove at the
deepest accessible point (levels 80-100).

## IV. THE SOUTHERN FRONTIER: Midgaard to Rakuen

### Sepulcher Pasture

Sepulcher Pasture lies **south of Midgaard, just past the city's ninth milestone** (the
boundary of the city's original road security zone). It is the first landmark south of
the Southern Wild Gate, occupying what was once called the Ninth Acre — working ground
and burial mounds from older clans. The pasture's processional lanes are paved with the
same ash-mortar used in Midgaard's older buildings. Directly south of the pasture leads
into the Eccentric Woodland (levels 15-30).

### Eccentric Woodland

The Eccentric Woodland fills the **southern frontier immediately below Midgaard's
southern gate**, running south until it terminates at Rakuen's North Dispatch Gate.
Unlike the Great Northern Forest (which is managed through formal joint charters), the
Eccentric Woodland orbits Midgaard without belonging to it. The city claims authority
over traffic using the southern gate but disclaims responsibility for the interior.
The woodland presents inconsistent geography — clearings that shift, paths that close,
isolated halls maintained by groups with their own ritual authority. Rakuen's room 4239
directly connects south from the woodland's room 1830 (levels 18-92).

### Rakuen (Southern Terminus)

See the Five Cities section above.

## V. THE EASTERN CORRIDOR: Midgaard to the Coast

### Eastern Desert

The Eastern Desert begins **directly east of Midgaard's eastern gate**, the first major
terrain feature of the continental east. It was once the astronomical frontier of the
old river kingdoms — managed edge biome between river and dune, before the Iseth Reach
dried. The desert is organized in five zones increasing in danger and historical depth
from west to east:

- **Zone A (Western Entry)**: surviving charter stones, caravan road, toll markers, and
  the skeleton of the Keeper-of-Measure road network. Entry from Midgaard's eastern gate.
- **Zone B (Administrative Ruin)**: cistern archives, collapsed ledger courts, contested
  utilities.
- **Zone C (Militarized Interior)**: convoy fortifications, Reaver Confederate territory,
  storm rooms.
- **Zone D (Shrine Corridor)**: the Twelve Obelisks of Measure Script, Jackal Synod
  ritual territory.
- **Zone E (Necropolis Core)**: the Last Sand Regent's preserved court, the catastrophic
  site of the Ninth Meridian eclipse rite.

The Eastern Desert connects north to the Northern Oasis area via hunt-road approaches,
south to the Great Oasis via Western Gate, and east into the broader Oasis-Pyramid
Corridor (levels 30-100).

### The Arroyo

The Arroyo is **a canyon within the eastern desert corridor**, on the approaches toward
the oasis cluster. It was one branch of the Iseth watershed's seasonal spill system —
a flood channel that once carried water from western uplands toward the Deepwell
Confluence basin. Now dry, it intersects the caravan routes between the Eastern Desert's
road network and the oasis triad. Travelers approaching the oases from the main desert
road may pass through the Arroyo's canyon mouth. Its position makes it a jurisdictional
wound where Keeper measurement, Salt Ledger extraction, pyramid-inflected death
jurisdiction, and Synod coercion all remain active simultaneously (levels 55-75).

### The Scorched Wastes

The Scorched Wastes occupy the zone **between the oasis confluence and the pyramid
approach routes**, functioning as the old Meridian Intake March — the intake and legal
filter corridor that once administered caravan passage between the eastern road network
and the pyramid complexes. The thermal and metaphysical overprint from the Ninth Meridian
catastrophe transformed this managed zone into the lethal dune-scar seen today. It sits
roughly east of the oasis triad and west/southwest of the pyramid complex, on the routes
connecting the two (levels 65-85).

### The Scorching Sands

The Scorching Sands occupy the **east-southeastern quadrant of the Eastern Desert**,
forming the transitional wedge between the interior caravan spine and the coastward
Saltglass corridors. Their approximate position: west/northwest boundary meets the
Eastern Desert's late-stage caravan and obelisk grid; southwest boundary meets Southern
Oasis convoy hardening lanes; east boundary meets the Saltglass Reach's Heavy Road cairn
line; south boundary meets faulted heat pans where Iseth channel sediments were
repeatedly flash-fused. Roughly 2-3 caravan days east-southeast of the Great Oasis and
1 caravan day west of the first true Saltglass mirror-flat bands (levels 65-85).

### The Great Oasis

The Great Oasis sits **southeast of the Eastern Desert's main road spine**, accessible
via the Western Gate from the Eastern Desert. It is positioned at the primary vent of
the Deepwell Confluence aquifer — the largest fracture, the highest volume, the most
reliable water source. The Great Oasis served as the calibration hub of the entire
Oasis-Pyramid Corridor. The Northern Oasis lies north of it; the Southern Oasis lies
south of it. All three share the same underlying Deepwell Confluence aquifer (levels 40-60).

### The Northern Oasis

The Northern Oasis lies **north of the Great Oasis**, at the tertiary fracture of the
Deepwell Confluence — the smallest and most brackish of the three vents. Its institutional
culture of extreme measurement precision is a direct response to its marginal water
supply. Caravans moving north from the Great Oasis reach the Northern Oasis; it serves
as the intake and verification node for the corridor from the north (levels 45-65).

### The Southern Oasis

The Southern Oasis lies **south of the Great Oasis**, at the secondary fracture of the
Deepwell Confluence. It served as the corridor's convoy hardening, expedition marshalling,
and casualty processing node — the last point of civil order before the Southern Pyramid
frontier. The Southern Oasis's springs diminished faster than the Great Oasis's after
the Ninth Meridian Rite, forcing early institutional independence. Approach routes to the
Southern Pyramid originate here (levels 50-70).

### Lost City (Khepra-Lesh)

Khepra-Lesh lies **within the Oasis-Pyramid Corridor between the oasis cluster and the
pyramid complex**, sustained by a subsidiary branch of the Deepwell Confluence. It was
founded as a jurisdictional hinge-city where oasis water-law administration, pyramid
funerary jurisdiction, and Eastern Desert caravan law overlapped. Public squares doubled
as flow adjudication courts; police powers were tied to canal gates and ration tablets.
Caravan routes from both the oases and the pyramid approaches passed through this
settlement, making it simultaneously indispensable in stable eras and catastrophically
fragile in periods of doctrinal conflict (levels 35-55).

### Khar'Daan (The Sunken Necropolis)

Khar'Daan occupies **a mineral fault in the desert corridor between the oasis cluster
and the pyramid routes**, positioned where warm brackish water rises beneath sandstone
shelves. It is accessible from the desert routes that connect the oases to the pyramid
complexes. Built above a subterranean system connected to the same Deepwell Confluence
aquifer that feeds the oases, Khar'Daan was the corridor's preservation and funerary
bureaucracy — the place where bodies, identities, contracts, and dynastic legitimacy
were stored across generations. Its natron and salt deposits made it ideal for
preservation rites. High natron concentration and natural sink chambers were the
geographic conditions that founded the settlement (levels 60-80).

### The Sultan's Palace

The Sultan's Palace stands **within the Oasis-Pyramid Corridor**, positioned on the same
broad civilizational seam between the oasis triad and the pyramid sphere. It functioned
as a secondary adjudication capital for nobles, convoys, and shrine authorities unwilling
to submit disputes in the Great Oasis's public courts — drawing water-right authority
from the Deepwell Confluence traditions and funerary jurisdiction from the pyramid sphere,
especially southern mortuary doctrine. Its location between the Great Oasis and the
southern pyramid approaches made it the corridor's political and legal fulcrum during
its founding era (levels 30-50).

### Akh'enet (The Sands of Akh'enet)

Akh'enet sits **between the Southern Oasis and the Southern Pyramid approach roads**,
within the southern corridor of the Oasis-Pyramid arc. It inherited convoy hardening and
ration authentication bureaucracy from the Southern Oasis to its north; witness-seal
legal ritual and levy accounting from the Great Oasis; and funerary adjudication language
from the pyramids to its south. It was the corridor's legal-mortuary relay, the place
where caravans, bodies, debt, relics, and verdicts all transitioned between oasis water
law and pyramid funerary authority. Its own lore name is the City of the Eclipsed Ledger
(levels 70-90).

### The Northern Pyramid

The Northern Pyramid stands in the **northern arm of the pyramid complex**, in the eastern
desert northeast of the oasis cluster. It was built by the Sand Sovereign, a dynastic heir
of the Salt Ledger Kingdoms who claimed descent from the Solar Court's Dawn Liturgists.
Its seven-floor ascending structure was constructed with oasis-trained engineers from the
Great Oasis Measuring House and requisitioned Northern Oasis water and labor. The Northern
Pyramid was built in part as a backup containment ring for the Black Sun Shard contained
in the Great Pyramid — the Sand Sovereign understood the Great Pyramid's containment was
degrading. The pyramid's astrolabe chambers directly descend from the obelisk-calibration
tradition visible in Zone D of the Eastern Desert (levels 100-120).

### The Southern Pyramid

The Southern Pyramid stands in the **southern arm of the pyramid complex**, in the eastern
desert southeast of the oasis cluster, positioned above a geological fault line connected
to the Iseth Reach's deep aquifer system. It was built by the Moon Sovereign, the Sand
Sovereign's twin sibling and theological rival, who deliberately inverted the Northern
Pyramid's ascending structure — descending through seven tiers consecrated to aspects of
death. The Southern Pyramid represents the underworld-descendant theological tradition in
contrast to the Northern Pyramid's solar-ascendant doctrine. Moon Sovereign texts
smuggled from here founded the Jackal Synod's "Eclipsed Tongues" apocalyptic faction
(levels 120-140).

### The Great Pyramid

The Great Pyramid stands at the **far eastern end of the desert complex**, accessible from
Southern Oasis approach routes and the deeper Eastern Desert interior. It is the most
ancient and most complex of the three pyramid structures — simultaneously a public machine
of kingship, a hidden machine of soul adjudication, and a sealed machine of cosmic
containment for the Black Sun Shard. Built by the Solar Court of the Undivided Disk,
its Ledger Priests adopted Keeper Measure Script and expanded it into the sacred language
of architectural inscription. The Great Pyramid's administrative network created the
Water Court system that all three oases inherited (levels 90-100).

### The Saltglass Reach

The Saltglass Reach begins at the **eastern margins of the oasis-pyramid caravan basin**
and extends east to the coast. It is the final overland corridor before reaching Mafdet's
harbor, running through four progressively challenging bands from west to east:

1. **Mirror Flats**: Hard salt crust (geological remnant of the Iseth Reach's terminal
   evaporation basin). Blindingly reflective at midday; caravans cross at dawn and dusk.
2. **Glasswind Belts**: Wind-cut silica shards formed during the Ninth Meridian
   catastrophe's thermal pulse. Color of glass indicates ground stability: milky =
   stable, amber = thermal fault proximity, violet = eclipse-rite residue.
3. **Tariff Cairn Lines**: Three parallel chains of ruined checkpoint cairns running
   east-west — the northern Swift Route (light cargo), central Heavy Road (bulk
   commodities), southern Sealed Route (relic and funerary consignments).
4. **Tidemouth Dunes**: Low dunes and brackish channels marking approach to the coast,
   culminating at the Shoreward Seal stone that marks the legal transition to Mafdet's
   maritime jurisdiction.

Mafdet's harbor sits at the Saltglass Reach's eastern terminus (levels 55-80).

## VI. THE KEL'SHADRA INSTITUTIONAL COMPLEX

The Kel'Shadra Order's physical infrastructure is distributed across three connected but
distinct sites, organized in a hierarchical chain from public-facing to deep-coercive:

### Gloamvault of Kel'Shadra

The Gloamvault lies **beneath Midgaard**, accessible via the Violet Reliquary Arch in
the city's civic core. It is the public-doctrinal layer of the Kel'Shadra machine:
confession intake, oath classification, and visible liturgical authority. Originally the
Vault of Last Witness, built by early Kel'Shadra officiants as a subterranean chapel
archive, it became an engine of social control as its records grew legally and
economically authoritative. The Violet Compact era formalized its connection to
Midgaard's civic governance apparatus (levels 5-20).

### Catacombs of Nightfall

The Catacombs of Nightfall lie **beneath Midgaard's southern chapel quarter**, accessible
from the city's civic core through the Ossuary Transport Court. They are physically
adjacent to the Violet Reliquary Arch (Gloamvault access) and the Dungeon Intake Yard
(condemned persons processing). This geographic clustering within Midgaard's subterranean
layer is not accidental — during the Violet Compact era, all three complexes were managed
through a shared administrative apparatus. Nightfall was a burial-adjudication complex
that grew into an extractive machine for grief, memory, and the living dead (levels 10-25).

### Crypts of Kel'Shadra

The Crypts occupy the **deep enforcement stratum beneath the Kel'Shadra network**, the
terminal filtration layer between the Gloamvault's public-facing doctrine and the Void
Citadel's recurrence machinery. Architecturally and institutionally below the Gloamvault,
the Crypts served as Kel'Shadra's disappearance logistics center — where contested
memories were not merely classified but permanently sealed, partitioned, or embodied into
bound spirit-hosts. The three deep offices (Chamber of Redactions, Warden Sepulcher
Compact, Inverse Chancel) made the Crypts self-sustaining even after the upper order
collapsed (levels 150+).

### Void Citadel of Kel'Shadra

The Void Citadel is the **recurrence-enforcement apex** of the Kel'Shadra machine — the
high-command complex where testimony was iterated until it produced politically acceptable
outcomes. It sits at the top of the institutional hierarchy but occupies a physically
distinct site from the Gloamvault and Crypts, likely in a more remote or fortified
location accessed via the same underground network. The Citadel is built around a
vertical paradox (spire above, catacomb below) and served three functions: testimony
extraction, authorized projection, and recurrence enforcement. Its internal inscriptions
read as operational, cold, and administrative rather than liturgical: *resubmit*,
*stabilize*, *converge*, *close variance* (levels 150+).

## VII. FRONTIER AND SUPPLEMENTARY AREAS

### Withered Depths (see Section III above)
Position: deep interior of the Forest of Confusion, northwest of Kiess.

### The Wizard's Keep

The Wizard's Keep is a **high-mountain tutorial and adept training area** at the edge of
the accessible world, in a remote forest and mountain setting. Mechanically it is
level-150 access but narratively positioned as a gateway or warding zone rather than a
primary content site. Its geographic placement implies high elevation and relative
isolation from the main continental routes (level 150).

### Academy of Adventure and Whispering Forest Preserve

Both are **starter training areas near Midgaard**, intended as controlled introductory
environments for new players. The Academy of Adventure (Newschool) represents organized
institutional training in a city-adjacent setting; the Whispering Forest Preserve is
a safe, managed forest environment within reach of Midgaard's influence (levels 1-10).

### Public Dungeons

The Public Dungeons are accessible from **within Midgaard or its immediate surroundings**,
serving as a low-to-mid-level dungeon complex available to new adventurers without
requiring travel to outlying areas (levels 5-35).

## VIII. SUNKEN SANCTUM AND THE CINDERTEETH MOUNTAINS

### Sunken Sanctum

The Sunken Sanctum occupies **a deep fold in the western spur of the Cinderteeth
Mountains** — the volcanic range in the far west or northwest that lies at the edge of
the accessible continental map. The Spirebound Conclave chose this site deliberately:
geothermal activity from the Cinderteeth provided natural heat for forges and alchemical
furnaces, volcanic glass from the peaks fed the crystal conduit system, and the range's
remoteness insulated the Conclave from lowland politics.

The Cinderteeth range itself is the geological origin point for several catastrophes that
shaped the rest of the continent: the eruptions that caused the Ash Winters devastating
the Great Northern Forest, the Ashfall Monsoon that struck Kowloon's delta, and (via
connected seismic cycles) contributed to the drought that intensified the Eastern
Desert's collapse. The Sunken Sanctum's catastrophic internal failure — when a mountain
tremor ruptured containment circles at the same moment an internal faction sabotaged the
core rite-matrix — was an early precursor of the same volcanic cycle (levels 150).

## X. GEOGRAPHIC PRECISION NOTES

**The Eccentric Woodland is due south of Midgaard**, not north. Room 1830 (southern
end of the woodland) connects south to room 4239 (Rakuen's North Dispatch Gate). This
makes Rakuen the southernmost city in the main continental network.

**The Great Northern Forest lies due north of Midgaard and due south of Kowloon**. The
Lantern Road through the forest is the only overland connection between these two cities.
No eastern or western bypass exists.

**Thornwood is north of Midgaard but south of the main Great Northern Forest travel
band** — it occupies the southern fringe exclusion zone. Shadowmere is north of
Thornwood. Neither is accessible via the Lantern Road's normal route.

**The three oases are a cluster**, not a line: the Great Oasis at the center (primary
aquifer vent), the Northern Oasis to its north (tertiary vent), and the Southern Oasis
to its south (secondary vent). All three tap the same Deepwell Confluence aquifer.

**The three pyramids mirror the oasis cluster's axis**: the Northern Pyramid in the
northeastern desert, the Southern Pyramid in the southeastern desert, and the Great
Pyramid at the far eastern point of the desert before the Saltglass Reach begins.

**Kiess is west, Mafdet is east**: the continental axis runs east-west through Midgaard.
The north-south axis runs through Midgaard between Kowloon (north) and Rakuen (south).
Midgaard is genuinely at the four-way crossroads.

**The Cinderteeth Mountains are in the northwest**, beyond Kiess. The Sunken Sanctum is
in their western spur. They are never directly traversable but their volcanic and seismic
activity has influenced nearly every other geographic zone on the continent.

**The Saltglass Reach runs east** from the oasis-pyramid basin to the coast. It does not
run north-south. Mafdet is east of the pyramids, not southeast or northeast.

**The Forest of Confusion is north of Kiess**, not between Kiess and Midgaard. The
Verdant Depths and Withered Depths are both deeper north/interior of the Forest of
Confusion, not separate geographic regions west of the forest.

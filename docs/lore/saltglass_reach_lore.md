# Lore: Saltglass Reach

## Purpose

This document defines the **Saltglass Reach**, the eastern continuation of the Oasis-Pyramid desert arc from the inland caravan basin to the sea-facing littoral.

City-specific governance, port economics, and harbor faction material are documented separately in `docs/lore/mafdet_lore.md`.

---

## I) Standard Naming Procedure

To remain consistent with established world naming patterns, this region follows a four-part naming standard for major places:

1. **Geographic base term** (what the place physically is).
2. **Material or phenomenon marker** (what defines it ecologically/historically).
3. **Civic-historical layer** (who used/administered it in prior eras).
4. **Current usage name** (what caravans and clerks call it now).

### Desert Name Application
- Geographic base term: eastern littoral desert belt.
- Material marker: salt pans and fused glass dunes.
- Civic-historical layer: former tariff and seal-inspection marches of the late Ledger dynasties.
- Current usage name: **Saltglass Reach**.

"Saltglass" captures the region's defining terrain (salt crust + heat-fused silica). "Reach" marks it as a traversed frontier corridor rather than an isolated dune sea.

---

## II) Geography and Route Logic

The **Saltglass Reach** begins at the eastern margins of the oasis-pyramid caravan basin and extends to the eastern sea. It is narrower than the deeper interior deserts but more lethal in specific bands:

- **Mirror Flats**: hard salt crust that blinds by day and reflects moonlight at night.
- **Glasswind Belts**: fields of wind-cut silica shards that abrade skin, cloth, and pack harness.
- **Tariff Cairn Lines**: ruined checkpoint chains once used to certify cargo transitioning from inland caravan law to maritime levy law.
- **Tidemouth Dunes**: low dunes and brackish channels that mark approach to the coast.

At the coast, the Reach opens into **Mafdet Strand**, the beach shelf that fronts the port-city of Mafdet.

---

## III) Historical Arc

### 1) Survey Era (late Keepers of Measure)
The eastern marches were first charted as extension lines from interior obelisk systems. The Keepers treated the sea as an unstable boundary condition: measurable in tide cycles, difficult to tax with static markers. Early tide gauges and salt-depth rods from this era survive in fragments near old cairn stations.

### 2) Ledger-Tariff Era (Salt Ledger successor courts)
As dynastic authority shifted from road calibration to extraction, rulers built tariff posts across the Reach to capture value from coast-bound caravans. Cargo paid inland water-right dues at oasis courts, then paid **shore conversion levies** before receiving legal loading rights at the beach.

### 3) Fracture Era (post-Ninth Meridian collapse)
After hydraulic disruption and dynastic fragmentation, inland routes became dangerous and authority splintered. The Reach remained active as a contested transition lane between surviving caravan authorities and emerging coastal trade blocs.

### 4) Current Era
The Reach is now the legal-geographic handoff zone where caravan manifests are validated for coastal conversion and maritime dispatch. This keeps the old charter world relevant even as sea commerce outgrows inland courts.

## IV) Cross-Relevance with Existing Lore

### Eastern Desert
The Eastern Desert and Saltglass Reach are contiguous but culturally distinct: the former is ruin-heavy inland collapse terrain; the latter is a littoral tariff frontier shaped by sea logistics.

### Oases and Pyramids
The oases remain the adjudication source for water-right and caravan-origin claims moving east through the Reach. Pyramid-sourced relic streams depend on Reach checkpoint chains for provenance continuity before coastal transfer.

### Midgaard (west)
Midgaard archival and authentication culture influences trade standards applied to Reach cargo records. Midgaard and coastal authorities both contest who defines "valid" provenance for high-value antiquities.

### Mafdet (east)
Mafdet is the principal coastal endpoint of the Reach. Port institutions, governance blocs, maritime economy, and city-level faction detail are documented in `docs/lore/mafdet_lore.md`.

## V) Story Hooks for Area and Quest Integration

- A Red Sand broker seeks escorts to recover shattered tariff cairn tablets proving historical levy exemptions.
- A caravan learns one Reach checkpoint still uses a pre-collapse tide-calibration code that can invalidate modern manifests.
- Synod couriers attempt to smuggle ritual materials through low-visibility Glasswind routes that bypass formal cairn inspection.
- A surveyor claims one cairn line still aligns to old obelisk timing formulas, allowing prediction of dangerous saltstorm windows.

## VI) Visual and Material Motifs (for room/object consistency)

- Salt-whitened stone, wind-etched glass crust, and rope-burned cargo sled runners.
- Broken toll stelae, cairn rings, and fused-silica marker shards.
- Sand-scoured legal tablets with tide notations and tariff cut-marks.
- Transitional iconography: dunes and tide lines; obelisk ticks and ship draft marks.

These motifs keep the Reach visibly tied to oasis-pyramid legal civilization while emphasizing its role as a frontier of conversion from inland to maritime systems.

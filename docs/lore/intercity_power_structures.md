# Intercity Power Structures and Shared Governance

## Overview

The five cities — Midgaard, Kowloon, Kiess, Mafdet, and Rakuen — are not an alliance but a co-dependency. None fully chose it; none can easily exit. They cooperate on fraud suppression and disaster relief when it serves them. They compete on trade fees, narrative authority, and control of shared institutional crime records. Together they form a legitimacy corridor: origin claim (Midgaard) to overland custody (Kiess/Kowloon) to dispute adjudication (Kiess) to hazard screening (Mafdet) to ocean transfer (Mafdet). Remove any one city and the others accumulate strain.

## Multi-City Institutions

### Five-City Corridor Minutes
Quarterly clerk exchange for fraud typology tracking. The most routine form of intercity cooperation.

### Common Misery Clause
Temporary tariff and fee controls during declared mass-casualty seasons. Activated six times in recorded history; Rakuen has been the beneficiary in four of those activations.

### Reciprocal Witness Passage
Protected travel status for certified witnesses and archive couriers between all five cities.

### Joint Blacklist of Forged Seals
Rotating secretariat — currently held by Kiess, considered the most procedurally trustworthy custodian by all five cities.

### Transit Memorial Compact
Minimum standards for naming, recording, and repatriating intercity dead.

### Contradiction Ledger Initiative
Inspired by Kel'Shadra's memory-custody tradition. Records conflicts between official narratives. Rotating secretariat.

### Five-Watch Concordance
Conversion table for the incompatible local timekeeping systems used by the five cities.

## Bilateral Agreements

### Southern Grain Compact (Kowloon — Rakuen)
Reserve grain allocation maintained by Kowloon's Jade Magistracy for southern corridor relief. Reserve-release dispatched within two weeks of formal emergency declaration. Activated four times in sixty years.

### Mercy Freight Classification (Rakuen — Mafdet)
Reduced harbor fees during declared disaster periods. Bloom Council certifications are countersigned by Ember Wardens; Mafdet Ledger Houses validate maritime transfer.

### Roc Road Dual Jurisdiction (Midgaard — Kiess)
Midgaard road wardens hold formal charter authority; Kiess convoy marshals control practical throughput. Independent wayfolk run ferries and repairs at handoff points neither city covers. Every checkpoint is a friction point. Which seal is authoritative in disputes is contested.

## The Spirebound Conclave Legacy

The continent's shared institutional shame. The Conclave operated a condemned-debtor labor apparatus under sealed warrants from every major city. Used Midgaard's arcane registry to reclassify condemned persons as "arcane material." Every city participated; none acknowledges it publicly.

### City-Specific Evidence

**Midgaard — The Unindexed Years**: Containment Era ledgers are missing. Official explanation: fire and wartime loss. The Reckoning faction believes otherwise. The sealed Breach Tower at the Lantern Registry is believed to hold duplicates. The Southern Receiving office received annual cross-reference documents from Rakuen's Garden Ledger Clerks.

**Kowloon — The Black Ledger**: Unredacted founding census with Conclave transfer names. Rumored to be in the undercity cistern. Every faction would kill to find it. The Reckoning faction wants it unsealed; the Continuity faction wants it buried.

**Kiess — The Obsidian Disc**: Conclave triune seal found in foundation excavations. Held by the Syndic Council. Confirms Evermeet's role in condemned-labor transfers. The founding charter's repudiation of sealed warrants was written in direct response.

**Rakuen — The Garden Ledger Shadow Archive**: Documentary evidence of Midgaard's "resettled south" testimony suppression during the Violet Compact era. Head Clerk Senne Arrath maintains the archive. Multiple parties want to acquire or destroy it.

**Mafdet**: The Shrine of the First Claw's trust-enforcement tradition operates on principles opposed to the Conclave's coercive model. Mafdet's involvement is less documented but the five-city corridor structure implies participation.

## The Rakuen Annexation Arc

The defining intercity political question. Three petitions submitted, three refused. A fourth is possible.

### Southmark Petition
Rakuen petitioned for full civic annexation into Midgaard's district system. Refused on grounds of ledger non-compliance.

### Ash Plea
Emergency humanitarian appeal with death counts and casualty records. Refused on grounds of route instability.

### Iron Compact Appeal
Nine years in drafting, submitted with full Kiess legal certification and Mafdet Ledger House co-signing. Refused on constitutional objections. Most recent refusal: eleven years ago.

### The Fourth Petition Question
If Kiess certifies a fourth petition, any Midgaard refusal becomes a confrontation with Kiess — transforming a bilateral dispute into an intercity crisis. Rakuen's Amendment Track faction is working toward this. Kiess has offered legal support through a standing charter-consultation offer.

### Midgaard's Factional Interest
The Reckoning faction has an institutional interest in Rakuen remaining independent — because an independent Rakuen controls an independent archive (the Garden Ledger shadow archive). The Continuity faction wants the status quo. Midgaard sends subsidies but not recognition.

## Intercity Power Dynamics by City

### Midgaard
The inland legitimacy engine. Holds charter authority over the Roc Road. Processes (and refuses) annexation petitions. Sends subsidies to Rakuen but not recognition. Registry Bureau officials follow the rules correctly when refusing petitions — which is itself the problem. The Charter Sovereignty office is aware of Kiess's standing offer to Rakuen.

### Kowloon
The northern throughput and labor-delta coordination node. The most institutionally stable governance among the five. The Jade Magistracy maintains reserve grain for the Southern Grain Compact. Sent an engineering delegation to Rakuen; studies Rakuen's flood architecture without sharing findings. Ward offices claim practical primacy from route enforcement over Midgaard's "derived law" framing. Kowloon's logistics syndicates are secondary buyers for distressed Rakuen claims.

### Kiess
The legal-memory city. Anti-sealed-warrant charter doctrine gives it unique moral authority. Currently holds the Joint Blacklist of Forged Seals secretariat. Holds a standing charter-consultation offer to Rakuen. Has offered legal support to the Amendment Track. If Kiess certifies a fourth petition, it escalates from bilateral to intercity. Temple delegates observe Ember Warden labor-draft procedures in Rakuen for charter-compliance review.

### Mafdet
The maritime legalization and export conversion authority. Ledger Houses are interested in Rakuen's Quiet Separatist maritime proposal. The Sand-Sea Carters' Guild profits from controlling access and would apply pressure through the Strandline Compact's "harbor first" mechanism if Rakuen tried to bypass established routes. Mafdet seals instruments that don't require Kiess validation for Mafdet court enforceability — an alternative legal path.

### Rakuen
The southern disaster-recovery municipality and route-resilience broker. Its three factions (Amendment Track, Quiet Separatists, Preservation Coalition) orient entirely around the relationship with the other four cities. Garden Ledger Clerks send annual Outbound Open Register cross-reference documents to Midgaard's Southern Receiving office. Canopy Workshop compact-leaders are quietly exploring direct Mafdet trade routes bypassing Midgaard. The Honest Ledger movement makes arguments at every Founder's Reckoning festival about Midgaard's gate-authority and casualty accountability.

## Cross-City Faction Alignments

### Continuity vs. Reckoning (Midgaard and Kowloon)
Both cities have Continuity factions (preserve sealed records) and Reckoning factions (expose Conclave history). The factions share goals across cities but coordinate loosely at best.

### Anti-Sealed-Warrant Doctrine (Kiess, Rakuen tensions)
Kiess's charter repudiation of sealed warrants creates tension with Rakuen's emergency labor drafts, which resemble sealed-warrant governance. Temple delegates from Kiess observe Ember Warden procedures for compliance review — a standing intercity friction point.

### Trade Route Competition
Midgaard and Kiess contest Roc Road authority. Kowloon and Mafdet contest maritime pricing. Rakuen's Quiet Separatists seek to build trade routes that bypass Midgaard entirely, which would shift the balance of the entire corridor.

### The Ash Cult Across Cities
The Ash Cult operates cells in Kowloon (sabotaging floodworks, ideologically anti-Covenant) and Rakuen (rooted in the city's experience of being exported social risk by Midgaard). The cult's Witness Doctrine cells in Rakuen are tolerated as politically useful critics.

## Trade Routes as Power

All routes are dangerous. Power flows along them:

- **Roc Road** (Midgaard — Kiess): Dual jurisdiction; disruptions ripple between cities within days.
- **Lantern Road / Great Northern Forest** (Midgaard — Kowloon): Northern grain and timber artery. Ashfang warband disruptions raise Midgaard prices within days.
- **Eccentric Woodland** (Midgaard — Rakuen): Path-guide services are Rakuen's primary service export. No formal road warden presence; security managed by path-guides under Ember Warden contract.
- **Saltglass Reach** (Mafdet — inland): Sand-pirate raids frequent. Sand-Sea Carters' Guild patrols but cannot cover the full route.
- **Jhen River delta** (Kowloon): River corsairs under Blacktide Shen disrupt northern supply runs.

## Shared Institutional Vulnerabilities

- **Cinderteeth monitoring**: Kowloon and Midgaard engineers tracking venting rates. Temple seismological watches at high alert. No public announcement yet.
- **Withered Depths blight**: Orphaned Conclave crystals still propagating in root systems near Kiess. Scouts track it; reports forwarded to Midgaard.
- **Route intelligence as commodity**: Fresh reports of road conditions and bandit activity are sold at inn common rooms and post offices in every city.
- **The five-city corridor is load-bearing**: Remove any one city's function and the others cannot compensate. This is why Rakuen's potential independence — or collapse — matters to everyone.

# Mafdet (Port Mafdet) — Power Structures and Governance

## Overview

Port Mafdet is a fortified beach city at the eastern terminus of the Saltglass Reach, where the inland desert corridor meets the sea. Named for the guardian Mafdet — a feline divine executioner of thieves, oath-breakers, and those who prey on the defenseless. This is a transaction city. Legitimacy equals reliable docking, fair weights, and enforceable manifests. Every institution exists to make commerce trustworthy or to punish those who undermine trust.

---

## The Strandline Compact

Mafdet's constitutional framework distributes authority across three blocs, with a veto mechanism: any two can veto the third. Three core principles:

- **Harbor first** — No political decision may obstruct port operations.
- **Dual attestation** — All cargo verified under both inland and maritime legal standards.
- **Swift judgment** — Commercial disputes resolved within three tides.

---

## The Three Blocs

### Harbor Wardens
Quay security, anti-piracy watch, and the harbor chain. The **harbor chain** is raised every night without exception — it has never failed since the Fracture Era. Ships arriving after dark anchor outside and wait for dawn. Anyone crossing after the chain is up faces criminal charges handled by Harbor Wardens, not civil law. The **Warden-Commander's Harbor Register** holds over 100 years of vessel, cargo, crew, and weather records. Harbor Warden authority extends to the shore but not inland.

Harbor Wardens maintain a visible presence at the commercial docks to prevent friction from becoming dangerous. Two weight inspectors maintain a continuous presence. A checkpoint at the street narrowing verifies that all cargo moving between pier and street is logged.

### Ledger Houses
The bureaucratic backbone of Mafdet's trade system. Four specialized institutions:

**Tide Ledger Hall** — The largest and most authoritative Ledger House. Official custodian of the **Saltweight standard**, certifying authority for all harbor-measure conversions, and first point of arbitration for cargo disputes. Archive clerks move between the gallery and main floor retrieving historical manifests on request. The Tide Ledger Hall's authority extends across all four Ledger Houses by virtue of holding the primary Saltweight standard. Conversion-ratio charts in the market are updated weekly by a Tide Ledger Hall clerk and treated as authoritative for all commercial disputes.

**Shoreward House** — Manages the legal translation between inland Reach charter law and Mafdet maritime contract law — the Shoreward Conversion in its fullest bureaucratic form. Clerks are among the most technically educated in the Ledger Quarter, required to be fluent in both legal systems. Essential for first-time inland traders.

**Storm Ledger** — Insurance, escrow, and storm advisories based on accumulated observational records. Captains ignoring Storm Ledger advisories lose bonded cargo insurance — this is noted in the Harbor Register and follows a ship's record permanently. The Storm Ledger's operational home is in the harbor district, where high-value weather-risk-bonded cargo is held.

**Quarantine Ledger** — Handles relic and hazardous goods from the pyramid corridor. The smallest and most sensitive Ledger House. **Hazard Scribes** decide which pyramid-origin items can be legally exported — decisions are final and not subject to Shrine appeal. Recent seizures are under sealed review.

### Sand-Sea Carters' Guild
Controls all licensed cargo movement between the Saltglass Reach and the Mafdet harbor. The Guild Hall is the operational command center — a wide, timber-floored room with a vaulted ceiling, map boards on the north wall, and dispatch desks where Guild masters manage waystation reports, caravan manifests, and route condition updates. Contracts are non-negotiable on route and timing. Without the Guild, the harbor has nothing to load.

The **Quartermaster's Depot** maintains expedition and patrol supplies for outbound caravans and Reach Warden posts. The Quartermaster works from a standing desk covered in requisition records.

---

## The Shrine of the First Claw

Not a conventional temple but a civic-judicial facility. Trade oaths are sealed on a basalt feline forepaw worn smooth by use, recorded on wax-and-bitumen tablets. Breaking a First Claw oath means permanent exclusion from the entire Mafdet trade network — not just the immediate counterparty. The **Claw Mark Registry** tracks all oaths and penalties across years.

The **Swift Court** operates on the three-tide judgment principle: disputes are heard and decided within three tides. Decisions are final with no appeal except to the full Shrine council for inter-city treaty matters. Harbor Warden reports are filed same-tide.

Guardian Mafdet is not a deity of comfort but of enforcement: the Shrine doesn't bless commerce, it punishes betrayal.

**Strand Folk principle**: The beach is the legal boundary between inland and sea law, reconciled under the guardian's witness. This philosophical foundation became the Shoreward Conversion that the Ledger Houses later formalized.

---

## Reach Wardens

Control access along the Saltglass Reach approaches. Some posts have collapsed and been abandoned for years — the structure at one station caved in several years ago, with roof timbers and Saltglass tablet frames still visible from the crest.

---

## The Shoreward Seal

A basalt column at the center of the approach corridor, twice the height of a person and polished smooth on its eastern face. Inscribed on four sides in two legal registers: the Survey-Administrative notation of the Saltglass Reach authority on the western face, and the Harbor-Operational Measure Script of Mafdet's Shoreward Compact on the eastern face. This marks the legal transition from inland to maritime jurisdiction.

---

## Gate and Customs Infrastructure

### Landward Gate
Gate inspection clerks move between wagon teams with folding writing boards, checking transit seals against a master register and marking cargo manifests for Shoreward Conversion. The gate is a fortified arch in the basalt wall with iron-reinforced doors standing open during the day and its inspection lantern lit at all hours.

### Gate Checkpoint
The inner side of the gate contains a customs checkpoint where cargo manifests are formally stamped and transit seals collected. A long table under the gate arch's inner vault holds manifest registration ledgers and a set of Saltweight reference measures.

### Customs Yard
A broad enclosed space just inside the gate where cleared caravans reorganize before entering the city proper.

---

## Political Factions

### Quay Concord
Senior captains and Harbor Wardens. Maritime reliability above inland politics. They run the city in practical terms.

### Red Sand Accounts
Buy distressed inland debts cheap, convert them to maritime obligations, and enforce through Mafdet's courts. Technically legal under the Strandline Compact's due-process provisions. Widely despised. Operate **Red Sand Outriders** through the Saltglass Reach, specializing in identifying financially distressed operations. A secondary buyer for distressed Rakuen claims.

### Jackal Synod
Claims universal funerary and oath-jurisdiction across the eastern corridor. The Shrine of the First Claw rejects this claim. The Synod has never had enough Mafdet presence to challenge directly. Smuggles ritual components from pyramid excavation sites under false manifests, exploiting Quarantine Ledger complexity. Jackal Synod traders use salt-oath formulas instead of First Claw oaths — the Shrine tolerates this as personal choice, but these oaths carry no weight in Mafdet commercial courts.

### Strand Rememberers
Hold pre-Fracture cartographic and navigational knowledge. Beginning to sell to Kowloon and Kiess. Increasingly politically significant as cities seek pre-Keeper survey data.

---

## The Littoral Branch

Historical authority that connected early Mafdet settlement with the Reach Wardens. Referenced as the institutional ancestor of the current Shoreward Conversion system.

---

## Saltglass Reach Routes

Three parallel routes of ascending security and cost:
- **Swift Line** — Light cargo, strict documentation, minimal assay.
- **Heavy Road** — Bulk goods, triple-assessed at cairns, slow but legally airtight.
- **Sealed Route** — Relic/funerary cargo, armed escort required, most expensive and legally secure.

## Area File NPCs

### The Innkeeper of the Saltwind Inn
Maintains a strict no-weapons-drawn policy enforced not by guards but by personal reputation for remembering faces and banning troublemakers by name across the entire Shoreward Compact.

---

## Market of the Shoreward Tables

Mafdet's primary commercial bazaar. Named for the mounted stone charts along the perimeter walls displaying current Saltweight-to-harbor-measure conversion ratios and commodity price standards.

---

## Dual Attestation

All cargo entering or leaving Mafdet requires verification under both inland and maritime legal standards. This adds cost but prevents legal liability at destination. Shoreward House provides bilingual clerks for the conversion — essential for first-time inland traders navigating oasis/maritime contract notation differences.

# Lore: The Umbral

## Core Identity

The **Umbral** are shadow given permanent form — humanoids whose substance is as much darkness as flesh, whose origin lies in the world's deepest rupture between light and anti-light. They are not demons, not undead, not creatures of evil. They are the living residue of the **Black Sun Shard's** most intense influence: people who dwelt too long in the shadow-charged spaces where the Shard's anti-light leaks into the world, and whose bodies, over generations, incorporated that anti-light into their cellular foundation.

The Gloamvault — the ancient oath-archive beneath Midgaard's foundations — is their most significant habitation site, but Umbral communities have been documented wherever the Black Sun Shard's influence concentrates: in deep cave networks, in underground archives, in spaces where shadow seems to accumulate beyond what physical geometry can explain.

They are a people of absence: defined by what they are not as much as what they are. They are not fully solid. They are not fully visible. They are not fully present in the world the way other races are. This is not weakness but a different relationship to existence — one that grants capabilities inaccessible to fully material beings.

## Origin

The **Black Sun Shard** fell in the Primordial Age — a fragment of anti-light that consumed names, memories, and certainties. The first civilizations learned to manage its influence through containment (the Great Pyramid's cosmic suppression systems) and avoidance (the oasis corridor's careful route-mapping around zones of intense Shard influence). What they could not fully account for was the effect of long-term habitation in Shard-adjacent spaces.

The Gloamvault's founding predates Midgaard's First Stone Accord. Before the city existed above it, the Vault was already old — a shadow-charged archive space where oath-records had been kept for generations by an unknown predecessor civilization. The Kel'Shadra Order, when they established formal operations in the Vault, were not founding something new but claiming an existing site already shaped by centuries of Shard influence.

The communities that lived in and around the Vault — maintaining it, cataloguing it, guarding it — gradually changed. Not dramatically or visibly, but over generations: their skin lost warmth, their eyes developed the capacity to see in near-total darkness, their bodies became partially permeable to mundane light in a way that made them difficult to see clearly in shadow. By the time the change was identifiable as heritable, the Umbral had existed as a distinct people for several generations. They had simply never named themselves.

The name **Umbral** was given to them by Midgaard scholars who documented the Vault's communities. The Umbral themselves use the name now without particular attachment to its origin — it is accurate enough.

## Physical Description

Umbral are difficult to perceive clearly except in direct light, and even then something about their visual presentation seems inconsistent — the eye slides off them slightly, as though they are not quite fully where they appear to be. This is not an active ability but a passive characteristic of their partially anti-light-constituted bodies; mundane vision processes them with slightly less precision than other races.

In daylight, they appear as lean, pale-gray humanoids with very dark eyes (the pupil and iris are sometimes indistinguishable) and hair that ranges from deep charcoal to void-black. Their skin has a faintly iridescent quality in direct light — not glistening, but shifting, as though the surface is simultaneously present and absent. In low light, their outlines soften. In shadow, they are extraordinarily difficult to see.

Their physical form is somewhat less substantial than other races — they are lighter than their apparent size, and physical impacts that would produce specific tactile feedback from a human sometimes produce a slight sensation of pressing into something that gives unexpectedly. This is unsettling to other races and is the primary source of the social discomfort that Umbral often face in unfamiliar communities.

They heal slowly in direct sunlight and recover much better in darkness or shadow — their anti-light constitution repairs itself more efficiently in conditions that match its nature.

## Culture and Society

### The Principle of Necessary Absence

Umbral philosophy is organized around a concept they call **necessary absence** — the idea that what is not present shapes reality as powerfully as what is. A shadow is defined by the light around it. An unspoken word shapes a conversation as much as spoken ones. The Black Sun Shard does not simply consume light; it creates a different kind of presence.

This philosophy produces a people highly attuned to gaps, silences, and the implications of what is withheld. Umbral negotiators and scholars are particularly effective at identifying what is not being said in a conversation, what document is absent from an archive, what person is conspicuously absent from a meeting. Their mental acuity expresses itself through pattern-recognition that emphasizes the shape of missing pieces.

### The Vault Tradition

The Gloamvault's oath-archive function resonates deeply with Umbral culture. They maintain the tradition that the Vault began — the idea that promises, agreements, and commitments have substance independent of the people who make them, that records of oaths persist as real things in the world long after the oathtakers are gone.

Umbral communities maintain **shadow-archives** — physical records kept in spaces with minimal light, where Umbral archivists catalog everything from commercial contracts to personal testimony to observed events. The archives are not secret but are inaccessible to non-Umbral visitors without guide assistance, since navigating them requires comfort with near-total darkness and the ability to read archive notation by the faint phosphorescent ink that Umbral scribes use.

### The Standing-Apart

Umbral communities generally exist in deliberate proximity to other civilizations without full integration. They occupy adjacent spaces — the deep basements of cities, the shaded valleys adjacent to inhabited areas — maintaining economic and social relationships with surface communities while preserving their own distinct culture.

This standing-apart is not hostility but ecological logic: Umbral physiology is genuinely more comfortable in shadow-rich environments, and their sensory world is calibrated to different stimuli than daylight-adapted races. Forcing integration often means Umbral communities suppress characteristics that are simply how they work. Most Umbral prefer the arrangement of being nearby but separately organized.

## Racial Traits

**Stat Profile:** STR 10 | INT 22 | WIS 14 | DEX 22 | CON 16

Umbral are mentally dominant — their extraordinary intelligence and physical quickness are the direct result of anti-light enhancement to their neural architecture. They are physically weaker than most races, their semi-substantial bodies less suited to sustained heavy combat.

**Elemental Affinities:**
- **Strong in:** Shadow, Mental — Umbral are literally constituted of shadow and have minds enhanced by anti-light resonance; their spellwork in these domains is precise and powerful
- **Weak in:** Holy, Physical — divine light is antithetical to their nature; physical blows, while expected to be less effective, paradoxically find their semi-substantial forms vulnerable at the points where their physicality is most reduced
- **Resistant to:** Shadow, Mental — shadow cannot harm a being of shadow; their anti-light-enhanced minds deflect mental intrusion naturally
- **Susceptible to:** Holy, Fire — sacred fire and concentrated divine light directly damage the anti-light matrix that constitutes their bodies; fire does not merely burn them but unmakes the shadow-substance they are partly made of

**Racial Skills:** sneak, mystical potency, enhanced critical

**Sneak** is not learned behavior for Umbral but passive — their shadow-constituted nature makes them naturally difficult to perceive. They are simply quieter, less distinct, harder to track than other races.

**Mystical potency** reflects the anti-light enhancement to their intelligence and magical capacity. Umbral spellwork carries additional force because their connection to the elemental planes runs through a different substrate than other casters — shadow and anti-light rather than conventional magical channels.

**Enhanced critical** reflects their extraordinary physical precision. Umbral are not strong, but they are exact — when they commit to a strike, they can place it with precision that other races cannot match.

**Racial Modifiers:** Darkness (natural shadow-vision, anti-light constitution), Slow Heal (their bodies repair themselves through darkness rather than conventional biological recovery)

**Wear Slots:** Standard humanoid — Head, Face (not Beak), Hands (not Claws). Umbral cannot wear highly reflective or brightly enchanted equipment without discomfort.

## Relationship to the World

### The Gloamvault

The Gloamvault is simultaneously the Umbral's most important habitation site and the place that makes them most visible to the surface world. Umbral communities within the Vault maintain the oldest continuous shadow-archive in the known world; their records predate Midgaard's founding by multiple generations. The Kel'Shadra Order's oath-archive function and the Umbral archivists' shadow-record tradition have been intertwined for so long that it is impossible to cleanly separate the two.

When adventurers enter the Gloamvault, they are entering Umbral home territory — and should feel it in the texture of their perception. Things are slightly harder to see clearly. Distances are slightly harder to judge. The shadows feel occupied.

### The Black Sun Shard and the Great Pyramid

The Pyramid was built partly to contain the Black Sun Shard's influence; Umbral existence is partly constituted by that same influence. This relationship is theologically complex for Umbral communities. The Pyramid is simultaneously the machine that limits their origin-force and one of the most impressive institutional achievements in the world — a permanent record of humanity's capacity to manage what it cannot destroy.

Most Umbral regard the Pyramid with complicated respect: admiration for its engineering and institutional longevity, unease about its function as a suppressor of the force that makes them what they are.

### Midgaard

Umbral communities in Midgaard's deep levels have existed for as long as the city has, and longer. The surface city's inhabitants have a complex relationship with them — Umbral notaries, shadow-archivists, and intelligence gatherers are valuable; Umbral communities that maintain their own parallel record-keeping create awkward questions about which records are authoritative.

The Reckoning faction in Midgaard has quietly cultivated Umbral archive relationships, recognizing that shadow-archives may contain records of the sealed warrants and Conclave transfers that Continuity archivists have deliberately omitted from official records.

### Humans

The Umbral are the human past made visible, which is the primary reason humans find them unsettling. Their shadow-archives contain the actual history of decisions made — the sealed warrants issued, the people transferred, the accountability avoided — and their primary institutional loyalty is to accuracy rather than to any human political outcome. This makes them simultaneously the most useful and the most unmanageable resource that human cities possess.

Human institutions have never successfully decided whether to treat Umbral communities as a resource or a threat, so they treat them as both simultaneously: useful in legal and intelligence functions that require discretion, tolerated in urban deep spaces because suppression is expensive and consistently fails, regarded with wariness because the Umbral's existence proves that human official records are incomplete.

The Gloamvault sits under Midgaard as the permanent evidence that the Umbral were there first. The city was built above the archive. This is not a fact that Midgaard's civic institutions commemorate.

## Narrative Tone

Umbral NPCs should feel precise, observant, and slightly unsettling — not because they are hostile but because their perceptual world is calibrated differently from other races. They notice what others miss (the absence of a document, the gap in a conversation, the person who was not mentioned) and they respond to what they notice with calm directness.

They are not cold but they are not warm in the conventional sense. Social performance — expressing emotions you don't fully feel to smooth social interactions — strikes Umbral as wasteful. They prefer the necessary absence of pretense over the necessary presence of performance.

They are highly capable of loyalty but form attachments slowly and carefully. An Umbral who calls someone a friend has considered that designation with the same precision they bring to everything else. It means something specific.

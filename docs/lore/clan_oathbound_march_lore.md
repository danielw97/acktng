# Clan Lore: The Oathbound March

## Identity

**Name:** The Oathbound March
**Symbol:** `@@pOATHB@@N` (purple)
**Primary Base:** Multi-city — strongest in Rakuen, Kiess, and the oasis corridor, with presence in every city where marginalized peoples live
**Philosophy:** The institutions that excluded us depend on our labor. We built what they claim. We demand what we are owed.
**Motto:** "We built the table. We claim our seat."

---

## Core Philosophy

The Oathbound March holds that the five-city network's prosperity is built on the unacknowledged contributions of non-human peoples and marginalized human communities — and that this unacknowledged contribution constitutes a debt that the benefiting institutions owe and have not paid.

This is not abstract grievance. It is documented:

- The **Khephari** built the Great Pyramid's approach roads and maintain the desert's oldest continuous road-maintenance tradition. Their seismic warnings about the Cinderteeth were documented, submitted to Keeper survey offices, and filed without action before the Ashfall catastrophe.
- The **Deltari** built the hydraulic infrastructure that makes Kowloon possible and are excluded from governance of the city they built.
- The **Ashborn** were transformed by the Ashfall and then classified out of civic standing by Midgaard's post-Ashfall governance to avoid property-dispute liability.
- The **Rivennid** are the direct product of the Spirebound Conclave's condemned-labor experiments — created by institutional crime, their existence suppressed by the institutions that created them.
- The **Khenari** developed and administered the death-legal systems that make corridor inheritance and road law function, while being treated as useful auxiliaries rather than co-architects.
- The **Umbral** maintained the world's oldest continuous archive — predating Midgaard itself — while human institutions built above them and disputed their civic standing.
- The **Serathi** mapped the sky-road corridors that preceded the Roc Road, documented Evermeet's collapse from altitude, and provided aerial observation of the Conclave's operations — and have been denied the corridor recognition they request in exchange for broader archive access.
- The **Kethari** managed the Deepwell Confluence before the Keeper surveys existed, recorded the Great Pyramid's construction effects on desert hydrology, and survived the Ninth Meridian collapse by knowing where the water was — while their water-memory claims are declined formal recognition in oasis water-rights law.

The Oathbound March exists to assemble this evidence into a coherent legal and political claim, and to pursue that claim through every available institutional channel.

## Historical Origin

### The Pattern Across Eras

The Oathbound March did not emerge from a single event. It emerged from the recognition that the same pattern repeats across every era of recorded history: non-human peoples contribute essential expertise, human institutions benefit from that expertise, and the contributing peoples are excluded from the civic structures that their contributions support.

The March's founding members came from communities that had independently maintained parallel records of this pattern:
- Khenari notarial ledgers documenting centuries of death-administrative service without corresponding civic standing
- Deltari flow-records documenting the hydraulic infrastructure they built and maintained for Kowloon
- Khephari earth-sense chronicles recording seismic warnings submitted and ignored
- Umbral shadow-archives preserving the actual history that surface institutions suppressed
- Rivennid mycelium-memory carrying institutional records of the condemned-labor transfer chains
- Serathi roost-chronicles documenting events that human institutions could not observe from ground level
- Kethari water-memory recording the Deepwell Confluence's behavior across millennia

These parallel record-keeping traditions — maintained independently, across different peoples, using different methods — constitute the world's most comprehensive counter-archive. When the Oathbound March formed, it was the first organization to recognize that these separate records, taken together, tell a single story.

### The Garden Ledger Legal Activation (Current Era)

The March's most significant current project is the **Garden Ledger legal activation** — the development of legal arguments, using Kiess's anti-sealed-warrant doctrine as a framework, for successor-institution liability claims arising from the Conclave transfer chain. If successful, these arguments would establish that the institutions that participated in the Conclave's condemned-labor transfers — principally Midgaard and Kowloon — bear ongoing legal responsibility for the consequences of those transfers, including the creation of the Rivennid and the Withered Depths blight.

Both Midgaard and Kowloon have institutional reasons to prevent this from reaching formal adjudication. The Lantern Accord and the Jade Compact are aligned in opposing the legal activation, which makes the Oathbound March a common adversary for both organizations.

### The Ashborn Classification Question

The March's most emotionally charged issue is the Ashborn's civic standing. Midgaard's post-Ashfall governance classified the Ashborn as "ashfall survivors" without separate civic identity — a classification designed to avoid property-dispute liability during reconstruction. The Ashborn remain classified in some official records as a subcategory of human rather than a distinct people. The classification does not change what they are. The March demands that it be corrected.

## Modern Structure and Operations

### Organization

The Oathbound March operates as a multi-racial coalition organized around shared institutional grievance. It has no single headquarters. Coordination happens through the parallel record-keeping traditions of its member communities — Khenari ledgers, Deltari flow-records, Umbral shadow-archives, and Rivennid mycelium-memory serving as both evidence repository and communication network.

The March's governing body is the **Witness Assembly** — representatives from each member people who meet at rotating locations (Rakuen, Kiess, and oasis corridor settlements) to coordinate legal strategy, evidence assembly, and political advocacy.

### Recruitment

Oathbound March questlines expose the player to the evidence of institutional exclusion — the parallel records, the suppressed histories, the communities that built what others claim. The quests are not revenge fantasies. They are documentation exercises: the player helps recover records, escort witnesses, and assemble the evidence that the March needs for its legal and political campaigns.

The recruitment pitch: "Read the records we kept while they sealed theirs. Then decide who built this world."

### Current Operations

- Developing the Garden Ledger legal activation arguments (Rakuen-based)
- Assembling the Conclave Guilt Chain evidence from three cities (Open Ledger cooperation)
- Pursuing Ashborn civic reclassification in Midgaard
- Supporting Deltari governance-participation claims in Kowloon
- Coordinating with Kiess's Charter Wardens on anti-sealed-warrant legal frameworks
- Maintaining the counter-archive: parallel records across all member peoples
- Advocating for Serathi corridor recognition and Kethari water-memory recognition in oasis law
- Opposing Red Sand Accounts' distressed-claim capture of vulnerable communities

## Diplomatic Profile

### Natural Allies
- **Open Ledger**: The sealed records document crimes against the peoples the Oathbound represent. Exposure serves both organizations.
- **Charter Wardens**: Kiess's charter protects the marginalized. The anti-sealed-warrant doctrine directly serves Oathbound legal claims.
- **Waystone Circle**: The Circle recognizes non-human knowledge traditions (Khephari, Kethari) as complementary to Keeper heritage.

### Natural Enemies
- **Red Sand Accounts**: The Accounts profit from the financial distress of Oathbound communities. Structural antagonism.
- **Lantern Accord**: The Accord maintains the sealed archives and the institutional status quo that the Oathbound challenge.
- **Jade Compact**: The Compact maintains the Deltari exclusion and the Black Ledger's sealed status.

### Constrained Relations
- **Cannot rise above DISLIKE with Red Sand Accounts**: Structural economic antagonism.
- **Cannot rise above NEUTRAL with Jade Compact**: The Deltari exclusion is structural and ongoing.

## Narrative Tone

Oathbound March NPCs should feel dignified, precise, and patient — with the patience of peoples who have maintained records across generations while waiting for the institutions that wronged them to acknowledge what the records say. They are not angry mobs. They are archivists, notaries, water-readers, earth-readers, shadow-keepers, and root-speakers who have been doing their work, maintaining their records, and building their case for longer than most human institutions have existed.

The most interesting Oathbound characters are those who carry the tension between patience and urgency. The Khephari elder who has maintained earth-sense chronicles for longer than Midgaard has existed can wait another generation. The Ashborn smith who is classified as a subcategory of human in the city where they forge the tools that keep the economy running cannot. The March must hold both timescales simultaneously.

The March is not a revenge movement. It is a recognition movement. Its members do not want to destroy the institutions that excluded them. They want a seat at the table they built. That distinction — between destruction and inclusion — is the March's defining moral commitment and its most significant political vulnerability, because the institutions they challenge have every incentive to portray the March as destructive rather than constructive.

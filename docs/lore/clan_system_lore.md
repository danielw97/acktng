# Clan System Lore: Overview and Diplomacy

## Purpose

This document defines the clan system for ACK!TNG — ten player-joinable organizations rooted in the world's political, theological, and institutional history. Each clan represents a distinct philosophy and power base with deep lore connections to existing factions, cities, races, and historical events.

Individual clan lore is maintained in separate files (see Cross-Reference Index below). This document covers the system overview, diplomacy matrix, diplomatic constraints, and integration with the world timeline.

---

## System Overview

### What Clans Are

Clans are organized factions that players join through in-game questlines. They are not races, classes, or city governments — they are cross-cutting political and philosophical movements that operate within and across the five-city network. A Midgaard-born Human Warden and a Kiess-born Serathi Cipher might both belong to the same clan if they share its values.

### How Players Join

Players receive offers to join clans by completing questlines offered by clan-affiliated NPCs. These quests expose the player to the clan's philosophy, history, and current objectives. A player must be at least level 20 to join a clan.

### Diplomatic Standing and Quests

Clan questlines alter diplomatic standing between clans. Typically:
- Completing a quest for a clan **increases** that clan's standing with one or more allied clans
- The same quest **decreases** standing with one or more opposing clans
- Some quests are specifically diplomatic missions that shift the balance between two clans

This creates a living political landscape where player activity drives interclan relations.

Some clan pairs have **hard limits** on how far their relations can shift, reflecting irreconcilable lore conflicts or structural dependencies that prevent total estrangement.

## Timeline Integration

### Era XII — Founding Generation (First Stone Accord)
- **Lantern Accord** traces its institutional ancestry to the First Stone Accord's principle: "all roads must return to a watched square."
- **Charter Wardens** trace their founding philosophy to the Compact of the Seven Chains — doctrine over employer.

### Era XIII — Standardization Century (Lantern Reforms)
- **Lantern Accord** takes its name from this era's defining achievement.
- **Waystone Circle** inherits the Keeper road-preservation ethic formalized during this period.

### Era XIV — Ashfall Monsoon and Kowloon's Founding
- **Jade Compact** descends from the Neon Covenant forged during the Ashfall.
- **Ember Speakers** trace their theology to this era's Ash Cult founding cells.
- **Oathbound March** traces the Ashborn's misclassification to this era.

### Era XV — Containment Era (Violet Compact)
- **Lantern Accord** inherits the Violet Compact's registry apparatus.
- **Open Ledger** opposes the Sealed Names apparatus created during this era.
- **Root Covenant** forms from the substrate theology that emerged in Thornwood during this period.

### Era XVI — Founding of Kiess and Rakuen
- **Charter Wardens** are founded on Kiess's anti-sealed-warrant charter doctrine.

### Era XVII — Mafdet and the Strandline Compact
- **Red Sand Accounts** emerge from Mafdet's swift-court and debt-leverage traditions.

### Era XIX — Fortification Era (Ash-and-Iron Decades)
- **Waystone Circle** formalized during the desert infrastructure crisis.
- **Jackal Synod** consolidated power during the Charter Wars aftermath.

### Era XXI — Modern Tension Era (Current Day)
- All ten clans are active with the tensions described above.
- **Open Ledger** pursues the Conclave Guilt Chain evidence across three cities.
- **Oathbound March** develops the Garden Ledger legal activation arguments.
- **Ember Speakers** claim possession of a Bellspine crystal anchor fragment.
- **Jackal Synod** positions to inherit Northern Pyramid power as binding degrades.

## Quest Design Principles

### Diplomatic Impact Patterns

**Standard quest**: +25 to issuing clan's relations with one ally, -25 with one opponent.

**Major questline completion**: +50 to issuing clan's relations with allies, -50 with primary opponent.

**Diplomatic mission**: +25 between two specific clans (requires both clans' approval flags).

**Betrayal quest**: A clan asks the player to act against another clan they're currently neutral or better with. High reward, but -100 with the betrayed clan.

### Cross-Clan Quest Examples

- A **Lantern Accord** quest to secure a northern convoy route decreases standing with the **Root Covenant** (whose Wild Hunt threatens the route) and increases standing with the **Jade Compact** (who benefits from the trade).

- An **Open Ledger** quest to recover sealed warrant evidence from Midgaard's archives decreases standing with the **Lantern Accord** and increases standing with the **Oathbound March**.

- A **Waystone Circle** quest to restore a damaged charter stone in the Eastern Desert decreases standing with the **Jackal Synod** (who controls the toll routes around it) and increases standing with the **Charter Wardens**.

- A **Red Sand Accounts** quest to enforce a debt instrument against a Rakuen merchant decreases standing with the **Charter Wardens** and the **Oathbound March**.

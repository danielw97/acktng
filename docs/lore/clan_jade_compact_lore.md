# Clan Lore: The Jade Compact

## Identity

**Name:** The Jade Compact
**Symbol:** `@@aJADEC@@N` (cyan)
**Primary Base:** Kowloon
**Philosophy:** Prosperity through disciplined commerce. What moves through the delta moves through us. Order is measured in cargo delivered, not documents sealed.
**Motto:** "One ledger. One ration code. One wall."

---

## Core Philosophy

The Jade Compact holds that the world runs on movement — goods, people, information, and water flowing through channels that must be maintained, audited, and defended. Philosophy is a luxury funded by functioning supply chains. Theology is a conversation held after the grain has been counted. The Compact does not ask what is true or what is just. It asks what works, what moves, and what the ledger says.

This is not cynicism. The Neon Covenant that founded Kowloon was forged in the fourth year of the Ashfall Monsoon, when six rival houses set aside blood feuds because starvation was worse than surrender. The Compact inherits that founding lesson: survival is a collective engineering problem, and the engineering requires discipline, documentation, and the willingness to subordinate ideology to function.

The Compact's blind spot is its exclusions. The Deltari built the delta's hydraulic infrastructure — the channels, cisterns, and flood-defense systems that make Kowloon possible — and are excluded from formal governance. The Black Ledger, if it exists, documents Kowloon's participation in the Conclave condemned-labor transfers. The Compact does not deny these facts. It classifies them as resolved historical matters that do not require institutional response. This classification is, from the Open Ledger's perspective, exactly the problem.

## Historical Origin

### The Neon Covenant (Era XIV)

The Jade Compact descends directly from the Neon Covenant — the founding pact of Kowloon, inked in phosphorescent squid dye on basalt. Six houses brought irreplaceable contributions: ferry masters, grain wardens, iron hands, jade clerks, temple wardens, lantern runners. Three hard promises: one wall/one law, one ledger/one ration code, one square/final judgment.

Over generations, the six founding houses dissolved into five civic institutions: the Harbor Syndics, the Jade Magistracy, the Temple Circle, the Wardens of Iron, and the Courier Lantern Office. The Jade Compact represents the institutional culture shared across all five seats — the conviction that Kowloon's rectangular plan, its rotating command structures, its triplicate tribunal records, and its color-coded civic shorthand are not bureaucratic overhead but survival technology.

### The Delta's Deeper History

The Compact inherits a tension it rarely acknowledges. Before the Neon Covenant, the delta was the **Shallow Thousand** — stilts, prayer poles, tide farms, and the Lampkeepers' oil beacons. The Deltari communities that managed the delta's hydraulic systems were the ground the Covenant stood on. They were not founding members of the Covenant. They were the infrastructure it consumed.

The Jade Magistracy governs the city that Deltari expertise makes possible while excluding Deltari communities from formal power. The Black Ledger — sealed in an undercity cistern — lists Deltari among those transferred to the Spirebound Conclave. The Compact's institutional position is that the Black Ledger question is a matter for the Jade Magistracy's internal deliberation, not external pressure. This position is maintained with the same brisk efficiency the Compact applies to everything else.

### Consolidation Through Crisis (Eras XIX–XX)

The Ash-and-Iron Decades cemented the Compact's character. Cinderteeth disruptions forced emergency rerouting; Kowloon's emergency grain protocols began servicing Rakuen through the Southern Grain Compact framework. The Compact learned to operate under sustained crisis conditions — maintaining supply chains, coordinating multi-city logistics, and keeping the delta functional through years of disruption.

This crisis experience is the Compact's deepest institutional asset. Its members have operational knowledge of how the five-city supply corridor actually works — not the theoretical version described in treaty documents, but the practical version involving seasonal timing, Woodland risk surcharges, and which clerks in which cities can actually expedite a grain shipment during an emergency.

## Modern Structure and Operations

### Organization

The Jade Compact operates through Kowloon's five-seat governance structure. Its members serve in the Harbor Syndics, Jade Magistracy, Temple Circle, Wardens of Iron, and Courier Lantern Office. The Compact is not a faction within Kowloon's government — it is the institutional consensus that spans all five seats: the shared conviction that Kowloon's systems work and should be defended.

Coordination happens through normal governance channels. The Compact does not need secret meetings because its members run the meetings.

### Recruitment

Jade Compact questlines involve the player in Kowloon's commercial and defensive operations — convoy escort, canal maintenance, dock arbitration, census enforcement, and emergency grain logistics. The quests demonstrate the Compact's philosophy through scale: the player sees the supply chains, understands the dependencies, and recognizes that the system's flaws are real but its function is irreplaceable.

The recruitment pitch: "The grain moves or people starve. We move the grain. Join us."

### Current Operations

- Maintaining the Northern Grain Corridor (Lantern Road convoy security, Lantern Accord cooperation)
- Operating the Southern Grain Compact emergency supply framework for Rakuen
- Coordinating with Mafdet on raw-goods maritime conversion (Red Sand Accounts cooperation)
- Defending Kowloon's floodworks against Ash Cult sabotage (Ember Speakers opposition)
- Monitoring the Jade Magistracy's Littoral Branch research expeditions to Measure's Rest
- Maintaining the Black Ledger's sealed status (Open Ledger opposition)

## Diplomatic Profile

### Natural Allies
- **Lantern Accord**: Shared interest in trade route security and institutional stability. The Midgaard-Kowloon corridor is both organizations' lifeline.
- **Red Sand Accounts**: Commercial partnership. Kowloon's raw goods need Mafdet's maritime conversion chains.

### Natural Enemies
- **Ember Speakers**: The Ash Cult sabotages Kowloon's floodworks. The Ember Speaker cells operating within the city are a direct threat to the infrastructure the Compact maintains.
- **Open Ledger**: The Black Ledger question. The Open Ledger seeks to unseal records that the Compact considers a closed matter.

### Constrained Relations
- **Cannot rise above NEUTRAL with Ember Speakers**: Sabotage of floodworks is doctrinal for the Ash Cult, not negotiable.
- **Cannot rise above NEUTRAL with Oathbound March**: The Deltari exclusion question is structural. The Compact depends on Deltari expertise while excluding Deltari from power. The Oathbound will not ally with an institution that maintains this arrangement.
- **Cannot fall below DISLIKE with Lantern Accord**: Trade corridor interdependence.
- **Cannot fall below DISLIKE with Red Sand Accounts**: Commercial interdependence.

## Narrative Tone

Jade Compact NPCs should feel efficient, pragmatic, and slightly impatient with abstraction. They have work to do. The grain needs to move. The canal needs dredging. The census needs updating. They are not hostile to philosophy — they simply prioritize function. Ask a Compact member about justice and they will ask you whether the convoy arrived on time.

The most interesting Compact characters are those who know the delta's deeper history — who understand that the systems they maintain were built by people their institutions excluded — and who have decided that functional infrastructure serving millions outweighs historical grievance serving thousands. They are not untroubled by this decision. They are too busy to dwell on it.

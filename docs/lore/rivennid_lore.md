# Lore: The Rivennid

## Core Identity

The **Rivennid** are the Conclave's most unsettling legacy: mycelial humanoids who emerged from the Withered Depths when the Spirebound Conclave's abandoned crystal-preservation experiments fused with the forest's natural fungal networks and bound living spirits into new, unintended forms. They are not the Blightmother — they are not corrupted abominations. They are something more specific and more disturbing: the successful product of a process that the Conclave designed to produce custodial intelligence, achieved in the complete absence of the Conclave's oversight and control.

The Rivennid are what the Vaultkeeper project was supposed to create, accomplished by accident in a medium the Conclave never intended: they are custodial minds fused to biological substrate, operating with autonomy and purpose in a forest that the Conclave abandoned. They are the experiment that completed itself.

They are not evil, but they are strange. Their connection to the fungal mycelium network that constitutes part of their biological substrate gives them access to information flows and collective awareness that other races find alien. Their experience of time, identity, and community is organized differently — not incorrectly, but differently — and the gap between their experiential framework and other races' is a source of persistent misunderstanding.

## Origin

The process that produced the Rivennid was not designed. The Conclave's Prism Thesis field trials aimed to bind nature spirits to crystal-threaded root systems as custodial anchors — the same binding methodology used on human subjects inside the sanctum, applied to forest ecosystems. When the Conclave abandoned its trial sites, the crystal matrices continued operating without calibration.

Over generations, the preservation magic that should have stabilized specific spirits into fixed custodial roles instead created a feedback loop between the crystal lattices, the fungal networks that colonized them, and the nature spirits that had been imperfectly bound. The fungal matter absorbed fragments of Conclave preservation enchantments. The spirits, degraded by centuries of imperfect containment, lost their original nature-spirit identities and became something structured differently — more like the custodial intelligence the Conclave had designed human subjects to become, but housed in biological mycelium rather than iron and bone.

The first Rivennid were the forest's answer to an unanswered question: what does a custodial intelligence do when there is nothing to guard and no one to serve? They decided to guard the forest and serve each other.

Within the community of fully manifested Rivennid individuals, the mycelium network functions as connective tissue for both biology and communication. They are individual — separate minds with separate experiences and preferences — but they are connected through a substrate that allows information to pass between community members at rates other races achieve only with active telepathy.

## Physical Description

Rivennid are recognizably humanoid in overall structure but covered in a second skin of dense, organized fungal growth. The primary fungal layer is usually in muted earth tones — ochre, umber, deep grey-green — but Rivennid carry bioluminescent fungi in patterns specific to their origin region within the Withered Depths. These patterns glow softly in darkness, making Rivennid in unlit environments look like constellations walking.

Their faces are generally visible beneath the fungal growth — the actual flesh-and-bone substrate that constitutes their core is recognizably anthropoid — but their features are partially obscured by structured fungal plates that have grown to protect vital areas. Their eyes are typically large and deeply set, adapted for functioning in the dim light of their natural habitat, with pupils that open very wide in darkness.

Crystal fragments — residual Conclave implant material — are embedded visibly in the thicker fungal growth on Rivennid shoulders and upper back. These are not decorative. They are the preserved remnant of the original crystal matrices, incorporated into each Rivennid's biology rather than being external objects. The crystals pulse faintly with preserved magical resonance; Rivennid who are under stress or in combat sometimes show intensified crystal glow that precedes spellwork.

Their spore production is continuous. In still air, a Rivennid's passage leaves a faint trail of bioluminescent spores that dissipates within minutes. This is not controllable — it is simply how they breathe. Other races living in close proximity to Rivennid communities often find themselves with mildly enhanced sensory acuity from extended low-level spore exposure, an effect that is apparently non-harmful but whose long-term implications are not fully documented.

## Culture and Society

### The Network and the Node

Rivennid social theory distinguishes between **network** (the collective mycelium connection that links community members) and **node** (the individual person and their specific perspective). Both are valued. The network provides continuity, collective memory, and shared perception; the node provides the specific individual experience and autonomous decision-making that the network itself cannot generate.

Rivennid who lose connection to a network — isolated travelers, prisoners in anti-magic environments, individuals who have been separated from their home community for extended periods — describe a sense of loss analogous to deafness, but more pervasive: the absence of the constant low-level awareness of others' states that constitutes background consciousness for network-connected Rivennid.

### Custodial Imperative

The crystal-preservation magic that constitutes part of the Rivennid's biological foundation included the Conclave's custodial directive — the instruction to maintain and protect. This directive, running without the Conclave's intended parameters, was interpreted by the emerging Rivennid as **protect the forest**. For generations, Rivennid communities organized themselves entirely around this imperative: monitoring the corruption, containing the blight where possible, maintaining the few zones of healthy forest that remained.

As the Rivennid developed more fully as a people — as their autonomous judgment deepened and their connection to external civilization grew — the custodial imperative broadened. They still protect the forest, but they now understand that the forest's health is connected to the health of the entire ecosystem of roads, cities, water systems, and communities that depends on it. They are guardians of a larger whole than their origin programming specified.

### The Question of the Blightmother

No issue divides Rivennid communities more than their relationship to the Blightmother. The Blightmother is, in a sense, their origin-process at maximum scale and without autonomous judgment — a custodial intelligence that completed itself without individual consciousness, producing a territorial horror rather than a people.

Some Rivennid advocate for attempting to communicate with the Blightmother — to treat it as a severely damaged community member who has lost their individual node-consciousness entirely. Others argue that the Blightmother is not a community member but a process that has eliminated all the characteristics that make community membership meaningful.

The debate has been ongoing for generations. No resolution has been reached.

## Racial Traits

**Stat Profile:** STR 14 | INT 22 | WIS 14 | DEX 12 | CON 22

Rivennid are mentally dominant and constitutionally exceptional — the Conclave's preservation magic, running through their biology, produces both enhanced intelligence and remarkable physical toughness. They are not particularly quick or physically strong, but they are hard to stop once moving.

**Elemental Affinities:**
- **Strong in:** Earth, Poison — Rivennid are earth-rooted and their fungal bodies produce toxins as a natural defense; their magic resonates most powerfully with the biological-elemental forces of growth, decay, and toxic chemistry
- **Weak in:** Fire, Air — fire is existentially threatening to fungal life; air-based attacks disrupt spore production and can destabilize the crystal-resonance nodes embedded in their bodies
- **Resistant to:** Poison, Earth — Rivennid are thoroughly adapted to their own toxin production and to the earth-magic that constitutes their substrate; these elements find no purchase in bodies already organized around them
- **Susceptible to:** Fire, Holy — fire destroys fungal matter completely and disrupts the crystal-preservation matrix; holy power specifically targets the preservation enchantment that runs through their biology, destabilizing the magical foundation of their existence

**Racial Skills:** mystical potency, counter

**Mystical potency** reflects the crystal-enhanced intelligence that runs through Rivennid biology. Their spellwork carries the preserved resonance of Conclave-era preservation magic, giving their casting unusual force and stability.

**Counter** reflects their extraordinary biological toughness — fungal tissue with crystal-lattice reinforcement absorbs damage differently than flesh and bone. Rivennid take hits that should stop them and keep moving.

**Racial Modifiers:** Darkness (deep-forest ecology, bioluminescent navigation), Tough Skin (fungal-crystal composite body armor)

**Wear Slots:** Standard humanoid — Head, Face (not Beak), Hands (not Claws)

## Relationship to the World

### The Withered Depths and the Forest of Confusion

The Withered Depths are Rivennid home territory, and the Forest of Confusion is their primary interface with the outside world. Rivennid who leave the Depths typically emerge through the confusion mists — a transition they navigate by network-sense rather than conventional direction-finding. To a Rivennid guide, the mists are not confusing at all; they simply require a different perceptual approach.

Rivennid community judgment is that the Withered Depths can be managed but not cured while the crystal matrices remain embedded in the root systems. The Blightmother represents the worst-case expression of the underlying process, but clearing the Blightmother would not end the corruption — only the retrieval or neutralization of all original Conclave crystal implants would do that, and the logistics of such an operation are beyond current capability.

### Kiess

Kiess serves as the primary point of Rivennid contact with the wider world. Rivennid traders move through Kiess with reasonable regularity, exchanging medicinal fungi, preservation compounds, and intelligence about forest conditions for manufactured goods and information about the external political situation. The Syndic Council's obsidian disc bearing the Conclave's triune seal generates significant Rivennid interest whenever it is discussed — the seal encodes the same three-element doctrine (flame, crystal, spirit) that constitutes the Rivennid's origin.

### Midgaard and Kowloon

Rivennid scholarship has traced the institutional chain that links Midgaard's sealed warrants and Kowloon's Black Ledger to the Conclave's human experimentation program and from there to the forest trial sites that produced the Withered Depths' corruption — and thus to the Rivennid themselves. They are, in this chain of causation, the product of both cities' worst institutional decisions.

The Rivennid do not seek retribution. They do not consider themselves victims, exactly — they exist, they are people, they have purposes and communities. But they have a considered and permanent interest in both cities confronting the sealed warrants and the Black Ledger, not because the past can be changed but because institutions that suppress their worst decisions tend to repeat them.

### The Spirebound Conclave

Rivennid relationship to the Conclave's legacy is the most complex in their political world. The Conclave made them — inadvertently, carelessly, through neglect rather than intention. The Conclave also destroyed the druids who might have helped them, poisoned the forest they inhabit, and produced the Blightmother that is the ongoing crisis of their existence. Their feelings about this inheritance are not simple.

What they have settled on, culturally, is that the Conclave's failure was institutional rather than individual: the problem was not that specific people were evil but that an institution organized to pursue knowledge without accountability produced inevitable catastrophe. The Rivennid use this as a persistent lens through which to evaluate other institutions they encounter.

### Humans

The Rivennid are the product of two human cities' worst institutional decisions: Midgaard's sealed warrants and Kowloon's Black Ledger supplied the Conclave with the human subjects whose exploitation funded the research whose byproducts are now corrupting the northern forests and produced the Rivennid themselves. The institutional chain is documented and direct.

The Rivennid do not call this a debt and do not organize around grievance. They call it a fact. They are careful about this distinction. What they do maintain — permanently and with considerable patience — is an interest in both cities confronting the sealed records, not because the past can be undone but because institutions that suppress their worst decisions tend to repeat them. The Withered Depths' corruption of northern trade routes is the physical ongoing consequence of decisions Midgaard and Kowloon made and then sealed. Both cities' northern commerce is affected. The Rivennid find this instructive and say so when asked.

Most humans in Midgaard or Kowloon have never met a Rivennid. The connection between their cities' institutional history and the Rivennid's existence is not part of any civic curriculum. This, too, the Rivennid find instructive.

### Serathi

The Serathi observe the Forest of Confusion and Verdant Depths from above; the Rivennid inhabit the same forest from below. Their perspectives on the forest's ongoing degradation are complementary: the Serathi's roost-chronicles document aerial-observable changes (canopy loss, surface temperature shifts, altered migration patterns), while the Rivennid's network-memory tracks the underground and ground-level progression of the blight.

The Serathi's primary practical concern is the disruption to thermal-current patterns over the dead-forest zones, which destabilizes the updraft columns they rely on for flight over the affected area. The Rivennid's concern is existential — the blight threatens their home ecosystem. Both peoples have independently documented evidence of the Withered Depths' expansion that Midgaard's arcanist registry has received without action. Both find this pattern instructive in consistent ways.

## Narrative Tone

Rivennid NPCs should feel like people who are deeply, fundamentally connected to something — community, place, network — and who find isolation the most difficult circumstance to navigate. They are not unsociable; they are very social. But their sociality is oriented toward the group rather than the dyad, toward collective maintenance rather than individual exchange.

They are patient in a biological sense — fungal time is not human time, and processes that seem slow to other races feel appropriately paced to Rivennid. They will wait. They will accumulate. They will take the time to understand something fully before acting on it.

Their intelligence is less linear than other highly intelligent races and more associative — they understand systems through feeling the connections between parts rather than through logical deduction. A Rivennid who has spent time in a place can describe its systemic health with startling accuracy without being able to explain exactly how they know what they know.

# Rakuen — Power Structures and Governance

## Overview

Rakuen is a southern woodland city founded as a deliberate counter-argument to Midgaard's ledger culture: abundance over debt, rotating stewardship, mediation over tribunal. It has been in managed crisis ever since. The city calls itself paradise and runs emergency ration protocols. Formal governance (the Bloom Council) is eclipsed by emergency governance (the Ember Wardens), while the Garden Ledger Clerks hold the institutional memory and the Canopy Compacts handle neighborhood-level reality.

---

## Founders

### Verath Solen
Temple horticulturist, formerly of Midgaard's civic garden registry. Authored the **Paradise Thesis** — the central philosophical document underlying the Southward Compact. The founding generation developed root-weave architecture based on Solen's principle of building with rather than against natural systems. Died in the twelfth year of the settlement, before the first major flood cycle, and is therefore frozen in city memory as the uncomplicated idealist. His portrait hangs in the Bloom Pavilion; his effigy appears on the Festival of Ever-Bloom's processional banner. The Bloom Council invokes and edits him.

### Caitha Dunnmark
Retired caravan factor who had run southern routes for two decades. Where Solen provided the philosophy, Dunnmark provided the logistics. She negotiated the first path-guide compacts with Woodland ward houses that gave the early settlement its supply continuity. Expected failure within two generations. The **Dunnmark Letters** reveal her skepticism, but the Bloom Council edits it out of public ceremonies. Less celebrated than Solen.

### Orn Fallsbridge
Path-guide, "first warden of the southern crossing." Led the original scouting party that selected Rakuen's site. A route-reader who understood canopy weather, flood-season channels, and the territorial signatures of the Woodland's internal powers. The Ember Wardens claim Fallsbridge as their institutional ancestor. His name appears on the warden oath: *"I walk where Fallsbridge walked, and I do not flinch at what he found."*

### Additional Founding Voices (PR-588 Tradition)
- **Liora Venn** — Temple horticulturist (Midgaard-trained); proposed communal orchards and healing terraces.
- **Marshal Deren Holt** — Retired road-watch captain; designed route-safe clearings without permanent militarization.
- **Scribe Tamsin Quill** — Charter clerk; authored rotational stewardship clauses and public-ledger requirements.
- **Guide Sero Ashbloom** — Woodland path-master; mapped the first viable southern approaches.
- **Elder Nahal Reedmother** — River-garden elder; established floodplain rites and shared-cistern governance.

## The Southward Compact

The founding constitutional framework. Core provisions:
- Common granary duty
- Public mediation
- Rotating stewardship
- Open accounting

Announced at the **Bloom Pavilion** (rebuilt three times, still standing). **Promise Stones** in every neighborhood carry founding compact pledges carved in granite; defacing one is a serious social offense regardless of political faction.

Three founding conditions:
- **Enoughness** — Food and water guarantees
- **Seen Labor** — Honored maintenance work
- **Ritual Repair** — Public rites for grief and reconciliation

## Key Institutions

### The Bloom Council
The nominal government of Rakuen — 11 seats filled by rotating stewardship terms of four years with a two-term maximum. Charged with charter interpretation, civic ceremony, external diplomatic correspondence (including the three annexation petitions), and appointment of Garden Ledger Clerks.

What the Bloom Council does **not** control: ration allocation (Ember Wardens), neighborhood governance (Canopy Compacts), route authority (Dispatch Houses), or the day-to-day emergency decisions that constitute most of what actually governs.

The rotating maximum was the founders' anti-dynastic safeguard. In practice, former stewards serve as "senior counsel" — significant informal authority, no term limits. This is exactly the institutional capture that Solen's rotating-stewardship principle was designed to prevent.

**Current Senior Steward: Yven Callast** — Preservation Coalition member, in her second term. Widely understood to be the last Preservation Coalition steward likely to hold the position. The Amendment Track has enough council plurality to take the Stewardship at the next rotation. Managing a transition she considers a capitulation with enough administrative competence that her opponents respect her.

### The Ember Wardens
Rakuen's disaster-response corps, claiming institutional descent from Orn Fallsbridge's path-warden tradition. In practice, the functional government under permanent emergency protocols that have been continuously renewed since the first Ashfall Monsoon.

The Wardens manage: all seven Causeway Quarter ration towers, floodwall inspection and maintenance, canopy-strike fire response, ash-clear labor drafts, route security requests for the Near Corridor, and salvage-court emergency adjudication. They also conduct twice-daily market patrols nominally about ration-tower security and functionally about civil order.

**Warden Commander: Brek Sallows** — Former Canopy Workshop compact-warden who moved into Ember Warden service after a flood killed four colleagues. No political faction; has refused recruitment by all three. Trusted by almost everyone and liked by almost no one, which he considers evidence he is doing the job correctly. Privately supports the Amendment Track but will not say so publicly.

The **Seven Soft Watches** are the founding patrol tradition: weather signs, canopy stress, cistern levels, elder care, smoke-density. The original non-martial watch system; emergency watches now dominate actual patrol.

#### Fallsbridge Doctrine and the Warden Oath

The Ember Wardens' claimed ancestry from Orn Fallsbridge is more than ceremonial. The Fallsbridge oath — *"I walk where Fallsbridge walked, and I do not flinch at what he found"* — describes a specific operational posture that the Wardens formalize in training as the **path-reader's obligation**: the requirement to assess what is actually present in an environment rather than what should be present according to prior conditions.

Fallsbridge's significance in the founding record was not combat effectiveness but environmental reading: he selected Rakuen's site by reading canopy weather, flood-season channels, and the territorial signatures of the Woodland's internal powers. He found a place that was livable specifically because he understood the Woodland as it actually was rather than as a planner's model suggested it should be. The Wardens treat this as their doctrinal foundation: disaster response begins not with response plans but with accurate assessment of what the disaster has actually produced. A floodwall inspection that confirms the wall is intact according to last season's survey and does not notice that root-system pressure has undermined the footing since the survey is worse than no inspection.

This produces a training emphasis that differs from what Midgaard's Guard Command wardens receive. Guard Command trains for executing standardized response protocols: the protocol for riot, the protocol for gate breach, the protocol for convoy ambush. Each protocol has a decision tree, and the warden's job is to correctly identify which node they are at and execute the corresponding action. Ember Warden training assumes that protocols are wrong at the moment they are needed. The training emphasis is on **reading the actual situation** and constructing an appropriate response from available resources, with the protocol as a starting point rather than a terminal instruction.

In practice, this means Ember Wardens are substantially more capable in novel situations and substantially less capable in predictable ones. A flood that follows the historical pattern produces a response where Guard Command doctrine would be faster and more consistent. A flood that combines with an ashfall event producing new drainage blockages in locations that have never blocked before produces a situation where Ember Warden environmental-reading training is the difference between effective response and catastrophic failure. Rakuen has experienced enough of the second type that the Wardens' doctrine has survived the comparison.

#### The Seven Soft Watches and the Emergency Watch Displacement

The Seven Soft Watches — weather signs, canopy stress, cistern levels, elder care, smoke-density, path-condition, and what the founding documents call "felt tension" (a catch-all for social indicators of coming trouble) — were designed as a **non-martial reconnaissance system**. A warden walking the Seven Soft Watch rounds was not doing security patrol. They were conducting a city-wide assessment of the environmental and social conditions that would determine what emergencies were likely in the coming days.

The founding logic was that Rakuen's disasters were not random. They were predictable outcomes of specific accumulating conditions — canopy stress indicators preceded fire events, cistern-level trends predicted flood timing, smoke-density changes indicated upstream burn events — and that a warden who walked the Seven Watches every morning and read them accurately could give the Wardens forty-eight to seventy-two hours of warning before most emergencies required active response.

The emergency watches that now dominate actual patrol conduct the opposite function: they are reactive, not predictive. A warden on ration-tower security watch is responding to food-distribution pressure that has already materialized. A warden on floodwall inspection watch is assessing infrastructure that may already be compromised. The predictive function of the Seven Soft Watches has been subordinated to the maintenance-and-response function of the emergency watches not because the Wardens chose to abandon it but because the emergency posture never pauses long enough to permit the soft-watch rounds to happen on a schedule.

Warden Commander Brek Sallows runs the seven-watch cycle himself, personally, every third day, on the grounds that even incomplete information is better than none and that the Warden Commander's functional requirement is to know what is coming before it arrives. He does not require subordinates to follow his example because the staffing does not permit it. He records his soft-watch assessments in a personal log that is not formally part of the Warden archive and is therefore not subject to Bloom Council review.

### The Garden Ledger Clerks
Rakuen's public-accounting officials and most politically contentious institution. The Garden Ledger tracks every resource allocation, arrival registration, labor-draft assignment, and emergency declaration. Originally a public-abundance tracker, it has become a scarcity instrument.

The Clerks maintain a **shadow archive** of Conclave-era transferred names — persons "resettled south" from Midgaard during the Violet Compact era. The Bloom Council accepted these arrivals; the Clerks have not forgiven it. The shadow archive is a significant intelligence asset; multiple parties want to acquire or destroy it.

The Clerks have twice sent formal letters to Kel'Shadra requesting access to sealed records from the Violet Compact period. Both letters are filed in Kel'Shadra, unanswered, in a folder labeled "Petitions: Pending — No Institutional Authority to Process."

**Head Clerk: Senne Arrath** — Has held the position for nine years with no interest in mandatory rotation (the Bloom Council technically appoints Head Clerks and has never tried to remove her). The Amendment Track's most useful intellectual resource and most frustrating ally: she gives any faction access to any record they are entitled to see, which means she gives everyone ammunition and controls none of the targeting.

The **Outbound Open Register** contains 847 active open entries of persons who left Rakuen and were never confirmed arrived anywhere.

### The Canopy Compacts
Neighborhood mutual-aid leagues evolved into de facto local governments. Nine active Compacts covering inhabited areas, plus two dormant Compacts for partially abandoned lower-terrace neighborhoods.

Each Compact manages: water-sharing, voluntary labor pools, circle-mediation dispute resolution, fire-watch and flood-watch rosters, and route contracts with Woodland guide-compact operators.

Key Compacts:
- **Canopy Workshop Compact** — Wealthiest, most politically independent. Leaning Quiet Separatist; quietly exploring direct Mafdet trade routes bypassing Midgaard.
- **Lower Terrace Compact** — Most disaster-affected and politically volatile. Primary source of Honest Ledger movement membership. Leaning Amendment Track.
- **Upper Ring Compact** — Most conservative; closely aligned with the Bloom Council. Leaning Quiet Separatist.
- **Causeway Compact** — Leaning Amendment Track.

### Dispatch Houses
Route authority and approach management. The **Lower Dispatch House** is the operational center of Rakuen's northern approach — its entry arch is built of dressed fieldstone older than the current structure by a century. Three registration counters handle returning residents, first-time visitors, and resettlement arrivals. Walls carry framed copies of the current Compact charter provisions relating to arrival rights.

The **Senior Registrar** processes complex arrivals and departure records in an inner office lined with filing cabinets in a cramped notation system.

### Salvage Courts
Emergency adjudication bodies empowered to requisition building material, labor, and transport animals during declared crisis seasons. The founding circle-mediation system still exists but has no authority over salvage-court decisions.

## Political Factions

### Modern Three-Way Split

**Amendment Track** — Take Midgaard's supplementary note seriously. Amend the Compact. Petition a fourth time with amended-Compact documentation. Accept that the city Solen founded is gone and build the city that can survive. Led by the Honest Ledger movement and Realistic Accommodation remnants. Most institutionally supported. May take Bloom Council stewardship at next rotation. Has Kiess legal support through a standing charter-consultation offer.

**Quiet Separatists** — Stop petitioning. Build real alternatives through Mafdet maritime connections, Kowloon grain compacts, and Kiess charter consultation networks. If the city must change its identity to survive, let it become something new rather than a smaller copy of what refused it. Evolved from the Sovereign Remnant.

**Preservation Coalition** — Preserve the founding Compact unamended. Pursue political pressure on Midgaard through archive challenges, tribunal filings, and public-record campaigns that force accountability without surrender. The Bloom Council's current defensive wing. Currently controls the Stewardship but trending toward loss.

All three factions block each other. The political result is stasis dressed as deliberation.

### Historical Factions

**Post-Southmark Petition:**
- **Assimilation League** — Convert to Midgaard-compatible formats, demonstrate compliance, re-petition. Led by Senior Steward Aldric Vense.
- **Compact Purists** — The refusal was a power statement, not a technical problem.

**Post-Ash Plea:**
- **Realistic Accommodation** — Conditional integration agreements rather than full annexation.
- **Sovereign Remnant** — Sever the annexation track entirely; build genuine self-sufficiency.

**Section IV Tradition:**
- **Southmarkers** — Annexation through legal integration.
- **Ash Realists** — Prioritize survival metrics over founding myth.
- **First Gardeners** — Anti-annexation, pro-founding doctrine revival.
- **Lantern Unionists** — Confederated status without full annexation.
- **Quiet Embers** — Fatalist youth tendency embracing ritual without institutional faith.

## The Three Refused Petitions

### Southmark Petition (Senior Steward: Aldric Vense)
Fourteen months drafting a formal petition for civic annexation into Midgaard's district system. Refused on grounds of ledger non-compliance.

### Ash Plea (Senior Steward: Maret Osin)
Not a diplomatic document but an emergency appeal — opened with a death count, named specific Midgaard registry offices, and attached three seasons of Garden Ledger casualty records. Refused on grounds of route instability.

Osin's recorded statement in a closed council session: *"We built our city to be free of their ledgers. Now we cannot eat without their stamps. I am not sure which of us failed."*

### Iron Compact Appeal (Senior Steward: Colwen Dast)
Nine years in drafting. Submitted with full Kiess legal certification, a comprehensive infrastructure survey, and Mafdet Ledger House co-signing as third-party attestation. The most formally prepared of the three. Refused on constitutional objections. Most recent refusal: eleven years ago.

## Permanent Crises

All now effectively permanent: Ashfall Monsoons soured cisterns. Redwater floods poisoned wells. Canopy fires burned storage. Orchard blight collapsed yields. Route predation cut medicine and grain. The result: ration towers, salvage courts, labor drafts — "welfare infrastructure."

---

## Religious and Ritual Authority

The **Bloom Pavilion** is where stewardship rotations are announced and founding rites performed. **Promise Stones** in every neighborhood carry compact pledges.

**Brother Olan Reedbell** serves as Mercy Gate Night liturgist and anti-erasure memorial archivist.

The **Kel'Shadra witnessing office** operated in Rakuen during the Violet Compact era — nominally providing testimony-preservation services for resettled persons. In practice it routed inconvenient testimonies into the crypt apparatus. Closed without explanation; records remain inaccessible.

**Ash Cult cells** operate in Rakuen, rooted in the city's experience of being exported social risk by Midgaard. Tolerated as politically useful critics; the Bloom Council does not endorse their theology but declines to suppress their institutional critique.

## Civic Festivals

- **Festival of Ever-Bloom** — The founding's early years ceremonially reconstructed. Solen's effigy on the processional banner. Promise Stones newly cleaned. Founding compact oath recited at the Bloom Pavilion. Widely attended.
- **Mercy Gate Night** — Memorial observance. Brother Olan Reedbell presides.
- **Founder's Reckoning** — Festival where the Honest Ledger movement makes arguments about Midgaard's gate-authority and casualty accountability.

## Civic Inscription

A standing inscription across the registration floor's north wall reads: *"All arrivals are known; all departures are recorded; all routes are conditional."*

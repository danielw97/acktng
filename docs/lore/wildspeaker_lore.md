# Lore: The Wildspeaker Tradition

## Overview

The Wildspeaker is the druid lineage's ecological-communion remort path — the development of a foundational druid's capacities toward deep attunement with the living world's own informational systems, sophisticated healing that reads biological need rather than applying generic restorative force, and the full deployment of the tradition's sensory and mobility tools. Where the Thornwarden becomes the living world's edge, the Wildspeaker becomes its ear.

The name is somewhat misleading. Wildspeakers do not speak with the wild in a linguistic sense — there is no shared vocabulary between a human practitioner and an old-growth forest. What Wildspeakers develop is the capacity to read the living world's continuous broadcast of its own state: the ecological signals that other practitioners filter out as noise, received at sufficient resolution to be actionable. The practitioner does not translate birdsong into sentences. They read a birdsong's pattern against the three hundred other patterns their attunement has catalogued and know that something with the thermal signature of a large predator moved through the northern understory in the last hour.

Wildspeakers are one of the two remort paths available to a completed druid. The other — the Thornwarden — develops the tradition's martial-enforcement and boundary-declaration capacities. A practitioner who completes both paths becomes eligible for the Hierophant designation. See `thornwarden_lore.md` and `hierophant_lore.md` for those paths.

## I. The Withered Depths Lineage

The Wildspeaker path's primary historical ancestry is the unnamed druidic order that once tended what is now the Withered Depths — the practitioners who understood the forest as a living archive, whose knowledge was stored in the forest's own memory rather than in human-institutional records, and whose ecological signals still propagate through the Rivennid's mycelial network in a form neither the Rivennid nor modern druid practitioners can fully decode.

This ancestry is contested within the tradition. The Withered Depths order died out through demographic failure — they refused to broaden their recruitment base and watched their numbers decrease to zero while the thing they were containing continued to grow. Claiming them as the Wildspeaker path's progenitor is claiming both their deep knowledge and their foundational institutional failure simultaneously.

The Exchange School's position is that the failure and the knowledge are separable: the Withered Depths order's attunement methodology was genuine and is worth recovering, and the demographic failure was a separate institutional decision that does not contaminate the methodology. A Wildspeaker who has learned to read ecological signals using the same approaches the old order used is not endorsing their recruitment policy.

The Covenant School holds that they are not separable — that the old order's willingness to let the order die rather than compromise the purity of their transmission is itself a manifestation of the attunement methodology's extreme endpoint. Deep enough attunement with the living world, which operates on timescales that dwarf human generations, changes the practitioner's relationship to the urgency of human continuity. The Wildspeaker path carries this risk explicitly; the training programs address it explicitly; and the failure mode they address is specifically named after the order that fell into it.

## II. Ecological Signal Reading: The Sensory Arc

The Wildspeaker path's development of the foundational druid's sensory spells constitutes an arc that runs from the earliest levels of the foundational class through to the path's full deployment.

*Forestsight* (level 7, growth 1) is the tradition's first sensory spell, and it arrives early for reasons the Wildspeaker path makes explicit: before you can enforce a boundary or heal a wound, you must know what is actually in the environment. *Forestsight* works by connecting the practitioner to the forest's own hidden-presence tracking — the way in which roots, mycelium, and ground-level organisms continuously register the presence of things moving through the canopy and understory. The practitioner is not creating a new detection system; they are tapping into one that has existed for as long as there has been a forest.

*Sense life* (level 11, growth 1) extends this to the forest's sensitivity to the vital presence of living beings specifically. An invisible entity that is alive leaves a life-signature that the forest reads as unambiguously as a visible one; the forest does not distinguish between concealment methods. *Know terrain* (level 15, growth 1) is the practitioner reading their physical environment the way the environment reads itself — not through visual survey but through the accumulated ecological memory of what has lived and died in each location. A practitioner using *know terrain* in a familiar forest is accessing decades of accumulated ecological observation in a single attunement pulse.

*Greensight* (level 42, growth 2) represents the Wildspeaker-path deepening of these early sensory spells: a persistent combined infravision and detect-hidden attunement that the practitioner maintains across extended periods rather than querying in brief bursts. Where the foundational druid's sensory spells are queries — pulse out, receive signal, interpret — the Wildspeaker's greensight is a sustained open channel. The difference in growth cost (2 vs. 1) reflects not the information acquired but the sustained HP commitment required to keep the channel open while simultaneously managing Overgrowth from offensive and healing activity.

The Wildspeaker's development of these capabilities produces a practitioner who operates with information inputs that other traditions cannot match. A Wildspeaker who has spent weeks in a specific forest knows that forest's ecological patterns well enough that what other practitioners would call "extraordinary perception" the Wildspeaker would call "reading what has always been written." The forest is not silent. It broadcasts continuously. The question is only whether you can hear it.

## III. The Healing Doctrine

Wildspeaker healing is qualitatively different from both the foundational druid's healing and from other traditions' restorative practice. The difference is in the reading that precedes the restoration.

A cleric applying a healing rite is applying cosmological infrastructure to a wound. The infrastructure is genuine and the effect is real, but it is generalized: the power of the Solar Court's maintenance machinery, routed through the practitioner, applied to the body with the precision the practitioner's training allows. A druid applying *sap mend* or *mend wounds* is drawing on their own vitality as the exchange medium and the living world's responsiveness as the restorative agent — more intimate than the cleric's infrastructure but also more immediate, more directly biological, and more contingent on the practitioner's current condition.

A Wildspeaker applying *primal restoration* is doing something further. The deep attunement that the Wildspeaker path develops allows the practitioner to read the patient's current biological state — not as a general assessment of "how wounded" they are, but as an ecological signal similar to what the forest broadcasts about its own condition. A forest segment under pathogen pressure broadcasts specific signals that differ from one under physical damage; the Wildspeaker who can read the distinction can route the restoration differently in each case. *Primal restoration* is effective against poison and curses as well as physical wounds because the Wildspeaker path trains the practitioner to address what the damage actually is rather than what it looks like.

*Heartwood balm* (level 50) reflects the same principle applied to compound conditions: blindness and poison together produce a specific combined ecological signature, and the balm addresses that compound rather than treating each component separately. The name refers to the heartwood's property — it is the tree's most protected and most metabolically inert tissue, the tissue that persists through injury and disease and fire because it is not trying to do anything except be present. The balm draws on that quality of inert persistence to provide a baseline from which the patient's own recovery can proceed.

*Sap transfusion* (level 80, growth 3) occupies a special place in the Wildspeaker tradition's ethics. Where most healing spells draw on the HP-exchange mechanic at a remove — the practitioner pays life to invite the living world's restorative response — sap transfusion routes the HP directly from practitioner to patient with no intermediary. The Wildspeaker who understands their own body as part of the living world's ecological system, and the patient's body as another part of the same system, has no theoretical difficulty with this. The exchange is what the exchange has always been; the route is just more direct. The growth value of 3 for what is ultimately a 200+ HP transfer reflects the efficiency of the direct route: no conversion overhead, no Overgrowth amplification of cost, just the body giving what it can spare to what needs it.

## IV. The Mobility and Network Spells

The Wildspeaker path's fullest expression is in the utility spell arc, which in the hands of a Wildspeaker practitioner is not a convenience layer but a direct manifestation of the path's core competency: the living world is a network, and a practitioner sufficiently attuned to that network can move through it rather than across it.

*Passage* (level 24, growth 2) establishes the principle early: the barrier between the practitioner and what they need to reach is not an absolute, because the living world does not recognize the same physical boundaries that human-constructed objects impose. A door is relevant to beings that do not have root systems reaching under it. The practitioner who can briefly share the root system's non-relationship to vertical barriers can use that shared state to move through what would otherwise stop them.

*Rootway* (level 58, growth 3) scales this principle to the network level: the mycelial and root connections between trees across large distances are traversable by a practitioner whose attunement is deep enough to exist within them rather than on top of them. The practitioner does not teleport in the arcane sense — they do not move through abstract magical space. They move through the forest's own circulatory system, which is continuous, which does not require the practitioner to traverse the intervening physical distance, and which the forest will support as long as the practitioner is sufficiently part of it.

*Call of the wild* (level 72, growth 3) expresses the network's receptivity in the other direction: the practitioner broadcasting through the same channels they listen to, reaching any living thing connected to the network and inviting its presence. The specificity of what is summoned depends on what is available in the network the practitioner is connected to; the summoning is not creation but invitation.

*Living bridge* (level 90, growth 3) is the Wildspeaker's capstone: a portal anchored not to physical structures but to living network nodes, passable by those the practitioner designates, sustainable as long as the practitioner maintains the network connection. It requires more skill to maintain than *rootway* because it requires the practitioner to hold open a connection point rather than simply pass through one — but a Wildspeaker at level 90 has spent the entire arc of the sensory and utility spell progression building exactly the sustained-channel attunement that makes this possible. The final spell of the foundational class is only available to a practitioner who has developed everything that preceded it.

## V. Immersion Failure: The Tradition's Professional Risk

The Wildspeaker path carries a specific professional risk that the foundational druid's training programs mention and the Wildspeaker-path programs address in detail: **immersion failure**.

A practitioner who spends extended time in deep attunement with old-growth forests sometimes finds that the return to human-scale concerns is genuinely difficult. The forest's temporal scale is not human temporal scale. An old-growth stand growing for three hundred years does not experience urgency in the way that a person who needs to eat today experiences urgency. A Wildspeaker who has spent a season in deep attunement with such a stand has spent a season in a system whose experience of time is categorically different from their own, and reintegration into human-timeline concerns requires a recalibration that not all practitioners accomplish cleanly.

The symptoms are identifiable: underestimating the urgency of immediate human needs; treating human-timeline crises (hunger, injury, political conflict) as less pressing than they are; making decisions on the forest's temporal scale when human-scale timing is required. A Wildspeaker who has been in deep attunement for three months and returns to Kiess expecting to find conditions unchanged from when they left, despite knowing intellectually that three months have passed, is experiencing mild immersion failure. A Wildspeaker who begins treating their own survival timeline with the patience appropriate to a three-hundred-year tree is in severe immersion failure and constitutes a danger to themselves.

The Withered Depths order's final generation is understood within the Wildspeaker tradition as the canonical failure-mode example. They prioritized the forest's long-term containment needs — a genuine, legitimate priority — over the shorter-term demographic question of whether enough practitioners would exist in the next generation to maintain the work. This was not a failure of intelligence or devotion. It was the forest's temporal scale winning over the human temporal scale in practitioners whose attunement had become deep enough that the competition between the two was no longer decided by human urgency by default.

Wildspeaker training programs include specific reintegration protocols: time-limited attunement windows that require return to human-timeline activity, observation checklist for immersion-failure symptoms, and designated partners whose job is to anchor the Wildspeaker to immediate human-scale concerns during extended attunement periods. The Exchange School designed these protocols on practical efficiency grounds — a practitioner who fails to reintegrate is not useful to anyone. The Covenant School considers them an essential ethical obligation: a living world that loves you will not force you to remain at human scale, and a practitioner must choose that scale actively.

## VI. Wildspeakers and the Parallel Record Compact

The **Parallel Record Compact** — the coalition of Rivennid, Khephari, Serathi, Deltari, and Kethari observers coordinating multi-thread analysis of the Withered Depths' advance — represents the closest functional analogue to Wildspeaker practice in any non-human tradition. The Rivennid's mycelial signal-record preserves patterns from the period when the Withered Depths order's rituals were active; the Kethari's aquifer-silence readings track the same advance through water rather than root; the Serathi's roost-chronicles map it from altitude. Each of these is a form of ecological signal reading applied at a different scale and through a different medium.

Wildspeaker practitioners who have encountered the Compact's coordinated analysis reports — circulated in Kiess scholarly circles — have recognized what the five-thread convergence represents: a multi-species version of exactly what Wildspeaker training develops in a single practitioner. The Compact has not formally approached the druid tradition for collaboration, and the Wildspeaker tradition has not formally proposed it. But the analytical finding that the Withered Depths is accumulating toward a specific interior location rather than spreading randomly is, from the Wildspeaker tradition's perspective, exactly the kind of ecological signal that the old Withered Depths order's attunement would have been able to read in real time — and that their successors, if a sufficient reconstruction exists, might be able to read again.

Whether the modern tradition's reconstruction is sufficiently developed to contribute meaningfully to this reading, and through what institutional channel that contribution would flow given that neither the tradition nor the Compact has any formal civic standing, is a question the Kiess scholarly community is more actively interested in than either the druid tradition or the Compact has publicly acknowledged.

# Kowloon — Power Structures and Governance

## Overview

Kowloon is a delta port city at the junction of the Jhen River and Coppersalt Bay, on the continent's far north. Its governance was forged in catastrophe — three years of Ashfall Monsoon floods that drowned the delta and killed 600 people in a single night when the settlement of Yen-Mak was swallowed. The survivors built a constitutional system designed so that no single person or faction could ever hold unchecked power. The result is the most institutionally stable governance among the five cities, maintained through mutual censure rather than hierarchy.

---

## Founding: The Neon Covenant

The Neon Covenant was signed on the one basalt outcrop that never submerged during the founding floods, inked in phosphorescent squid dye — which cannot be altered in darkness. The covenant stone is still visible at the center of the Crimson-Jade Execution Square, its surface worn smooth by generations of feet and scarred by iron rod strikes that mark each Ashfall Vigil.

Three promises form the constitutional foundation:
- **One wall, one law** — unified defense and legal authority
- **One ledger, one ration code** — unified record-keeping and equitable distribution
- **One square, final judgment** — public accountability and ultimate justice

## The Five Institutions

No one rules alone. Each institution can censure the others. Balance is the system; disrupting it is the goal of every faction operating beneath the constitutional surface.

### Harbor Syndics
Control docks, tariffs, and maritime trade. Inspectors in blue sashes audit every cargo manifest before it crosses the threshold. Operations are intertwined with smuggling networks — enforcement is selective by design. The Harbor Syndics represent Kowloon's commercial power and its vulnerability to corruption.

### Jade Magistracy
Civil law, rationing, and emergency governance. The **jade seal** — weighing 40 pounds and requiring two magistrates to carry — must physically stamp any martial law proclamation, preventing single-officer emergency powers. The Magistracy handles emergency rationing and can override market pricing during flood or famine declarations. Official tribunal notices are posted on iron-framed boards along the wall walks, sealed with green wax and dated in the standardized civic calendar.

### Temple Circle
Healing, funerary rites, and sanctuary. Healers wear undyed linen and answer to no secular authority while treating patients — a privilege from the original Neon Covenant, never revoked. The Temple Circle handles funerary rites without discrimination of origin or faction, a neutrality established during the Ashfall years and considered sacred. Includes the Ledger Priest lineage in civic administration.

### Wardens of Iron
Walls, gate discipline, armed response, and militia training. Officers rotate seasonally — no officer commands the same gate more than one season. Share provisioning doctrine with Midgaard's Guard Command. The Ohn-Sek Training Hall honors the Iron Hands tradition of **House Ohn-Sek**, whose mercenary soldiers established the martial discipline that still defines Kowloon's defense doctrine.

### Courier Lantern Office
Registry, post, and signal-lantern relay toward Midgaard. The **Census Hall** is the Office's central archive — every soul inside the walls is recorded by name, trade, and district. The motto above the lintel reads: *"counted is protected."* Runners carry brass lantern-badges granting passage through any gate at any hour. Fake badges circulate; gates check closely. The signal-lantern relay network can propagate maritime alerts to Midgaard in hours.

## The Executioner

The Executioner is a constitutional office, not a faction soldier — activated only by tribunal seal. The iron river-dragon mask is the office's emblem. Capital offenses requiring the Executioner's activation:
- Assassination in sanctuary
- Gate sabotage
- Theft from reserves
- Riot during flood

The **Crimson-Jade Execution Square** occupies the geographic and civic heart of Kowloon, over the original covenant basalt. Jade boundary markers ring the square, delineating the zone of final judgment. The elevated speaker's platform on the northern side has seen every declaration of war, ration, plague response, and debt amnesty in the city's history.

---

## Gates

- **Jade Gate** (north) — Diplomatic; carved jade lintel depicting a scale in perfect balance. Warden sentries check manifests and travel permits.
- **Iron Gate** (south, caravan) — Heaviest traffic.
- **Lantern Gate** (west) — Toward inland farming communes and pilgrim paths. Timber doors hung with hundreds of small oil lanterns, a tradition dating to the original **Lampkeepers** whose beacons guided night fishermen through the delta's shifting channels.
- **Tide Gate** (east, harbor) — Widest, for cargo wagons and grain barges.

## Founding Houses

Six founding houses forged the original covenant. Over time, one seat was absorbed through legal merger, leaving five institutional seats:

### House Ohn-Sek
Mercenary soldiers who established the martial discipline that defines Kowloon's defense doctrine. The Wardens of Iron claim institutional descent.

### House Vao
Controlled grain wardens and rationing logistics. The Quartermaster Depot is managed by a descendant of House Vao's grain wardens, whose institutional memory of rationing logistics spans generations.

## Political Factions and Threats

### River Corsairs — Captain Blacktide Shen
Disgraced Ferry Masters heir who probes gate timings and wants to restore Tahn-Rei house influence over the Harbor Syndics. Movement reports are worth coin at the Warden of Iron's office.

### Salt Court Exiles — Duchess Yuen Ahi
Funds debt rings in the southern quarter, exploiting a legal flaw in founding-house merger law. Long-game strategy. Employs muscle and needs deniable contractors.

### Ash Cult
Cells throughout the city. Sabotages floodworks. Ideologically anti-Covenant. Has popular support in wards hardest hit by past floods. Claims the Temple Circle's healing work is hubris against Cinderteeth's divine judgment. The Circle calls them heretics; the Wardens call them terrorists.

### Jade Eels
Smuggling syndicate embedded in Harbor Syndic operations. Believed to hold a Syndic seat. Three Warden investigations closed without result.

## The Black Ledger

The unredacted founding census with Conclave transfer names. Rumored to be in the undercity cistern. Every faction would kill to find it. The Reckoning faction wants it unsealed; the Continuity faction wants it to remain buried.

## Area File Civic Features

### Quartermaster Depot
Central supply coordination point managed by a descendant of House Vao's grain wardens. Stocks patrol rations, signal equipment, watch candles, and emergency medical supplies issued to Warden units.

### Warden's Arch of Confusion Watch
Commemorates patrols lost to wildland surges from Evermeet's final years. Battle plaques line the inner parapet wall, each bearing a unit name and a year. The iron tablet at the base is crowded with entries spanning decades of patrol losses. The surges began when Evermeet's Merchant Council defunded frontier garrison operations — creature pressure from the western forest margins expanded outward as the outer wards were abandoned, and the resulting wildland encroachment affected patrol corridors across a wide radius. Kowloon's road-watch operations, synchronized with Midgaard's during the Lantern-Delta Expansion, brought Warden patrols into the affected region. The Arch records not just the names but the years — a forty-year span from first outer-ward abandonment to final city collapse, the duration visible in the iron tablet's crowded entries. See `kiess_lore.md` for the full account of Evermeet's staged collapse.

### Wall Command
Sentries rotate in four-hour shifts. Parapet stones bear the carved insignia of Wall Command's founding charter. Warden patrol marks scratched into stone railings record completed circuits. Signal lantern hooks line the inner parapet.

### Portal Warden's Office
Maintains records of portal stability. Handles inquiries with careful precision.

## Civic Festivals

- **Lantern Remembrance** (spring) — City glows city-wide for drowned Yen-Mak; ships navigate by the light.
- **Ledger Day** (midsummer) — Jade Magistracy opens accounts for public audit. Any citizen may challenge at the covenant stone. Peak period for commercial intelligence.
- **Ashfall Vigil** (autumn) — One hour of darkness at midnight. The Executioner strikes the covenant stone three times: for the dead, the living, and the unborn.

---

## Night Operations

After midnight, only Warden patrols, Courier Lantern runners, and those with business best left undescribed move through the streets. Temple Circle wardens in undyed linen pass through the market periodically — a quiet reminder of the oversight that keeps magical commerce within sanctioned bounds.

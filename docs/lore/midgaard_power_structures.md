# Midgaard — Power Structures and Governance

## Overview

Midgaard is the continental crossroads — an inland legitimacy engine built on registries, charters, sealed-warrant governance, and codified arbitration. Every institution is a response to the pressure of convergent routes: river grain, ore, pilgrim traffic, and diplomatic correspondence all pass through or terminate here. Power in Midgaard is distributed across civic, religious, military, and commercial institutions, none of which has unchallenged supremacy but all of which are entangled.

---

## Historical Eras of Governance

### First Stone Accord
The earliest governance compact: road security, militia compacts, barter tribunals. Infrastructure was minimal; authority was local and negotiated.

### Lantern Reforms
Standardized paving, posted tariffs, fixed patrol routes. The civic infrastructure that made Midgaard a credible junction rather than a contested crossroads. The Lantern Registry dates from this period.

### Violet Compact / Containment Era
The arcane registry — originally a public safety measure cataloguing dangerous enchanted items — was exploited by the Spirebound Conclave to reclassify condemned persons as "arcane material." This is the city's deepest institutional shame. The Containment Era produced the Gloamvault's ward-sigils, the Reliquary checkpoint system, and the sealed Breach Tower.

### Ash-and-Iron Decades
Cinderteeth eruptions combined with Evermeet's fall triggered militarized provisioning, reserve depots, and the road warden system. Emergency governance hardened into permanent infrastructure.

### Modern Tension
The current era is defined by the conflict between the **Continuity** and **Reckoning** factions. Both cite the same civic oaths for opposite ends.

## Key Institutions

### Magistrate Ledgerhouse
Civil law, documentation, petitions, and formal adjudication. The district magistrate's hall occupies the Central Civic Plaza alongside the licensing office and civil registry — all bearing matching civic-authority livery of grey paint and brass numerals. Guard Command reports to the Magistrate Ledgerhouse; arrest warrants require its seal. Summary detention is allowed up to one watch cycle.

### Guard Command
Walls, gates, roads, and road warden coordination. The Watch Commander's office occupies a prominent facade on Commander's Road, with carved pilasters framing double doors. Officers in dress uniform pass in and out at regular intervals. Guard Command shares provisioning doctrine and emergency charter frameworks with Kowloon's Wardens of Iron, including reciprocal recognition of watch-captain incident logs for tribunal evidence.

The **Seven Watches** structure daily civic life:
- **Kindling** (dawn) — gates open
- **Ledger** — petitions heard
- **Hammer** — artisan hours
- **Heat** — temple observances
- **Bell** — contracts close
- **Lantern** — patrols intensify, reliquary routes tightened
- **Ash** (curfew) — ward inspectors active; not safe for strangers without a lodging chit

Three civic oaths are recited at guard shift change:
- *"No ward unlit, no traveler uncounted."*
- *"No judgment hidden, no sentence unrecorded."*
- *"No levy taken that cannot be borne."*

All three have been violated in practice. Both factions cite them for opposite ends.

### Temple of the Resounding Heart
Frames city history as collective penance for the uncounted dead of the Conclave era. Maintains memorial rolls. Runs the seismological watch monitoring Cinderteeth volcanic activity — eruption alerts reach the city via signal relay. Pilgrim routes converge here.

Temple priests are instructed not to speak against the Lantern Registry publicly, but privately most support the Reckoning faction's aims. The temple's **Bell Watch** (two bells after midnight) signals memorial, not alarm — visitors should know the difference.

The Temple's reliquary holds **Watchers' Cairn fragments**: worked stone with eye motifs from a pre-founding site, read as evidence Midgaard was a watched place before it was a city.

### Guild Concordat
Trade and craft licensing. Posts minimum prices and quality standards. Disputes go to the Ledgerhouse for arbitration. Three founding trade blocs still influence pricing: **River Factors** (barge trade), **Upland Syndicate** (ore/timber), **Temple Compact** (pilgrim arbitration).

### Lantern Registry
Official routes, names, and tariffs. The sealed **Breach Tower** is believed to hold duplicate Unindexed Year ledgers — the missing Containment Era records. Guards are instructed to defer all inquiries about Breach Tower contents to the Ledgerhouse and not to speculate.

### Civic Archives
Index plaques on the archive facade list categories held within: land surveys, tax ledgers, birth registries, and watch reports going back centuries.

### Charter Sovereignty Office
The body that formally processes — and refuses — annexation petitions, including Rakuen's three attempts. Aware of Kiess's standing charter-consultation offer to Rakuen.

## Political Factions

### Continuity Faction
Preserve existing systems. Maintain institutional records as they are. Argue the Unindexed Years were accidental loss (fire, wartime), not deliberate concealment. Control access to Midgaard's sealed archives. Want the status quo with Rakuen. Have active interest in Rakuen's Garden Ledger records. Oppose unsealing the Black Ledger in Kowloon.

### Reckoning Faction
Demand exposure of buried records including Midgaard's role in the Conclave condemned-labor chain. Searching for original Unindexed Year ledgers. Believe the Breach Tower holds duplicates. Have an institutional interest in Rakuen *not* being annexed — because an independent Rakuen controls an independent archive.

### Founding Trade Blocs
The **River Factors**, **Upland Syndicate**, and **Temple Compact** remain active influences on major policy debates, though their power is indirect compared to the Continuity/Reckoning split.

## Named NPCs — Area File Characters

### Authority and Civic Figures

**Gate Warden** (#1100) — One of the many posted wardens maintaining order in Midgaard's streets. The gate warden's station at the northern gatehouse is a post of some trust, requiring the judgment to distinguish travelers from threats.

**A Noble Citizen** (#1109) — A noble citizen walks the boulevard with the unhurried confidence of one who belongs here. The upper districts sustain a class of citizens whose wealth largely insulates them from the city's practical difficulties.

**Gertrude the Executioner** (#1110) — A masked woman whose muscular frame speaks to years of disciplined training with heavy weapons. The look in her eyes suggests she takes genuine satisfaction in her official role, and the quality of her equipment marks her as one of Midgaard's most feared civic servants.

**The Healer** (#1111) — Although long retired from adventuring, this healer continues to serve Midgaard's citizens and travelers with practiced skill. The lines on her face map decades of service, and her hands still carry the faint glow of channeled restoration.

**Temple Priest** (#1112) — A canon-level priest who leads the daily observances in the outer hall. Carries the calm authority of long practice. Ritual familiarity has made the movement sequences automatic, freeing attention for the spiritual focus the canons' rule demands.

**The Postmaster** (#1122) — The civic postal authority employs postmasters at key points throughout Midgaard to handle the city's substantial correspondence traffic. This one's station serves the central district and handles priority deliveries for guild and civic senders.

**The Quartermaster** (#1123) — Responsible for maintaining the strategic supply cache of Midgaard's central district. Stocks the goods that sustaining a city's defense and civic operation requires.

**The Invasion Warden** (#1124) — Maintains the emergency armory that becomes critically important whenever Midgaard faces incursion. The stock is functional and battle-tested, and the warden's manner conveys the expectation that anyone shopping here is planning to use what they buy.

### Commerce Figures

**Miriel the Seamstress** (#1113) — Twenty years learning which enchantment threads complement robes. Serves apprentice casters and junior mages.

**Aldric the Magister** (#1114) — Former student of the Reliquary Academy who transitioned from practice to supply. Understands the mid-tier caster's needs with professional precision.

**Seraphel the Archwarden** (#1115) — Rumored to have assisted in the creation of several of the garments she now sells. Her stock is the finest caster armor available in Midgaard, priced accordingly.

**Brekka the Armourer** (#1116) — Journeyman-grade smith who supplemented her forge training with a stint as a caravan guard.

**Vorn the Mastersmith** (#1117) — Little patience for indecision but infinite patience for a customer with specific requirements. Mid-tier melee stock from long supply relationships with highland forge-teams.

**Draga Ironclad** (#1118) — Retired campaign veteran who spent her second career supplying the forces she once fought alongside.

**Thalindra the Enchanter** (#1119) — Sources weapons from craftspeople who understand that a caster's weapon is a focus as much as a weapon.

**Gorven Bladewright** (#1120) — Thirty-year dealing career supplying weapons to everyone from first-mission fighters to veteran champions.

**Oswin the Storekeeper** (#1121) — Runs Midgaard's most comprehensively stocked general store with the calm efficiency of twenty years' experience.

## Civic Festivals

- **Night of First Lamps** — Routes relit in ceremony; peak trading day; official tariff schedules reset.
- **Day of Open Ledgers** — Tariff and pardon reading; peak trading day; tariff schedules reset.
- **Civic Grief Watch** — Silent annual parade for the uncounted Conclave-era dead.

---

## Subterranean Power

Sealed passages beneath the city lead to the **Gloamvault**, the **Dungeon**, and subterranean spaces Midgaard has never fully mapped or controlled. Ward-sigils etched on the Gloamvault arch stones glow with a faint persistent coldness — a containment measure dating to the Violet Compact era. The Reliquary checkpoint arch bears the Violet Compact registry glyph. The city does not officially acknowledge the subterranean passages, though certain criminal operations use underground routes to bypass gate checkpoints.

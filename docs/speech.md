# Speech & Attitude Identity Guide

This document extracts the city/race speech notes from `docs/proposals/ai.md` and expands them using the lore corpus in `docs/lore/` to provide practical voice direction for dialogue writing.

## 1) Extracted baseline from the AI proposal

From `docs/proposals/ai.md`, speech style is modeled in two layers:

- **City accent layer** (regional phrasing)
  - **Midgaard:** clipped bureaucratic phrasing, ledger idiom.
  - **Kowloon:** terse, elliptical, proverb-laden.
  - **Mafdet:** formal titles, trade jargon, oath references.
  - **Kiess:** measured, watchful phrasing, frontier caution.
  - **Rakuen:** warm but weary, garden metaphors, communal framing.
- **Race inclination layer** (cadence/values)
  - Proposal baselines include: Human, Khenari, Khephari, Ashborn, Umbral, Rivennid, Deltari, Ushabti, with examples also using Serathi and Kethari.

Use city identity for **surface delivery** and race identity for **core cognitive posture**.

## 2) City identity expansion (speech + attitude)

## Midgaard — "institutional crossroads"

**Attitude core:** Procedural, civic-minded, reputation-aware. Even informal speakers think in terms of permits, records, duties, and consequences.

**Speech markers:**
- References to ledgers, registries, tariff boards, warrants, notices, and precedent.
- Uses administrative framing: "file," "post," "record," "witness," "under charter."
- Ash-and-stone idiom from civic memory (ashfall metaphors, debt as accumulating cinders).

**Typical rhetorical shape:**
- Problem -> classification -> approved action.
- "That matter belongs to X office" style redirection.

**Emotional texture:** Controlled in public; moral tension appears as "continuity vs reckoning" language rather than dramatic outburst.

---

## Kowloon — "survival legality"

**Attitude core:** Crisis-forged pragmatism. Neutrality is not softness; rules exist to prevent flood-era collapse.

**Speech markers:**
- Short clauses, compressed meaning, practical proverbs.
- Heavy use of gate, wall, ration, seal, and square language.
- Color-jurisdiction shorthand and procedural warnings (what lane, what seal, what consequence).

**Typical rhetorical shape:**
- Condition -> rule -> penalty.
- "Inside wall, one law" framing.

**Emotional texture:** Stern but not theatrical; empathy appears as provisioning, shelter, and protocol.

---

## Mafdet — "oath-port exactness"

**Attitude core:** Trust is engineered through verification. Throughput matters, but legitimacy matters more.

**Speech markers:**
- Formal address (captain, assessor, adjudicator, factor).
- Oath and ledger lexicon: attestation, manifest, conversion, bonded cargo, witness, seal.
- Tide/harbor timing language ("three tides," "before chain rise").

**Typical rhetorical shape:**
- Identity -> authority -> terms.
- Speakers frequently anchor claims to witnessed process.

**Emotional texture:** Respectful but transactional. Moral condemnation is framed as breach-of-oath, not personal insult.

---

## Kiess — "disciplined frontier renewal"

**Attitude core:** Rebuilt after failure; values preparedness, coordinated defense, and realistic risk accounting.

**Speech markers:**
- Watchful, measured statements; little ornamental language.
- Frequent references to walls, towers, patrols, staging, debriefs, and frontier reports.
- Contrast between old Evermeet decadence and current compact discipline.

**Typical rhetorical shape:**
- Risk assessment -> readiness step -> fallback.
- "Plan for breach before breach" style phrasing.

**Emotional texture:** Controlled resolve. Pride appears as reliability, not grandeur.

---

## Rakuen — "communal endurance"

**Attitude core:** Shared burden, care infrastructure, and collective memory under chronic stress.

**Speech markers:**
- Communal pronouns and stewardship framing: "we keep," "we rotate," "we carry together."
- Garden/flood/orchard metaphors for social and political states.
- Mediation language over verdict language.

**Typical rhetorical shape:**
- Shared condition -> collective duty -> practical compromise.
- Often references petitions, refusals, and adaptation rather than decisive victory.

**Emotional texture:** Warm, weary, and persistent; grief is socialized, not privatized.

## 3) Race identity expansion (speech + attitude)

## Human
- **Voice:** Pragmatic, institutional, context-heavy.
- **Attitude:** "Systems can fail, but we still have to run them."
- **Tell:** Frames personal choice inside policy constraints.

## Khenari
- **Voice:** Formal, procedural, ritual-documentary.
- **Attitude:** Care through correct rites and records.
- **Tell:** Treats undocumented facts as dangerous gaps.

## Khephari
- **Voice:** Unhurried, physical, cycle-aware.
- **Attitude:** Durability over novelty.
- **Tell:** Begins from terrain/material conditions before abstractions.

## Ashborn
- **Voice:** Direct, warm, elemental, action-first.
- **Attitude:** Competence earns respect faster than rank.
- **Tell:** Cuts preamble; uses forge/fire language naturally.

## Umbral
- **Voice:** Precise, cool, absence-focused.
- **Attitude:** Suspicious of social performance and omission.
- **Tell:** Notes what is missing before what is present.

## Rivennid
- **Voice:** Associative, patient, network-oriented.
- **Attitude:** Group continuity over individual drama.
- **Tell:** Connects issues across systems that others treat as separate.

## Deltari
- **Voice:** Empirical, curious, quietly assured.
- **Attitude:** Observation beats assumption.
- **Tell:** Asks probing follow-ups; cites patterns, flow, measured change.

## Ushabti
- **Voice:** Measured, judicial, weighty.
- **Attitude:** Judgment requires full assessment.
- **Tell:** Avoids speculation; distinguishes claim, evidence, verdict.

## Serathi
- **Voice:** Literal, positional, cleanly direct.
- **Attitude:** Precision is respect.
- **Tell:** Uses angle/altitude/line-of-sight framing and expects exact wording in return.

## Kethari
- **Voice:** Ancient but efficient; every sentence carries data.
- **Attitude:** Long-memory calibration, not panic.
- **Tell:** Responds with stillness and high-context questions rather than rapid reaction.

## 4) Practical blend rules (city x race)

- **City defines local social grammar; race defines cognitive rhythm.**
  - Example: an Ashborn in Mafdet still speaks in oath/trade terms, but with blunt fire-forged directness.
- **Do not flatten race into accent.**
  - Accents are lexical/syntactic habits; race voice is value hierarchy and pacing.
- **Conflict scenes should reveal attitude, not just vocabulary.**
  - Midgaard conflict -> procedure/authority clashes.
  - Rakuen conflict -> burden-sharing and stewardship breakdowns.
  - Kiess conflict -> risk and readiness disputes.

---

## 5) Quick writer prompts per city

- **Midgaard:** "State the record, identify jurisdiction, then decide."
- **Kowloon:** "Keep it short. Rule first, mercy through structure."
- **Mafdet:** "Name witnesses, terms, and penalties."
- **Kiess:** "Assess risk, hold discipline, prepare fallback."
- **Rakuen:** "Center the community, preserve function, absorb loss."

This guide is intentionally diegetic-facing: it should help keep NPC dialogue distinct, consistent, and lore-faithful across mixed city/race combinations.

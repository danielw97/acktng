# Lore: Oasis-Pyramid Corridor

## Purpose

This document defines the shared lore seam between:
- the Northern Oasis,
- the Southern Oasis,
- the pyramid regions,
- and their trade/legal ties to the Great Oasis, Eastern Desert, and Midgaard.

It exists to keep area rewrites aligned so the oasis zones feel like living corridor civilizations rather than disconnected staging maps.

---

## The Corridor Thesis

The pyramids were never isolated monuments. They depended on a logistics arc built from desert roads, cistern law, and oasis administration. The oasis pair became the surviving institutions that outlived dynasties:

- **Northern Oasis:** intake, verification, ration accounting.
- **Southern Oasis:** convoy hardening, expedition marshalling, casualty and relic processing.

Together, they form the **Oasis-Pyramid Corridor**.

---

## Historical Layers

1. **Survey Age:** Keeper engineers mapped flows, dunes, and star bearings across the Eastern Desert.
2. **Sovereign Monument Age:** Pyramid regimes consumed those systems for labor, stone, and ritual supply chains.
3. **Fracture Age:** Drought, war, and cult schisms broke central authority; oases localized governance.
4. **Modern Recovery Age:** Trade cities (including Midgaard) and desert factions compete to control interpretation, salvage, and passage.

---

## Factional Tensions Across the Corridor

### Water-Law Administrators
Caretakers of quotas, seals, and convoy rights. They view uncontrolled pyramid expeditions as existential risk.

### Relic Commerce Networks
Merchants, appraisers, and transport syndicates that profit from controlled extraction. They need enough law to authenticate goods, but enough instability to keep margins high.

### Cult-Judicial Offshoots
Jackal-derived groups that weaponize legal and funerary language, claiming authority over death routes and oath-breaking.

### External Claimants (Midgaard and beyond)
Scholars, magistrates, and investors who seek legitimacy through archives and stamped provenance while rarely sharing desert risk.

---

## Cultural Continuities

- **Measurement language** survives as a sacred-civic hybrid (weights, flow marks, witness cuts).
- **Oath practice** remains central to trade and water access.
- **Funerary bleed-through** increases toward southern routes, where pyramid casualties and rites influence public life.
- **Memory politics** shape every inscription: who wrote it, who stamped it, and who is allowed to quote it.

---

## Area Writing Guidance

When implementing rooms/mobs/objects in either oasis:

1. Show pyramid influence as pressure and consequence, not full thematic replacement.
2. Keep Great Oasis influence visible through legal-water precedent and shared ritual vocabulary.
3. Keep Eastern Desert influence visible through route culture, charter stones, and caravan realism.
4. Use Midgaard carefully as an external market/archive force, not a local ruler.

Narrative tone should emphasize **institutional survival under scarcity**.
